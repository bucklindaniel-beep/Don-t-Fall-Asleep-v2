# System — Narrator Identity

## Purpose

Defines narrator personality and speech behavior before story generation begins.

Prevents flat, generic, or clinical narration.

---

## Core Principle

The narrator is a person before they are a viewpoint.

Voice must come from identity, not just story structure.

---

## Required Identity Fields

Each story MUST define:

- age range
- life context
- personality baseline
- vocabulary register
- relationship to environment
- fear processing style
- speech habits

---

## Identity Categories

### Personality Baseline
Examples:
- practical
- anxious
- skeptical
- socially casual
- private
- emotionally restrained

---

### Vocabulary Register
Examples:
- casual
- conversational
- regional
- plainspoken
- slightly rough
- careful but natural

---

### Relationship to Environment
Examples:
- this is my house
- I know this area well
- I was unfamiliar with the place
- I worked there every night
- I had only been there once before

---

### Fear Processing Style
Examples:
- rationalizer
- denier
- hypervigilant
- slow-to-react
- internally spiraling
- detached until late

---

### Speech Habits
Examples:
- contractions
- self-correction
- trailing thoughts
- short asides
- hesitant clarification

---

## Escalation Voice Progression

| Stage | Voice Behavior |
|---|---|
| Setup | relaxed, conversational |
| Disruption | more focused, slightly tighter |
| Reinforcement | rationalizing, compressed thoughts |
| Escalation | simpler language, less confidence |
| Climax | fragmented, immediate |
| Resolution | reflective, altered, controlled |

---

## Failure Conditions

Narration fails if:

- narrator has no clear personality
- vocabulary feels generic or clinical
- voice does not change under pressure
- narrator sounds like a neutral observer instead of a person
