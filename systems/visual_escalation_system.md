# System — Visual Escalation

## Metadata
- Type: System
- Domain: Visual Progression / Cinematic Tension
- Purpose:
  - align visual intensity with narrative escalation
  - control how scenes visually degrade or intensify
  - enhance immersion and tension through visual changes
- Dependencies:
  - /frameworks/01_horror_escalation_model.md
  - /systems/continuity_lock_system.md
- Status: active
- Confidence: high

---

## Purpose

Controls how visuals evolve as tension increases.

Ensures:
- visuals reflect escalation stage
- scenes become progressively more intense
- environment and lighting support narrative tension

---

## Core Principle

Visual intensity MUST increase with escalation.

Every escalation stage must produce a visible change.

---

## Escalation Mapping

Each narrative stage MUST correspond to visual changes:

---

### 1. Setup (Baseline)

Visual State:
- clean environment
- stable lighting
- balanced composition
- no distortion

Goal:
- establish normalcy

---

### 2. Disruption

Visual Changes:
- subtle irregularities
- slight lighting inconsistency
- minor environmental anomalies

Examples:
- shadows slightly off
- objects slightly misaligned

Goal:
- introduce unease

---

### 3. Reinforcement

Visual Changes:
- repeated anomalies
- stronger inconsistencies
- environmental details accumulate

Examples:
- objects moved
- repeated lighting flickers
- signs of presence

Goal:
- confirm pattern

---

### 4. Escalation

Visual Changes:
- environment becomes unstable
- stronger contrast
- tighter framing
- reduced safe space

Examples:
- darker shadows
- closer camera angles
- clutter or damage increases

Goal:
- increase pressure

---

### 5. Climax

Visual Changes:
- extreme contrast or darkness
- aggressive framing
- chaotic or distorted environment

Examples:
- motion blur
- close proximity shots
- visual obstruction

Goal:
- maximum tension

---

### 6. Resolution

Visual Changes:
- partial return to stability OR
- lingering abnormality

Examples:
- environment mostly normal but altered
- subtle unresolved anomaly

Goal:
- release tension without full reset

---

## Visual Escalation Levers

Each stage may adjust:

- lighting (brightness, contrast, direction)
- framing (wide → tight → extreme close)
- environment condition (clean → disturbed → chaotic)
- object density (minimal → cluttered)
- visibility (clear → obstructed)

---

## Progression Rules

Visual escalation MUST:

- increase gradually
- follow narrative escalation stages
- never jump directly to extreme states

---

## Continuity Integration

Visual escalation MUST respect:

/systems/continuity_lock_system.md

Changes must:

- build on previous state
- not reset environment
- evolve logically

---

## Failure Conditions

Visual escalation FAILS if:

- scenes look visually identical across stages
- intensity jumps too quickly
- environment resets between shots
- visuals contradict narrative stage

---

## Compression Summary

Stable → Subtle Shift → Repetition → Instability → Chaos → Residual Change
