## Purpose

Controls how the camera behaves across shots.

Ensures:
- intentional framing
- emotional perspective control
- escalation through camera decisions

---

## Core Principle

Camera behavior MUST reflect narrative tension.

The camera is not neutral.

It guides what the viewer feels.

---

## Camera Dimensions

Each shot MUST define:

### 1. Shot Scale
- wide (environment dominance)
- medium (balanced)
- close-up (character focus)
- extreme close-up (intensity)

---

### 2. Camera Position
- eye level (neutral)
- low angle (power/threat)
- high angle (vulnerability)
- over-the-shoulder (presence)
- hidden/obstructed (voyeuristic tension)

---

### 3. Camera Distance

Progression SHOULD move toward:
- farther → closer proximity as tension increases

---

### 4. Camera Movement

Options:
- static (calm, controlled)
- slow push-in (tension increase)
- tracking (following subject)
- handheld/shaky (instability)
- sudden shift (shock)

---

### 5. Framing Style

- centered (stable)
- off-center (unease)
- partially obstructed (hidden threat)
- negative space (anticipation)

---

## Escalation Mapping

Camera behavior MUST evolve with escalation:

---

### Setup
- wide shots
- stable framing
- static camera

---

### Disruption
- slight framing imbalance
- subtle push-in
- off-center composition

---

### Reinforcement
- tighter framing
- more negative space
- slight movement

---

### Escalation
- closer shots
- obstructed views
- tracking or slow push

---

### Climax
- extreme close-ups
- chaotic or handheld movement
- aggressive framing

---

### Resolution
- partial return to stability
- wider framing OR lingering close tension

---

## Camera Progression Rule

Across shots:

- camera MUST move closer over time (on average)
- framing MUST become less stable
- visibility MUST decrease or become obstructed

---

## Continuity Integration

Camera changes MUST:

- respect /systems/continuity_lock_system.md
- not reset perspective randomly
- evolve logically between shots

---

## Visual Escalation Integration

Camera MUST reinforce:

/systems/visual_escalation_system.md

Example:

- darker lighting → tighter framing
- instability → shaky camera
- proximity → close-ups

---

## Forbidden Behavior

- random shot scale changes
- repeated identical framing across shots
- static camera across entire scene
- inconsistent perspective shifts

---

## Failure Conditions

Camera language FAILS if:

- shots feel visually repetitive
- camera does not evolve with escalation
- framing contradicts emotional tone
- viewer perspective feels disconnected

---

## Compression Summary

Wide → Off-Balance → Tight → Obstructed → Close → Intense
