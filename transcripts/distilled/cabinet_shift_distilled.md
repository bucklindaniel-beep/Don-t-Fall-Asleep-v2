# Distilled Patterns — Cabinet Shift

## Core Pattern: "Environmental Desynchronization"

Small environmental details change independently of the subject’s actions, creating a conflict between memory, evidence, and reality.

---

## High-Value Techniques

### 1. Verifiable Reality Conflict
- Introduce physical proof (photo, recording)
- Break trust between evidence and reality

---

### 2. Controlled Repetition Loop
- Repeat same action (cabinet left open)
- Slight variation escalates tension

---

### 3. Agency Removal
- Subject stops interacting
- Becomes observer instead of participant

---

### 4. Delayed Direct Confirmation
- Delay the moment where anomaly is witnessed live
- Build tension through implication first

---

### 5. Sound-Based Presence
- Transition from visual anomaly → auditory confirmation
- Use subtle, intentional sound cues

---

## Behavioral Rules Extracted

- Do not escalate immediately to visible entity
- Prioritize environmental manipulation first
- Use repetition to build credibility before breaking it
- Introduce proof before contradiction
- Shift control away from the protagonist over time
