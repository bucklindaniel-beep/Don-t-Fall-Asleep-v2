## Case Summary

Herbert Richard Baumeister (1947–1996) was an Indianapolis businessman and married father of three who led a hidden double life as a serial killer targeting gay men in Indianapolis, Indiana. Operating primarily between 1993 and 1995 from his 18-acre estate known as Fox Hollow Farm in Westfield, Indiana, Baumeister lured victims from gay bars, brought them to his estate under the pretense of socializing, and strangled them using autoerotic asphyxiation. He disposed of remains on his own wooded property, burning and fragmenting bones to prevent identification. When investigators searched the property in June 1996, they recovered more than 10,000 bone fragments representing an estimated 25 or more individuals. Baumeister fled to Canada and died by suicide on July 3rd, 1996, leaving no confession. As of 2025, 10 victims have been positively identified; the case remains officially open.

## Crime Scene

**Property:** Fox Hollow Farm, Westfield, Indiana
**Key features:** Tudor-style mansion with indoor swimming pool; wooded rear acreage used for body disposal
**Disposal method:** Burning remains in open-air fire pits; crushing larger bones; extracting and discarding teeth; partial dismemberment
**Evidence recovered (1996 search):** 10,000+ bone fragments; handcuffs; shell casings; personal items belonging to victims

---

## Method of Operation

1. Frequented Indianapolis gay bars using alias "Brian Smart"
2. Targeted men who were alone; built rapport; provided free drinks
3. Invited targets to Fox Hollow Farm (offered indoor pool, private party)
4. Murders conducted at indoor pool using autoerotic asphyxiation — ligature (pool hose, belt)
5. Disposed of remains in wooded property sections under cover of darkness
6. Timed killings to coincide with wife and children's absence (primarily summer breaks)

## Key Investigative Figures

**Tony Harris (pseudonym):** Friend of victim Roger Goodlet. Conducted personal investigation, met "Brian Smart," accepted invitation to Fox Hollow Farm, survived multiple strangulation attempts. Reported to police; later obtained Baumeister's license plate, enabling identification.

**Virgil Vandergriff:** Former Marion County Sheriff's Detective, private investigator. Hired 1994 by Goodlet and Broussard families. Identified pattern of at least 8 missing men; surfaced "Brian Smart" alias through bar interviews.

**Julie Baumeister:** Wife. In late 1995 was informed by detectives of investigation; initially denied possibility. In 1994, her son had found a human skull on the property; Herb explained it as a medical specimen; Julie accepted explanation. In June 1996, after filing for divorce, she granted permission to search the property.

**Jeff Jellison:** Hamilton County Coroner. Led 2020s renewed identification effort using forensic genetic genealogy. Resulted in identifications of Livingston (2023), Resendez (2024), and Jones (2024).

## Community and Systemic Context

- Indianapolis LGBTQ+ community documented fear and frustration with perceived law enforcement indifference to missing gay men throughout the early 1990s
- Families of multiple victims hired private investigator after police response was deemed insufficient
- Community advocacy and private investigation were credited with surfacing the key alias and generating investigative pressure
- Case contributed to documented tensions between law enforcement and LGBTQ+ communities in Indianapolis in the early-to-mid 1990s

---

## Outcome

- Baumeister died by suicide before trial; no criminal charges were ever filed
- Suicide note: three pages; addressed to wife and children; cited failed marriage and business; contained no confession and no reference to victims
- Case officially remains open
- Ongoing: forensic genetic genealogy work continues toward identification of remaining unknown victims
