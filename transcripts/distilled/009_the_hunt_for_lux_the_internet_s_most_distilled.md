## Story 01 — The Hunt for Lux

### Content Observations

**Subject type:** Real-world criminal — single individual documented across a multi-year arc from rise to conviction.

**Structural shape:** Linear escalation — entry, expansion, overreach, unraveling, arrest. No ambiguity about outcome; resolution is confirmed and dated.

**Scale of operation:** The source is explicit about scope. 400,000 daily hits. 15,000 daily downloaders on the flagship site. A wiki with 3 million visits and 1,000 contributors. These figures are sourced from the subject's own public statements.

**Arrest trigger:** The subject voluntarily offered his phone to investigators, confident it was clean. Three cached images were found. The entire prosecution depended on this single voluntary act. Without it, investigators had no grounds to hold him.

The email was written in a negotiating register — framing his cooperation as a business transaction with itemized value propositions. The offer was declined without negotiation.

**Sentence:** 15 years, parole after 10, 570 days credited. The source explicitly notes this was widely considered insufficient given the scale of the crimes. The judge's own language during sentencing was among the strongest on record; the sentence itself was among the lighter ones available.

**Downstream outcomes:** Thirty victims identified and rescued from data recovered post-arrest. Australia passed new legislation on encrypted device password disclosure as a direct result of this case.
