### Structural Phases

**Phase 1 — Entry and rise (2011–2012)**
- Lux discovers dark web via Operation Darknet publicity
- Offers security services; quickly gains status
- Identifies gap: no extreme hurtcore content exists
- Builds his own suite of sites

**Phase 2 — Empire building (2012–2014)**
- Establishes Hurt to the Core as flagship
- Creates tiered access requiring original illegal content for entry
- Builds wiki; reaches global scale
- Boasts 400K daily hits; 15K daily downloaders on flagship site
- Obtains and releases Daisy's Destruction footage

**Phase 3 — Pursuit and unraveling (2014)**
- FBI, Interpol, and Victorian Police launch joint investigation
- Anonymous tip narrows focus to Melbourne
- Lux senses capture; attempts immunity deal
- Shuts down empire June 2014

**Phase 4 — Arrest (2014)**
- Officers search Graham family home
- Father cleared — no computer knowledge
- Son Matthew becomes primary suspect
- Matthew voluntarily shows phone; three cached images found
- Arrested; charged with possession

**Phase 5 — Trial and sentencing (2015–2016)**
- FBI breaks encryption on hard drives
- Matthew offered ultimatum: US extradition or Australian charges; chooses Australia
- Pleads guilty to 13 charges
- Sentenced to 15 years; parole after 10
- Sentence criticized internationally as insufficient

### Key Mechanics

**Tiered access model:**
- New users restricted to surface content
- Access to higher tiers required submission of original, never-before-seen illegal material
- Highest tier (producer lounges) required verified original production
- Made law enforcement infiltration structurally impossible

**Security:**
- Lux never appeared visually on any platform
- Conflicting self-reported identity details (US doctor vs. Australian resident)
- 64-character encryption password
- Investigated agencies described him as "a ghost"

**Critical error:**
- Voluntarily showed police his phone, believing it was clean
- Cache contained three images — sufficient for arrest
- Arrest gave investigators time to break hard drive encryption

**Immunity offer:**
- Sent unsolicited email to FBI cybercrime tip line
- Demanded $50,000 Bitcoin + worldwide immunity
- FBI declined; Lux sent follow-up messages; FBI did not respond

### Observable Facts

- Matthew was 21 at time of arrest; raised in a working-class Melbourne household
- Worked as a babysitter for six children while running the empire; no evidence of direct harm found
- Parents were unaware; believed he was gaming
- Graffiti in his closet: "Parents should be afraid of raising children like us"
- Previously told a US journalist he was attracted to ten-year-olds; claimed asexuality at trial
- Judge described the case as "without parallel" and "the worst thing he had ever seen"
- Multiple people left the courtroom during verbal description of the material
- Thirty victims rescued as a result of data extracted from his empire
- Australia enacted a new law requiring encrypted device passwords to be surrendered on lawful order
