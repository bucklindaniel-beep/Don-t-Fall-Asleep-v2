## Story 04 — David Sonboly / Munich Mall Shooting / Gun from Dark Web

**Setting:** Munich, Germany; July 22, 2016
**Subjects:** David Ali Sonboly (shooter, deceased); Philip Korber (33, gun seller, Marburg); unnamed forum administrator (31)
**Threat type:** Mass shooting; weapon and ammunition sourced via encrypted dark web forum
**Outcome:** Korber sentenced to 7 years 2 months — first time German law held an arms dealer criminally responsible for a mass shooting; forum admin sentenced to 6 years

### Story Beat Map
1. July 22, 2016: Sonboly opens fire at Olympia shopping mall, Munich
2. 9 killed, 27 wounded; victims mostly young people with migrant backgrounds; Sonboly suicides
3. Date chosen as 5th anniversary of Anders Behring Breivik's Norway massacre
4. Weapon source identified as Korber, 33, Marburg, ~100 km north of Frankfurt
5. Connection made via encrypted dark web forums; price negotiated; two in-person meetings
6. Meeting 1: Glock 17 handover
7. Meeting 2: 350 rounds of ammunition
8. Total transaction: ~€8,000
9. Shared ideology: Nazi salutes in communications; Mein Kampf found on Korber's computer
10. August 2016: police track Korber via dark web activity
11. Sting operation: undercover officers pose as buyers seeking automatic weapon and another Glock 17
12. Korber confirms during sting that he sold Sonboly the gun
13. Korber confesses, expresses remorse
14. Jan 19, 2018: Munich court sentences him to 7 years 2 months — 9 counts involuntary manslaughter, 5 counts bodily harm, illegal weapons dealing
15. Dec 2018: forum administrator (31) convicted in Karlsruhe — negligent homicide, negligent bodily harm, facilitation; 6 years

### Source-Truth Anchors
- 9 dead, 27 wounded
- Glock 17 + 350 rounds
- ~€8,000 transaction
- Korber sentence: 7 years 2 months — first such legal precedent in Germany

## Story 08 — Stephen Allwine / Used Dark Web to Murder Wife

**Setting:** Cottage Grove, Minnesota; 2016–January 2018
**Subjects:** Stephen Allwine (perpetrator, church deacon); Amy Allwine (victim, dog trainer); 9-year-old son
**Threat type:** Husband used scam dark web hitman platform (Besa Mafia) and direct staging to murder his wife
**Outcome:** January 2018 — convicted of first-degree premeditated murder; life without parole

### Story Beat Map
1. 2016: Amy Allwine begins receiving anonymous, detailed, relentless death threats
2. Threats designed to psychologically destroy her — example: "Commit. If you do not, then you will slowly see things taken away from you..."
3. She reports threats to FBI
4. Investigators trace threats to Besa Mafia — fake murder-for-hire site (takes Bitcoin, never sends hitmen)
5. Someone had paid thousands in cryptocurrency for her death
6. Investigation stalls (no actual hitman to find)
7. Amy attempts to move forward, shaken
8. Nov 13, 2016: Stephen calls 911, claims he came home with their 9-year-old son, found Amy dead with 9mm pistol nearby; suggests she shot herself due to threats
9. Forensic red flags within minutes:
   - Gun in non-dominant hand
   - No gunpowder residue on either hand
   - No blood on weapon
   - Pool of blood cleaned up before officers arrived
10. Toxicology: scopolamine in Amy's system (heavy sedation/confusion drug, bought online by Stephen)
11. FBI traces Bitcoin transactions from Besa Mafia → Stephen's IP address
12. Stephen authored every single threatening message Amy had received
13. Motive (1): Stephen active on Ashley Madison; living separate life behind church-deacon image
14. Motive (2): Amy held $700,000 life insurance policy with Stephen as sole beneficiary
15. Jan 2018: convicted, first-degree premeditated murder; life without parole

### Source-Truth Anchors
- Platform: Besa Mafia (scam, no actual hitmen)
- Drug used: scopolamine
- Forensic anomalies: non-dominant hand, no GSR, no blood on weapon, scene cleaned
- Life insurance: $700,000
- Affair platform: Ashley Madison
- Sentence: life without parole, January 2018
