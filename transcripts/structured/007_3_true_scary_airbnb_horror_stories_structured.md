## Story 01 — Adirondacks Airbnb / Ex-Fiancé in the Closet

**Setting:** Secluded mountain Airbnb, Adirondacks, fall, weekend stay
**POV:** First person / solo female business traveler
**Threat type:** Stalker / ex-fiancé of property owner hiding inside the rental
**Outcome:** Police arrived during attack; suspect arrested with kitchen knife on belt

### Story Beat Map
1. Narrator books secluded Adirondacks Airbnb for solo work weekend
2. Saturday morning jog: persistent feeling of being watched, attributes to anxiety
3. Hours of strange noises throughout the day, dismissed as cabin sounds
4. Returns from town drinks ~10:45 p.m., triple-checks all locks
5. Hours of restless sleep; persistent noises through music
6. Goes downstairs after midnight for a cigarette
7. Sees obstructed moonlight from kitchen — turns light on, finds tall bearded man
8. ~10–15 second standoff, then man sprints; counter buys narrator time
9. Locks self in bedroom and closet; calls 911
10. Man pounds door, fist breaking through wood, repeating "I love you" / "I've changed"
11. Police tackle man as fist breaches door; narrator escapes via roof window
12. Reveal: man was the ex-fiancé of the property owner, had been living in a closet for days with beer cans and snack food, had a kitchen knife in his belt loop

### Key Craft Elements
- **Persistent dismissal:** Multiple early warnings (watched feeling, noises) dismissed as anxiety
- **Triple-locked irony:** Narrator's safety ritual is meaningless because the threat is already inside
- **Smoke break as accidental save:** Random act of getting up is what prevents the worst outcome
- **Misdirected love language:** Repeated "I love you" / "I've changed" while attacking creates dissociative horror — directed at the wrong person
- **Closet evidence:** Beer cans and snack food confirm long-term occupancy
- **Knife on belt loop:** Late reveal that escalates retroactively — narrator interrupted him mid-arming

## Story 02 — Out-of-State Interview Airbnb / Fake Pizza Delivery

**Setting:** Suburban Airbnb, out-of-state, weeknight after job interview
**POV:** First person / solo male traveler
**Threat type:** Suspected coordinated home invasion attempt; fake delivery as door-opening pretext
**Outcome:** Narrator avoided contact; door later found unlocked and ajar; chain held; Airbnb listing later removed

### Story Beat Map
1. Narrator books last-minute Airbnb after job interview
2. Friendly owner provides keypad code and early access
3. Settles in to watch a movie on the couch, falls asleep ~11:00 p.m.
4. 11:33 p.m.: doorbell rings repeatedly; narrator investigates
5. Skinny man with head down holds brown paper bag, claims pizza delivery
6. Narrator says no; man insists, recites the address
7. Man grabs screen door handle; narrator slams door
8. Man returns to old red sedan with one busted headlight; sits in driveway with high beam on
9. Narrator goes outside to confront — car running, but driver missing
10. Narrator runs back inside, locks door and chain, messages owner (no reply)
11. Later sees man return to car, appearing to come from the front door direction
12. 4:00 a.m.: narrator finds front door unlocked and ajar, held only by chain
13. Brown paper bag found beside porch; contents not opened
14. Drives away, sees same red car parked at green house at end of street
15. Owner stops responding mid-message after narrator describes the night
16. Listing later removed from Airbnb

### Key Craft Elements
- **Wrong-prop tell:** Brown paper bag instead of a pizza box — first concrete signal something's off
- **Insisting on the address:** Recitation of correct address confirms targeted delivery, not random mistake
- **Empty running car:** Single most disturbing visual — car visibly active, driver gone
- **Chain as accidental save:** Screen door left unlocked; only the chain prevents entry
- **Geographic proximity:** Same red car at house at end of street suggests local coordination, not random
- **Owner's typing-then-silence:** Conversational tell — owner saw the message, started replying, stopped
- **Delisting:** Independent confirmation that something was off

## Story 03 — Beach Family Vacation / Three Masked Men

**Setting:** Beachside vacation Airbnb at end of dead-end street, summer family vacation
**POV:** First person / father of two (8-year-old son, 13-year-old daughter)
**Threat type:** Coordinated three-person harassment/robbery crew using masks; targeting tourist families
**Outcome:** All three arrested at sliding door; all three armed with knives

### Story Beat Map
1. Family books Airbnb on dead-end street with creek behind
2. Night 1: 8-year-old son reports "ghosts" in the backyard — shadows that approach the house, one waves at him
3. Family dismisses as imagination; son comforted
4. Day 2: family returns from beach, finds front door unlocked (wife unsure if she locked it)
5. Night 2: son wakes parents, says ghosts are back; backyard floodlight is on
6. Son reports one ghost tapped on his window, threatened the family if he screamed, offered to leave if he opened the door
7. Day 3: father proposes living-room sleepover so son isn't alone
8. 2:30 a.m.: son wakes father — three hooded figures in black masks with bulging white eyes at the sliding door
9. One tries to open the door; locked; family screams; daughter calls 911
10. Father grabs walking stick; one figure waves a knife
11. Police tackle one at the door; other two flee but are caught nearby
12. Reveal: three young men, ages 18 and 20; all three armed with knives
13. Police describe a known local crew targeting tourist families; not surprised by the incident

### Key Craft Elements
- **Child as early sensor:** Son's "ghosts" are the actual reconnaissance team; adult dismissal allows escalation
- **Window tap + threat language:** Specific verbal threat to child establishes deliberate targeting and method
- **Unlocked door across days:** Pattern suggests prior unauthorized access during day 2
- **Mask design specificity:** Black masks with bulging white "golf ball" eyes — visually unique and remembered
- **Multi-night escalation:** Three escalating nights (observed → window tap → masked sliding-door attempt)
- **Demographic reveal:** Unmasking shows ordinary-looking young men — banality-of-evil beat
- **Local pattern confirmation:** Police indifference reframes the event as part of a known recurring crime — not an isolated incident
