## Section 2: Fox Hollow Farm

- Location: Westfield, Indiana (north of Indianapolis)
- Size: 18 acres
- Structure: Tudor-style mansion; 1,000+ square meters; stained glass windows; multiple fireplaces; indoor swimming pool
- Purchased: November 1991
- Reported value: approximately $1 million
- Named by Baumeister: Fox Hollow Farm
- Indoor pool area: decorated with mannequins posed in festive positions
- Wooded rear property used as remains disposal site

## Section 3: Killing Method and Body Disposal

### Method of Killing
- Targeted men at Indianapolis gay bars
- Approached as "Brian Smart" — friendly, bought drinks, invited men to estate
- Used indoor pool as scene of murders
- Method: autoerotic asphyxiation presented as consensual sexual kink
  - Tools: pool hose, belt, or similar ligature
  - Allowed victim to participate first to gain trust; then refused to release grip when reciprocating
- Victims strangled to death at or near the indoor pool

### Victim Profile
- Predominantly young white males
- Age range: teens to 46 years old
- Frequented Indianapolis gay bars
- Targeted when alone

### Body Disposal
- Bodies transported to wooded sections of the 18-acre property under cover of darkness
- Post-mortem actions to prevent identification:
  - Dismemberment of some victims
  - Burning of remains in open-air fire pits
  - Crushing of larger bones to fragments
  - Extraction of teeth; teeth reportedly tossed down a hallway
- Remains left on or near the surface; not all buried

### Operational Timing
- Murders occurred when wife and children were out of town
- Primary window: summer breaks (Julie took children to her mother's lake cottage in northern Indiana)
- Also exploited claimed business trips and late work hours

## Section 5: Suspected Earlier Crimes — I-70 Strangler

- Active period: 1980–1991
- At least 11 men found strangled and dumped near Interstate 70, Indiana and Ohio
- Victims: mostly young gay men last seen at Indianapolis gay bars
- Bodies found unclothed or partially clothed, discarded in streams or ditches off highways
- Killings stopped in 1991 — same year Baumeister purchased Fox Hollow Farm
- Theory (1998, investigators): once Baumeister had a private property, interstate dumping became unnecessary
- Attribution: suspected but unconfirmed

## Section 6: The Community Response and Investigation

### LGBTQ+ Community Response
- Fear spread within Indianapolis LGBTQ+ community by early 1990s as gay men continued to vanish
- Perception that law enforcement was not treating the disappearances seriously
- Quote attributed to Josh Thomas (former publisher, local gay magazine), 1996: contrasted police response to missing gay men versus hypothetical missing cheerleaders
- Families of victims took independent action

### Private Investigation — Virgil Vandergriff
- Former Marion County Sheriff's Detective, retired; operating private investigation firm
- Hired in 1994 by families of Roger Goodlet and Alan Broussard
- Identified pattern: at least 8 young men missing under similar circumstances in a short span
- Coordinated informal task force; visited gay bars; distributed missing flyers; interviewed patrons
- Surfaced alias: "Brian Smart"

### Survivor Account — Tony Harris (pseudonym)
- Friend of Roger Goodlet
- Independently investigated Roger's disappearance by frequenting his bars
- Met man calling himself Brian Smart
- Accepted invitation to go to Brian's estate (Fox Hollow Farm) — hoping to gather information
- At the estate: observed indoor pool, mannequins
- Brian offered a drink — Tony substituted water without Brian's knowledge
- Brian initiated autoerotic asphyxiation; Tony declined to fully reciprocate
- Brian wrapped a pool hose around Tony's throat; multiple strangulation attempts
- Tony allegedly faked unconsciousness; Brian eventually drove him back to Indianapolis
- Tony reported to police immediately; provided description of Brian Smart and approximate location
- Several months later: obtained Brian's license plate number
- Police traced plate to: Herb Baumeister, Westfield, Indiana

### Police Investigation Timeline
- Late 1994: Tony Harris reports to police; Brian Smart alias identified; plate traced to Baumeister
- Detectives from Hamilton County Sheriff's Department and Indianapolis Police Department begin background investigation
- Late 1995: Police approach Baumeister directly requesting consent to search Fox Hollow Farm — Baumeister refuses
- Late 1995: Detectives meet with Julie Baumeister; inform her of investigation; Julie denies possibility
- Early 1996: Julie files for divorce
- Late fall 1994 (earlier, surfaced in 1996): Baumeister's 13-year-old son found a human skull in the wooded area of Fox Hollow Farm; brought it to Julie; Herb explained it as an old medical specimen from his deceased physician father; Julie accepted the explanation; Herb disposed of the skull
- June 24, 1996: Julie grants permission to search Fox Hollow Farm
- Last week of June 1996: Search begins — Hamilton County Sheriff's Office, Indianapolis Police, forensic anthropologists from University of Indianapolis
- Within first day of search: human leg bone found on surface; large clusters of bone fragments located
- Two-week search: 10,000+ bone fragments and personal items recovered; handcuffs found; shell casings found
- June 29, 1996: Arrest warrant issued for Baumeister on multiple homicide charges
- Baumeister had already fled to Ontario, Canada

## Section 7: Baumeister's Capture and Death

- Fled Indiana upon learning of the search; drove to Ontario, Canada
- July 3rd, 1996: Body found by campers in Pinery Provincial Park, Lake Huron, Ontario
- Manner of death: suicide (self-inflicted)
- Left a three-page handwritten note addressed to wife and children
  - Apologized for "messing up the scenery" of the park
  - Cited failed marriage and failed business as reasons
  - Made no confession or reference to the murders
  - Made no mention of victims

## Section 8: Post-Death Investigation and Ongoing Identification

- Forensic identification effort began late 1996
- Methods: dental records, DNA comparison with family members, personal items
- By 1999: several victims confidently identified
- December 2022: Investigators returned to Fox Hollow Farm with cadaver dogs; located additional bone fragments
- 2020s: Hamilton County Coroner Jeff Jellison spearheaded renewed effort using forensic genetic genealogy
- 2023: Alan Livingston identified
- 2024: Manuel Resendez identified; Jeff Jones identified
- Case status: officially open; no charges ever filed
