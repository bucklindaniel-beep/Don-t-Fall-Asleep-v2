# Transcript — Structured

## Core Premise
A person notices small environmental inconsistencies that escalate into confirmed autonomous changes within their apartment.

---

## Event Sequence

1. Initial anomaly:
   - Cabinet left open → found closed

2. Verification attempt:
   - Cabinet intentionally left open
   - Photo taken as proof
   - Reality contradicts photo

3. Behavioral shift:
   - Subject stops interacting with environment
   - Switches to passive observation

4. Escalation:
   - Subject stays awake
   - Hears controlled movement

5. Confirmation:
   - Cabinet closes in real time
   - Subject confirms no prior interaction

---

## Escalation Pattern

- Subtle → Verifiable → Psychological Disruption → Direct Evidence

---

## Tension Mechanics

- Repetition with variation (cabinet open/closed loop)
- Evidence contradiction (photo vs reality)
- Removal of control (subject stops acting)
- Sensory shift (visual → auditory)
- Delayed confirmation

---

## Perspective

- First-person
- Retrospective recounting
- Calm, controlled tone
