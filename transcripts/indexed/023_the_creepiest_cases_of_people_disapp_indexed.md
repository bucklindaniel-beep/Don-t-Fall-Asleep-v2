## Story 01 — Noah Donohoe

**Entities:** Noah Donohoe (14, deceased), Noah's mother, investigative reporter Donald McIntyre, PSNI (Police Service of Northern Ireland), coroner's office, Noah's Army (advocacy organization)
**Setting:** South Belfast / North Belfast / underground storm drain — June 21, 2020
**Status:** Officially ruled drowning; inquest ongoing; investigation contested by family and public; inquest held January 2026

**Event Sequence:**
1. June 21, 2020 — Noah Donohoe, 14, leaves home in South Belfast at approximately 3:30 a.m. without his mother's knowledge; wearing shorts, t-shirt, flip-flops; carrying headphones
2. CCTV captures him walking toward Queens University; approximately 38 minutes later, same cameras capture him returning; headphones and flip-flops are gone — reason unknown
3. Approximately 5:40 p.m. — Noah leaves home again; tells his mother he is cycling to Cave Hill Country Park (approximately 6 miles north) to work on a school project with classmates; wearing shorts, hoodie, jacket, athletic shoes, helmet; backpack with laptop
4. CCTV and one eyewitness capture him cycling through downtown Belfast; falls off his bike en route; gets up and continues
5. Approximately 6:00 p.m. — seen on another street in North Belfast; backpack is gone
6. Shortly after — seen cycling through a residential area with no clothes on; location is miles from stated destination
7. 6:03 p.m. — Noah gets off his bike, walks past a house toward a stream and storm drain on Department for Infrastructure land; last confirmed sighting
8. Under 20 minutes elapsed between leaving home and last confirmed sighting
9. Shortly before 10:00 p.m. — mother reports him missing; notes he had been dealing with mental health issues (nature unspecified)
10. Search launched; Noah's bike, discarded clothes, and helmet found along his route
11. June 27, 2020 — Noah's naked body found approximately 3,300 ft (1,000 m) inside an underground storm drain; cause of death ruled drowning by medical examiner
12. Mother notes a 6-inch bump on Noah's forehead not present at last sighting
13. Official conclusion: Noah injured his head in the bike fall, became disoriented, entered the storm drain, and drowned; clothing discarded due to disorientation
14. Backpack and laptop not initially found along his route or with his body; later, a man arrested (early 2021) for stealing the backpack and laptop after attempting to pawn the laptop; courts rule the theft was opportunistic and unrelated to Noah's death
15. Family disputes the official account; raises questions about: why Noah was in an unfamiliar part of the city; why a 14-year-old with a documented fear of darkness would enter a dark underground drain; how he drowned inside the drain; the storm drain being at the end of a cul-de-sac in a gated area with restricted public access
16. Inquest repeatedly delayed; final date set for November 2025, extended to January 2026
17. January 19, 2026 — inquest held; new detail: two nearby residents independently reported hearing screams at midnight or early morning on the night of disappearance; at least one described a muffled scream that sounded far away
18. Case information partially classified under public interest immunity — authorities not obligated to release it
19. Investigative team assembled by Donald McIntyre (crowdfunded) conducting independent review; findings not yet public as of recording date

**Observable Mechanics:**
- Two separate departures on the same day with unexplained behavior on each (lost items at 3:30 a.m.; progressive shedding of possessions, bike fall, nudity, and route deviation in the evening)
- 180 CCTV cameras reportedly on route; only a few dozen documented his movements — gaps in coverage during key period
- Storm drain location is gated, restricted, and known only to long-term local residents — raises access question for a disoriented teenager
- Fear of darkness documented by mother is contextually inconsistent with voluntary storm drain entry
- Public interest immunity classification deepens the informational gap for family and public

## Story 02 — Carrie Farver / Liz Golyar

**Entities:** Carrie Farver (37, victim / missing — presumed murdered), Dave Krupa (target of harassment), Liz Golyar / Shauna Golyar (perpetrator), Nancy (Carrie's mother), Amy (Dave's ex, mother of his children), Detectives Ryan and Jim
**Setting:** Omaha Nebraska / Macedonia Iowa — November 2012 through 2016
**Status:** Liz Golyar convicted of first-degree murder (2016); sentenced to life in prison; no body recovered

**Event Sequence:**

Phase 1 — Relationship and Disappearance (2012):
1. 2012 — Dave Krupa, recently moved to Omaha, meets Carrie Farver (37, computer programmer from Macedonia IA) through his auto repair shop; brief casual relationship begins; both explicitly agree to keep things casual
2. November 13, 2012 — Carrie sends Dave a text suggesting they move in together; Dave declines; Carrie responds with hostile and profane messages; tone continues to escalate
3. Around same time, Carrie's mother Nancy receives hostile, misspelled texts from a number claiming to be Carrie — announcing a new job in Kansas and accusing Nancy of being a bad mother; messages are stylistically inconsistent with Carrie's known writing
4. Carrie fails to appear at: her half-brother's wedding (November 16), her son's 15th birthday, Thanksgiving, and her father's funeral
5. Nancy reports Carrie missing, noting her bipolar diagnosis and medication history; police response limited — Carrie is an adult sending texts and emails; no immediate investigation

Phase 2 — Escalating Harassment (2012–2015):
6. Dave receives approximately 15,000 emails and 50,000+ text messages attributed to Carrie over three years
7. Messages alternate between rage and attachment; Dave blocks numbers and changes his phone number multiple times — messages continue arriving through new channels
8. Dave begins relationship with Liz Golyar, a woman he had met through online dating before meeting Carrie; Liz also begins receiving hostile messages attributed to Carrie
9. One text to Dave accurately describes his exact posture and clothing while he is alone in his apartment — surveillance confirmed
10. Liz's home is vandalized; profane spray-painted message found inside her garage; Liz reports it to police in late November, attributes it to Carrie
11. Messages escalate to explicit threats; Dave receives a photo of a woman bound inside a vehicle; receives an email with a fake online obituary for Liz
12. August 2013 — Liz's house catches fire; all her pets killed; fire investigators determine the blaze was deliberately set; Liz attributes it to Carrie
13. Dave's auto shop is vandalized
14. January 2014 — a brick is thrown through Dave's apartment window while he is home with another woman
15. Dave's legally owned firearm goes missing from his apartment; reported to police

Phase 3 — Investigation and Reversal (2015–2016):
16. 2015 — detectives Ryan and Jim assigned to case; Ryan investigates assuming Carrie is the harasser; Jim investigates assuming it is someone impersonating Carrie
17. Ryan checks Carrie's finances — zero bank account activity since November 2012; inconsistent with claimed move to Kansas
18. Digital forensics review of Dave's and Liz's phones:
    - Dave alone received ~15,000 emails and 50,000+ texts attributed to Carrie
    - Liz's phone contains a photo of Carrie's Ford Explorer taken approximately one month before police located the vehicle in January 2013 in Omaha
    - Liz's fingerprint matches an unidentified fingerprint found on a mint container inside Carrie's Explorer during the 2013 search
    - Multiple calls placed from Liz's phone to Carrie's home using *67 prefix to hide caller ID
    - Photo of bound woman sent to Dave traced back to Liz's phone
    - YouTube video of someone outside Dave's apartment uploaded under Carrie's name linked to an IP address registered to Liz's home
    - Furniture sale following Carrie's disappearance: Carrie texted Nancy asking her to let a buyer in; check sent as proof bears the name Shauna Golyar — Liz's legal name
19. December 2015 — Liz walks into the sheriff's office to file a harassment claim against Amy (Dave's ex); claims Amy is behind all the messages and may have Dave's missing gun
20. Following day — Liz calls 911 claiming she has been shot in the leg by Amy; investigators determine Liz shot herself with Dave's stolen gun to redirect suspicion
21. Second review of Liz's phone: dozens of fake email accounts using Carrie's name; scheduling app used to send messages automatically (explains simultaneous receipt by Dave and Liz); estimated 40–50 hours per week spent maintaining the deception
22. Police begin receiving emails from someone claiming to be Amy, confessing to killing Carrie; emails originate from Liz
23. Police search Carrie's Ford Explorer a second time; remove passenger seat upholstery; find bloodstains belonging to Carrie
24. 2016 — Liz arrested; charged with first-degree murder
25. Liz found guilty despite no body recovered; sentenced to life in prison; maintains innocence
26. Carrie's body has never been found

**Observable Mechanics:**
- Perpetrator impersonated victim continuously for 3+ years while simultaneously portraying herself as a fellow victim
- Scheduling app used to create appearance of 24-hour harassment activity — enabled simultaneous message delivery to Dave and Liz herself
- Self-vandalization (garage graffiti, house fire, self-inflicted gunshot wound) used to sustain the narrative that Carrie was targeting Liz
- Estimated 40–50 hours per week dedicated to maintaining the deception
- Victim's fingerprint in perpetrator's car and financial silence since disappearance were the two primary forensic anchors
- Legal name (Shauna Golyar) appeared on a check in Carrie's furniture sale — the single identity slip that directly linked perpetrator to victim's home after disappearance
