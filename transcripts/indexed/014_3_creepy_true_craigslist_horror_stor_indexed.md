### Story 01 — Couch Sale Robbery

**Narrator Profile:** Male, 22, living alone after roommate moved out, preparing to downsize to apartment.

**Platform:** Craigslist (selling furniture)

**Setting:** Narrator's own home, evening (~6–7:30 PM Saturday)

**Characters:**
- Narrator (seller, alone)
- "David" (false name, lead buyer)
- Two unnamed male associates

**Narrative Beats:**
1. Narrator lists couch on Craigslist; only serious buyer after 2+ weeks is "David"
2. Phone call establishes rapport; narrator overshares — confirms living alone, roommate gone
3. Buyer arrives 90 minutes late (7:30 PM); two unannounced associates enter behind him
4. "David" creates distraction — points at couch, lures narrator to kneel down to look
5. Associates reposition; narrator surrounded and feeling trapped
6. "Just messing with you" — false alarm used to test compliance and establish dominance
7. Narrator attempts to accelerate sale; tense silence, shift in "David's" demeanor
8. One associate tackles narrator, pins him to floor
9. "David" and third man loot entire house while narrator is restrained
10. Assailants flee by truck; narrator locked in shock on floor
11. Police called; fingerprints and shoe prints collected — case goes cold
12. Shoe prints matched to prior similar robbery; victim profiling confirmed (targets lone occupants)

**Key Detail:** Narrator identified as vulnerable by volunteering roommate had left.

**Outcome:** Robbery. No arrest. Case cold.

**Tension Devices:** Late arrival anxiety, uninvited guests, false distraction kneel, silence before attack, physical restraint.

### Story 02 — Truck Purchase Scam

**Narrator Profile:** Male, young, financially limited, no prior Craigslist experience.

**Platform:** Craigslist (buying vehicle)

**Setting:** Burger King parking lot → secondary empty parking lot off main road

**Characters:**
- Narrator (buyer, arrived by Uber, cash in hand)
- Unnamed seller (described as average, middle-aged white male, rusty white truck)

**Narrative Beats:**
1. Narrator borrows $4,000 cash from father to buy truck listed on Craigslist
2. Seller is responsive, gives credible answers, agrees to neutral public meet (Burger King)
3. Narrator arrives first by Uber; seller arrives ~5 minutes later
4. Conversation builds trust; test drive agreed upon
5. Narrator attempts to call Uber for separate transport; seller redirects — "just across the street, ride together"
6. Narrator defers out of politeness and inexperience
7. Destination is not across the street — several blocks away, empty lot, off main road (first clear red flag acknowledged retroactively)
8. "Let's switch" — seller parks, initiates key transfer
9. Keys thrown short — narrator bends to retrieve them
10. Seller kicks narrator while bent down, pins him, takes wallet from back pocket
11. Seller flees in truck; keys thrown were fake/decoy
12. Narrator retains phone — immediately cancels bank card, calls police
13. Returns to Burger King; files report
14. No plate number, generic description; ad taken down, email was fake name (video game character)

**Key Detail:** Cash transaction; no plate; decoy keys used to create bend-down vulnerability.

**Outcome:** $4,000 cash stolen, wallet taken, no arrest.

**Tension Devices:** Trust built over distance, redirection from public space, isolation to empty lot, thrown keys as positional trap.

### Story 03 — House Cleaning Stalker

**Narrator Profile:** Female, self-employed house cleaner, Latina (Spanish-speaking), previously employed at a cleaning company.

**Platform:** Craigslist (service listing)

**Setting:** Client's residential home, two floors, no basement agreed upon

2. Client submits video walkthrough; house appears clean/maintained

10. Client appears in archway — requests basement be vacuumed (not part of agreed scope); offers trade: skip unused dining room, do basement floors instead
11. Narrator defers, proposes returning tomorrow for full basement job

- Basement push was singular and escalating despite multiple redirects

**Outcome:** Narrator escapes safely. No payment received. No arrest. Police unable to act.

**Tension Devices:** Gaze fixation, room repositioning, false preservation story with physical contradiction, zero-photo home as environmental red flag, dead-wife slip, escalating basement pressure, cold dismissal, window surveillance at departure.
