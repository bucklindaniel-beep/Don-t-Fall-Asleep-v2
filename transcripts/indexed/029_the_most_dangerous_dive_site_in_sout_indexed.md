## Site
- name: Boesmansgat (Bushman's Hole)
- location: Northern Cape, South Africa; Mount Carmel Game Farm (11,000 acres); edge of Kalahari Desert
- dimensions: Chamber ~833 ft long x 259 ft wide x 927 ft deep (253 m x 79 m x 300 m)
- classification: Third deepest known freshwater cave globally

## Stories

### Story 01 — Eben Leyden Death (1993)
- date: August 1993
- event: Exploratory dive; Eben ran through first air cylinder before descent complete; backup regulator pinned under harness; out of air; panicked; attempted to bolt to surface; died before reaching surface
- subject: Eben Leyden (male; uninvited addition to expedition)
- dive_partner: Boetie Visser; forced to complete decompression stops; could not assist; recovered body to surface; CPR 90+ minutes; declared dead by doctor
- same_expedition: Sheck Exley set world record cave dive of 863 ft (263 m)

### Story 02 — Deon Dreyer Death (1994) and Recovery (2004–2005)
- date_of_death: December 17, 1994
- subject: Deon Dreyer (20, male; 200+ logged dive hours)
- event: Practice dive for Nuno Gomes's second record attempt; blacked out during ascent decompression stop (~200 ft); sank to bottom (~927 ft); cause believed hypercapnia (CO2 poisoning)
- recovery_attempt: Dave Shaw located Deon's body January 8, 2005 during rebreather record dive at ~886 ft
- recovery_dive: January 8, 2005; Dave Shaw (Australian, Hong Kong-based); descended; located Deon; struggled to load body into bag; light snagged on rope; blacked out and drowned 22 minutes into dive
- dave_shaw_outcome: Body found floating at ~66 ft attached by rope to Deon; recovered by Peter; helmet camera footage reviewed; camera rig implicated in death (prevented light-around-neck technique)
- don_shirley: Dave's dive partner; rebreather controller crushed at 800 ft; severe decompression sickness on ascent; permanent inner ear damage; 27 hours decompression treatment
- deon_body: Recovered attached to Dave at ~66 ft; returned to parents 10 days after recovery

### Story 03 — Nuno Gomes World Record Dive (1996)
- date: 1996
- subject: Nuno Gomes (civil engineer, Johannesburg)
- depth_reached: 927 ft / 282.6 m (new world record; beat Sheck Exley's 863 ft)
- complications: Hit bottom hard on hands and knees; kicked up silt cloud; stuck in mud; nitrogen narcosis; helium tremors; inflated buoyancy wings to escape; total dive time 12 hours 15 minutes (descent 14 minutes; 4 minutes freeing from mud)
