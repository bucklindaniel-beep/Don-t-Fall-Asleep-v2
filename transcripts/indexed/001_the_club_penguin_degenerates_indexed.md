## Case Index

### Case 01 — Club Penguin Online / Aiori
- Operator: Aiori (Adiori Adabal), UK-based, early 20s; actual owner nominally Timmy
- Platform: Club Penguin Online (formerly Virtual Penguin); ~6M registered, ~300K active, majority minors
- Mechanics: Admin/mod roles offered in exchange for explicit images from minors; blackmail loop using compromising material to silence staff and victims; swatting and doxing of critics and competitors; hacking competitor accounts and credential stuffing stolen databases; flew to US to meet at least two alleged underage victims in person
- Exposure path: YouTuber Joey Hughes (2020 video series) → YouTuber Cabos (1M+ audience signal boost) → YouTuber Some Ordinary Gamers (viral) → Disney DMCA → shutdown May 2020
- Outcome: Arrested on charges related to illegal material involving minors; conviction status unknown; online presence wiped
- Collateral: Club Penguin Rewritten also shut down; three CPR staff arrested for copyright violations
- Key pattern: Fake resignation (passed server to Kiwi); continued operating under alt account within weeks

### Case 02 — Club Penguin Vanilla / Icy
- Operator: Icy (identity linked to CPO admin role and hacking of Club Penguin Rewritten under Aiori)
- Platform: CP Vanilla; launched after CPO shutdown
- Mechanics: Doxing; hacking; concealed admin role behind deleted Discord role while retaining server ownership
- Exposure path: Reddit post by Puffle Cutie (2022); community pressure
- Outcome: Discord deleted; website taken down within days of exposure; possible relaunch under aliases, unconfirmed

### Case 03 — Super Club Penguin / Dan
- Operator: Dan (Dan Pengui); developer role under owner Samu; Spanish-language server
- Platform: Super Club Penguin / Super CPPS; active since 2013 in various forms; 500K+ players across Latin America
- Mechanics: Explicit conversations with minors; alleged in-person meetup with 14-year-old boy at age 19; misrepresented own age to partners (claimed 22, was 25); replaced server ads with own to pocket ~$300; took all server files when forced out
- Exposure path: YouTuber Mr. Tuki (Spanish) → staff member Hibani coordinated server deletion with ~4 staff members
- Outcome: Super Club Penguin shut down November 11, 2023; Dan retained files via personal OneDrive; no legal outcome documented
- Key pattern: Staff team partially defended Dan despite knowledge; owner Samu attempted apology, rejected by community

### Case 04 — Super Club Penguin / Zenner (mod)
- Operator: Zenner, mod for ~4 years; early 20s during allegations
- Platform: Super Club Penguin Discord
- Mechanics: Sent unsolicited explicit messages and images to underage girls; operated openly under real username with no alias; four documented victims ages 12, 13, 14, 14 (at time, Zenner was 18); multiple additional victims alleged; threatened doxing against accusers
- Exposure path: Community-compiled document submitted to staff; staff took no action; later surfaced via Mr. Tuki and Hibani disclosures
- Outcome: Resurfaced December 2023 under alias "Rem" in Valorant community; no legal action documented

### Case 05 — Super Club Penguin / Jatine
- Operator: Jatine, staff member
- Mechanics: Threatened to expose minor-era information about Hibani unless given Discord owner role; relationship with 15–16-year-old girl; stole identity of former staff member Amy; sought defense from Justin Capro (noted as member of Discord server promoting grooming)
- Exposure path: Hibani public statement on Discord and pinned comment under Mr. Tuki video
- Outcome: Not denied by Jatine; no legal action documented

### Case 06 — Club Penguin Journey / Aman
- Operator: Aman (server creator); launched alpha January 2023; 100K+ accounts, ~1K daily active players within months
- Platform: Club Penguin Journey
- Mechanics: Server exploit (Q system) abused to modify in-game items without account access; patched and compensated (100K coins to affected players); targeted by hacker group Pigeon Patrol (also attacked CPR reboot 2022) via takeover of side Discord "CPJ Cheats"
- Allegations against Aman: Grooming/inappropriate contact with minors — disputed; Aman issued detailed public denial; no corroborated evidence presented
- Outcome: Aman announced stepping down; staff declined to continue due to harassment risk; server revived ~1 month later with new security measures; active as of video publication
- Key pattern: Server targeted specifically because it did not engage in exploitation of user data
