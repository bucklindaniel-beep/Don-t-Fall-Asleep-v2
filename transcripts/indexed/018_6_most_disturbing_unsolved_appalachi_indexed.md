## Story 01 — Dennis Martin

**Entities:** Dennis Martin (6, victim), Bill Martin (father), Harold Key (witness), FBI, National Park Service rangers, National Guard, Green Berets
**Setting:** Great Smoky Mountains National Park, Spence Field, near Appalachian Trail — June 14, 1969
**Status:** Unsolved / missing — never found

**Event Sequence:**
1. Dennis Martin, age 6, on Father's Day camping trip with father, grandfather, and brother near Spence Field
2. Boys split up to prank adults on trail; Dennis goes one way, others another
3. Dennis does not return; father begins immediate search — no response, no movement
4. Hard rainstorm moves in shortly after disappearance, eliminating tracks and scent
5. Search escalates to full operation: rangers, Boy Scouts, horse teams, helicopters, National Guard, Green Berets — up to 1,400 searchers in a single day; 56+ square miles covered
6. Small footprints found near stream, appeared to match Dennis's Oxford shoes — ruled likely a scout's
7. Witness Harold Key claims he heard a scream and saw a rough-looking man carrying something red over his shoulders; timeline and distance do not fit — FBI and rangers dismiss connection
8. Active search called off June 29 — no physical trace found
9. Father maintained belief in abduction; officials lean toward lost-and-died theory; animal attack theory also raised but no blood or torn clothing found

**Mechanics:**
- Child vanishes in seconds on steep, heavily wooded terrain
- Rain wipes forensic trail in real time
- Largest known child search in Smoky Mountains history yields zero physical evidence
- Abduction, accident, and animal attack all lack conclusive support

## Story 02 — Scott Lily

**Entities:** Scott Lily (30, victim), FBI, National Park Service, US Forest Service, Virginia State Police
**Setting:** Appalachian Trail, Nelson County Virginia, near Cow Camp Gap Shelter — July–August 2011
**Status:** Homicide — unsolved, no suspect identified

**Event Sequence:**
1. Scott Lily, 30, sets out June 15, 2011 southbound on the Appalachian Trail from Maryland toward Georgia
2. Reaches Virginia mountains of Nelson County, near the Priest (above 4,000 ft)
3. Last known contact approximately July 31; believed heading toward Cow Camp Gap Shelter
4. August 12, 2011 — hikers find a body in a shallow grave near the Cow Camp Gap side trail; identified as Scott Lily
5. Estimated dead approximately 12 days — placing death near end of July
6. January 2012 — medical examiner rules death homicide; cause: asphyxia by suffocation
7. Missing items: backpack, trail shoes, Nintendo game, Appalachian Trail handbook
8. FBI leads investigation: 100+ pieces of evidence collected, 270+ miles of trail covered, 83 hiker interviews across multiple states and two foreign countries
9. $10,000 reward announced one year after murder — no arrest, no suspect publicly identified

**Mechanics:**
- Body deliberately concealed in shallow grave in a well-used recreation area
- Suffocation as cause of death indicates deliberate killing, not opportunistic violence
- Stolen gear raises robbery motive but never confirmed
- Federal jurisdiction (national forest land) — FBI primary
- High-traffic trail location increases risk of discovery but also suspect pool

## Story 03 — Derek Luking

**Entities:** Derek Luking (24, missing), Ryan (roommate), Michael Giovani Kaini (second disappearance), Great Smoky Mountains National Park rangers
**Setting:** Great Smoky Mountains National Park, Newfound Gap (NC/TN border) — March 2012
**Status:** Unsolved — missing, no remains found

**Event Sequence:**
1. Derek Luking, 24, does not show up for work March 15, 2012; no phone contact
2. Roommate Ryan alerted; family drives overnight from Virginia to Tennessee
3. Computer searches reveal Great Smoky Mountains NP research and a hotel reservation — planned destination
4. March 17 — surveillance footage captures Derek leaving Microtel Inn and Suites, Cherokee NC at approximately 4:00 a.m. with only a daypack; last confirmed sighting
5. Hotel room contains a Bible on the bed and a liquor bottle on the floor
6. Same morning — family finds Derek's white Ford Escape at Newfound Gap parking area; note inside reads: "Do not follow me"
7. Vehicle contains large amount of outdoor gear (tent, sleeping bag, food, maps, cutting tools, lights, cord) — nearly all left behind; wallet and cash also in car
8. Search: approximately 60 people and 3 dog teams, 70+ miles of trail — no confirmed sightings at Newfound Gap despite sunny day with visitors
9. Around same time, Michael Giovani Kaini disappears in same area — last seen March 18; his vehicle found parked unusually; his remains later found in the park — cases possibly unrelated
10. Derek Luking never found; case remains open

**Mechanics:**
- Note and survival gear create contradictory signals: possible intentional disappearance vs. planned wilderness outing abandoned mid-execution
- Gear left behind eliminates rational survival logic
- No blood, witnesses, or signs of attack
- Second simultaneous disappearance in same zone adds anomaly but no confirmed link

## Story 04 — Louise Chabot (rendered in transcript as "Luis Shapoo")

**Entities:** Louise Chabot (52, victim), boyfriend (Quebec), NH Fish and Game, Mountain Rescue Services, Androscoggin Valley Search and Rescue, US Forest Service, US Army National Guard
**Setting:** Pinkham Notch, White Mountains NH, Lost Pond Trail — November 2001
**Status:** Homicide — unsolved, no suspect identified

**Event Sequence:**
1. Louise, 52, experienced hiker from Quebec, drives to Pinkham Notch Lodge alone after group trip falls through — November 15, 2001
2. Arrives mid-afternoon, asks visitor center clerk for short walk; directed to Lost Pond Trail (directly across the road from the lodge)
3. Does not return; boyfriend contacts NH authorities when she fails to return for work Monday November 19
4. Silver Ford Focus station wagon remains in visitor center parking lot — no departure
5. Search launches in severe conditions: high winds, blowing snow, windchills well below zero
6. Second day — US Army National Guard helicopter joins search; still no result
7. November 22 (Thanksgiving) — body found by NH Fish and Game, close to trailhead near well-used area
8. November 24 — medical examiner rules death homicide; Louise had been stabbed multiple times
9. No suspect ever identified; case remains unsolved

**Mechanics:**
- Short, familiar-adjacent trail (across road from lodge) rules out deep backcountry accident
- Scene staged to appear as wilderness tragedy — solo hiker, known dangerous mountain region, bad weather
- Homicide ruling collapses the assumed narrative (weather/exposure)
- Proximity to lodge and parking area indicates attack was accessible, not remote

## Story 05 — Trenny Gibson

**Entities:** Trenny Gibson (16, missing), Robert Simpson (classmate/witness), Wayne Dunlap (teacher), Berean High School (Knoxville TN), FBI, Great Smoky Mountains NP rangers
**Setting:** Great Smoky Mountains National Park, Andrews Bald / Clingmans Dome area — October 8, 1976
**Status:** Unsolved — missing, no remains found

**Event Sequence:**
1. October 8, 1976 — Trenny Gibson, 16, on school trip to Great Smoky Mountains with approximately 40 students from Berean High School, Knoxville TN
2. Underdressed: left coat in Knoxville, hiking in blouse, sweater, jeans, tennis shoes; classmate Robert Simpson lends her his wool jacket
3. Students told to stay on trail, not go past Andrews Bald, return to bus by 3:30 p.m.
4. After lunch at Andrews Bald, Trenny separates from Robert around 1:30 p.m. — she heads back toward bus, he stays
5. Multiple students see Trenny on trail; one girl walks briefly with her; a group invites her to sit — she declines, appears to be in a hurry
6. Approximately 2:50 p.m. — witnesses see Trenny stop, bend down, and step off trail to the right; last confirmed sighting
7. 3:30 p.m. — students at bus notice she is missing; teacher Wayne Dunlap begins search immediately
8. 4:30 p.m. — rangers contacted; official search begins as evening conditions deteriorate: rain, fog, wind, falling temps, wet leaves
9. Broken undergrowth found near last sighting; three cigarette butts and a partially consumed beer can found nearby — possible third party in area
10. Tracking dogs follow scent past Kuwohi Tower lookout approximately 1.5 miles before scent disappears — possibly toward road
11. 756 total searchers over the course of full search; FBI opens investigation; all sightings and leads go cold
12. No body, no confirmed crime scene — case remains open nearly 50 years later

**Mechanics:**
- Disappears in daylight on marked trail on school trip — maximum witness environment
- Singular act (stepping off trail) is the only departure point — no extended lead-up
- Physical evidence (cigarette butts, beer, broken undergrowth) suggests possible third party
- Scent trail ending near road raises vehicle-involved abduction as possibility

## Story 06 — Thelma "Polly" Melton

**Entities:** Thelma "Polly" Melton (58, missing), Pauline Cannon (friend/witness), Trula Gudger (friend/witness), Robert Melton (husband), National Park Service rangers
**Setting:** Deep Creek Trail, Great Smoky Mountains National Park — November 25, 1981
**Status:** Unsolved — missing; declared legally dead 1988; NPS still lists as missing

**Event Sequence:**
1. November 25, 1981 — Polly Melton, 58, begins afternoon hike on Deep Creek Trail with two friends, Pauline Cannon and Trula Gudger; Polly's husband Robert waits at trailhead
2. Polly described as heavy smoker with high blood pressure; trail is familiar, well-used, and near a parking area
3. Approximately 4:00 p.m. — group reaches turnaround point and begins return; Polly, who had been walking slowly, suddenly accelerates sharply
4. Friend jokes about racing; Polly laughs, waves once, and continues ahead over a rise
5. Friends come over the same rise — Polly is gone; no trail debris, no screams, no sign of passage
6. Friends return to trailhead; Robert confirms Polly has not arrived; search begins
7. Approximately 6:00 p.m. — rangers contacted; formal search begins around 7:00 p.m.
8. Tracking dogs brought to last-seen point — circle the area and howl; scent trail does not lead into woods or down trail; appears to end at the rise
9. 150 volunteers and 9 search dogs over 5-day search; helicopter aerial survey after leaf fall; nothing found
10. Post-disappearance details surface: pastor notes Polly had seemed depressed since her mother's death; Robert's Valium prescription found missing — raises disorientation or self-harm theory
11. Unusual pre-disappearance behavior noted: skipped volunteer shift, made unusual phone calls
12. Months later: check made out to Polly cashed in Birmingham AL — person who cashed it never identified
13. Declared legally dead 1988; NPS continues to list as missing

**Mechanics:**
- Disappears on a busy, popular trail near a parking area with witnesses in sight
- Last action (wave) is calm, not distressed — no behavioral signal of flight or struggle
- Scent trail termination anomaly: dogs circle and howl at a fixed point with no outbound trail
- Multiple post-disappearance financial and behavioral anomalies introduce voluntary departure theory
- Road and parking area proximity supports both abduction and voluntary exit theories
- No physical trace in four decades despite repeated searches
