### Story 01 — West Hobart Haunted House (Tasmania, Australia; late 1980s–mid 1990s)
- location: West Hobart, Tasmania, Australia
- event: Residents of a house exhibited symptoms of apparent mental deterioration; attributed by locals to haunting; investigation revealed datura vine dropping leaves into family's rainwater tank; family slowly poisoned by micro-doses of hallucinogenic plant
- plant: Datura (nightshade family; hallucinogenic; can cause delirium, paralysis, death in high doses)

### Story 02 — Cropsy / Andre Rand (Staten Island, New York; 1970s–2004)
- location: Staten Island, New York, USA
- subject: Andre Rand (b. 1944, Manhattan)
- background: Worked at Willowbrook State School for children with disabilities; arrested 1969 for attempted attack on 9-year-old girl; served 16 months
- crimes: Prime suspect in disappearances and deaths of multiple young girls across Staten Island throughout 1970s–1980s; 1983 arrested for taking 11 children from YMCA (10-month sentence); 1988 convicted of murder of Jennifer Schweiger (12, Down syndrome); sentenced 25 years to life; 2004 convicted of kidnapping Holly Ann Hughes; sentence extended to 2037
- legend_origin: Urban legend of "Cropsy" (hooked hand, scarred killer) predated and became associated with Rand's crimes

### Story 03 — The North Pond Hermit (Maine, USA; 1986–2013)
- location: North Pond area, Kennebec Valley, Maine, USA
- subject: Christopher Thomas Knight (b. ~1966; Lawrence High School class of 1984)
- event: Disappeared into woods at age 20 (~1986); lived alone in nylon tent in unheated woods for ~27 years; survived Maine winters at -20°F; committed 1,000+ break-ins at North Pond cabins (food, propane, toiletries, books); spoken only one word to a human in 27 years ("Hi" to a hiker)
- arrest: April 2013; officer arrested him in woods
- sentence: 7 months in prison; afterward disappeared again into rural Maine life

### Story 04 — Purple Aki (Northwest England; 1980s–present)
- location: Liverpool and wider northwest England
- subject: Akinwale Arobieke ("Purple Aki")
- behavior: Approached young men and teenage boys; requested to feel their muscles; asked them to perform squats; offered exercise and nutrition advice
- serious_incident: 1986; 16-year-old Gary Kelly fled from Aki to a train station; jumped onto railroad tracks; electrocuted
- conviction: Involuntary manslaughter; sentenced 2.5 years; overturned on appeal; awarded £35,000 compensation
- subsequent_record: In and out of jail 1988–2015 for repeated harassment; multiple convictions; 2003: 6 years for 15 charges of harassment and witness intimidation; forbidden from waiting outside schools
- status (as of 2023): Alive; reportedly seen in Leeds asking men to feel their muscles

### Story 05 — Le Loyon (Fribourg Forest, Switzerland; early 2000s–2013)
- location: Fribourg forest near Maules, western Switzerland
- description: Figure ~6 ft tall; wore vintage gas mask, boiler suit, cloak; walked same paths regularly
- public_record: First photograph published by Swiss-French newspaper Le Matin (2013); drew international attention; figure named "Le Loyon"
- last_sighting: June 2013 (local woman Maryanne de Clue)
- resolution: Clothing found in woods with written note; note accused media of murdering a harmless being and stated daily walks (described as "happiness therapy") had to end; fate unconfirmed; never seen again after 2013

### Story 06 — The Bunnyman (Fairfax County, Virginia, USA; 1970)
- location: Colchester Road, Fairfax County, Virginia, USA
- event: 1970; couple parked in driveway; man appeared, yelled about trespassing, threw hatchet at car; man wore light-colored clothing; couple never clearly described bunny costume in original report
- legend_evolution: Media and public added bunny ears; legend grew over decades to include full bunny suit, axe-wielding, hanging of children; bridge renamed Bunnyman Bridge
- status: No identified perpetrator; bridge remains a regional cultural attraction
