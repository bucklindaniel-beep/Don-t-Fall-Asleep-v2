## Story 01 — Trapped in the Fire

**Entities:** Damonte Cheryl (30, victim), Ruben Holmes (victim), Charlotte NC fire department, construction companies
**Setting:** 7741 Liberty Road Drive, Charlotte NC — seven-story apartment complex under construction
**Date:** May 18th, 2024
**Status:** Confirmed fatalities; companies fined; civil suit ongoing

**Event Sequence:**
1. 9:00 a.m. — fire reported at construction site; 17 workers clocked in that morning
2. Firefighters arrive; fire already advanced; structure is primarily wood and insulation on upper floors
3. 15 of 17 workers evacuated; Damonte Cheryl and Ruben Holmes confirmed missing — on floor 6
4. Damonte opens Facebook Live from inside the building; footage shows thick smoke, both men visible, waving for rescue
5. Floor 6 has one access stairway — on the opposite end of the building; path to it blocked; jumping is not survivable
6. Crane deployed with rescue basket; crane operator believes he spots the men but smoke prevents approach; operator himself requires rescue
7. Rescue team climbs the stairway toward the men, guided by their shouting; before reaching them, evacuation order issued — structure at collapse risk
8. Firefighters make one final verbal call; no response; evacuate
9. Structure falls silent; fire consumes building; interior temperature reaches 2,000°F; building collapses
10. Damonte and Ruben are the only fatalities
11. Live stream watched in real time by friends, family, and Damonte's mother; his brother arrived on scene and pleaded with firefighters to watch the footage
12. Investigation reveals: organic foam insulation used — highly flammable; application machine caught fire; no smoke detectors or alarms installed; temporary stairway recently removed from the men's section
13. Companies fined $50,000; families pursuing civil suit

**Observable Mechanics:**
- Structural failures compounded: flammable material + no alarms + removed stairway + single blocked exit + smoke too thick for crane
- Live stream used as both plea for rescue and means of documenting location — neither was sufficient
- Mother and family viewed the footage in real time; brother arrived on scene during the event

## Story 02 — Johnny Cashman

**Entities:** Johnny Cashman (38, victim), Steven Church (person of interest), Lynchburg Virginia PD, Johnny's family and ex-girlfriend
**Setting:** Lynchburg, Virginia — apartment building
**Dates:** April 19, 2022 (death); October 2022 (case closed)
**Status:** Officially closed — natural medical emergency; Steven Church cleared

**Event Sequence:**
1. Johnny, 38, is found dead in his apartment on April 19, 2022, after six days without contact with family
2. Officers classify death as natural GI bleed; describe it as instant and painless; advise no autopsy needed; body cremated
3. Police collect neighbor's security camera footage the same day
4. Ten days later, ex-girlfriend and her mother return for belongings; find blood throughout — pooled, wiped on doors, splattered on walls with streaking handprints
5. Neighbor reveals security footage: Johnny arrives at 3:03 p.m. normally; moments later, sounds of struggle from inside — shouting, loud thuds; unit goes quiet
6. 7 minutes after Johnny enters, an unknown man exits; wipes door handle with his sleeve; pauses visibly shaken at the base of the stairs; composes himself and leaves
7. 1 minute later, Johnny is heard calling for help from inside; then silence
8. No further activity until police arrive five days later
9. Footage made public; investigators revise account — bleed was not instant; blood spread consistent with vomiting; unknown man identified as Steven Church
10. Steven Church tells police: verbal-only argument about rent; he left; saw Johnny hunched over but not in crisis; wiped fingerprints because of prior felony record
11. Audio on footage: no argument audible; phrases suggest physical altercation; only one voice heard throughout
12. October 2022 — case closed; Church cleared; basis: his shoe prints not in the blood; Johnny's prints were
13. No autopsy possible — body already cremated; case structurally unresolvable

**Observable Mechanics:**
- Police had the footage from day one; classified the death without reviewing it fully
- Cremation eliminated the only means of establishing cause beyond doubt
- Steven's account directly contradicted by the audio — no argument audible, phrases suggest physical struggle
- Door handle wiped with sleeve on exit; visible pause and distress at bottom of stairs
- Johnny's blood-covered phone found in the unit — 911 was never called

## Story 03 — Bethany Martin

**Entities:** Bethany Martin (17), unnamed 16-year-old, Marcus Adams (25, deceased)
**Setting:** San Antonio, Texas — roadside drainage ditch
**Date:** July 2019
**Status:** Resolved — Bethany charged, minimal sentencing; medallion returned

**Event Sequence:**
1. Girls discover body of 25-year-old Marcus Adams in a drainage ditch; he had taken his own life that morning
2. Girls call police and are sent home
3. Before police arrive: 16-year-old films Bethany approaching the body and removing a necklace medallion; chain tossed into grass
4. Girls return home; film a TikTok presenting themselves as shocked and traumatized; post it publicly
5. 16-year-old posts the theft footage to Snapchat story
6. A viewer downloads the unedited Snapchat footage and reposts it to the Next Door app
7. Members of Marcus Adams's family — some not yet informed of his death — encounter the footage on Next Door
8. Both girls questioned by police; Bethany charged with theft of a human corpse; 16-year-old's charges not publicly released
9. Bethany later posts a TikTok claiming she was pressured by the 16-year-old; received negatively
10. Medallion returned to the Adams family; Bethany avoids significant jail time

**Observable Mechanics:**
- Audio of the theft footage clearly indicates enjoyment during the act
- TikTok presenting grief was posted same day as the theft footage
- Family members learned of the death through unedited theft footage on a public platform
- Bethany's later account positioned herself as a victim of peer pressure

## Story 04 — Janelle Matthews

**Entities:** Janelle Matthews (12, victim), Steven Panky (convicted killer), Jim Matthews (father), Ronald Reagan (referenced), Weld County construction crew
**Setting:** Greeley, Colorado (disappearance and burial); Weld County, Colorado (remains); Idaho (Panky's later residence)
**Dates:** December 20, 1984 (disappearance); July 23, 2019 (remains found); 2019–2020 (arrest and conviction)
**Status:** Resolved — Panky convicted; life sentence; parole eligible 2040

**Event Sequence:**
1. December 20, 1984 — Janelle, 12, dropped off at home ~8:15 p.m. after choir recital; calls father to confirm she's home; approximately one hour later father returns to find house empty
2. Coat and shoes left behind despite freezing weather; footprints in snow leading to a window — consistent with someone watching the house
3. Case becomes national news; Janelle is one of the first milk carton kids; President Reagan references her disappearance in a speech
4. Case goes unsolved for over three decades
5. Post-crime: Steven Panky, living in the same area, takes his wife on an unplanned California trip the day after the disappearance with no prior notice
6. Panky's car subsequently catches fire and is scrapped — no investigation possible
7. Panky begins obsessively following news coverage; has wife read every article about Janelle aloud, repeatedly; makes frequent unsolicited calls to police providing leads; claims to fear being blamed despite claiming not to have known Janelle
8. During some calls, provides details about the case not made public
9. Panky eventually moves to Idaho without apparent reason
10. 2008 — Panky's son Carl dies (unrelated murder, fully solved); at Carl's memorial, Panky is observed sobbing at the grave and saying aloud: "I hope God didn't allow this to happen because of Janelle Matthews"
11. 2019 — Panky, running for governor of Idaho, publishes Facebook post calling for a fresh investigation into Janelle's case; argues police were wrong to focus on kidnapping and murder theory
12. July 23, 2019 — construction crew installing pipeline in isolated Weld County field unearths child's remains
13. Remains confirmed as Janelle Matthews; still wearing recital clothing; cause of death: single gunshot wound to the head; killed the night she disappeared
14. Months after his Facebook post, Panky arrested for abduction and murder
15. Evidence primarily circumstantial — unplanned trip, car fire, police calls with non-public details, memorial statement, Facebook post
16. Convicted; sentenced to life in prison; parole eligible 2040; conviction came 12,634 days after disappearance

**Observable Mechanics:**
- Perpetrator spent 35 years unable to disengage from the case — unsolicited police calls, media obsession, non-public details disclosed
- Memorial statement is the only direct self-implicating utterance; observed by his ex-wife; introduced at trial
- Facebook post in 2019 calling for fresh investigation into his own crime drew public scrutiny at a moment of maximum visibility (gubernatorial campaign)
- Physical evidence was circumstantial throughout; conviction was reached without direct forensic link to the burial site
