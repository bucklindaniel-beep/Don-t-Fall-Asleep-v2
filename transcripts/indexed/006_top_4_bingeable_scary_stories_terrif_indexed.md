### Story 01 — Susan's Haunted House in Southern California (Haunting / Poltergeist)

**Type:** Paranormal / haunting
**Format:** Third-person narrative
**Setting:** Southern California house ("the Sanctuary"); 2006

**Core Arc:**
Susan, her daughter Jamie, boyfriend Matt, and Matt's daughter Marie moved into a house they called their Sanctuary. Poltergeist-like activity escalated from peanut butter melting and objects rearranged, to the word "cat" written across all walls after Matt smashed a figurine offered as a peace offering. Family fractured internally as each blamed others. Activity became violent — television controls, bleach overturned on clothes, rocking chair moving, pictures falling, entity attempting to trap and burn Jamie inside the house. Family went bankrupt to leave; five neighboring houses also became vacant.

### Story 02 — The Dead Woman Calling 911 (Paranormal / Unexplained)

**Type:** Paranormal / unexplained
**Format:** Third-person narrative
**Setting:** Reno, Nevada area; 2011 onward

**Core Arc:**
An elderly widow living 20 miles east of Reno began calling 911 multiple times per week — first for mundane help (boiling water, thermostat), then believing she was calling her deceased husband John. One day she called sounding frantic. Responders found her dead — rigor mortis suggested she had been dead for hours. Yet the 911 call had just come from her address. Calls continued after her death: same voice, same distress, same plea for John. Calls persisted for a year — even after her house was broken into and burned to the ground. Phone company confirmed her line had been disconnected. Calls stopped abruptly one year after her death.

### Story 04 — The DeFeo Family Murders / Amityville (True Crime / Paranormal)

**Type:** True crime / paranormal
**Format:** Third-person narrative
**Setting:** Amityville, New York; November 13, 1974

**Core Arc:**
Ron DeFeo Jr. woke at approximately 1 a.m., picked up a rifle, and methodically walked through his family's home shooting all six family members as they slept — parents, brothers (ages 12 and 9), sisters (ages 13 and 18). None showed signs of struggle. The rifle was one of the loudest available; heard a mile away indoors. Ron claimed a shadowy figure emerged from the basement and commanded him to "catch them, kill them," and that he felt possessed. He was sentenced to life. One year later the Lutz family moved in; left after 28 days citing voices from the basement. Ed and Lorraine Warren investigated; declared the house haunted. Famous photograph taken on second floor — claimed to show the ghost of John DeFeo. House still stands; bought and sold repeatedly.
