## Story Index

### Story 01
- **Setting:** Remote mountain trail outside Denver, Colorado; 3-day backpacking route; campsite on a cliff ledge 3/4 up a mountain with valley views
- **Narrator Profile:** Adult male; camping with brother Jason; planned trip specifically to avoid crowded trails
- **Core Event Sequence:**
  1. Day 1: 8-mile hike to cliffside campsite; only one other car (dusty SUV) at the trailhead
  2. Evening at camp: fire going, brothers talking for roughly 2 hours
  3. Footsteps heard on the trail; a man walks into firelight — no headlamp, large grin
  4. Man claims to be a park ranger; says the section is closed for the season and they must leave immediately
  5. Brothers know the park is open and that they have valid permits obtained the day prior; also know rangers do not patrol trails at night
  6. Man insists they pack up and follow him back to the trailhead — now, immediately
  7. Jason refuses; man turns and walks back into the dark with the same grin
  8. Brothers stay up all night feeding the fire; hear possible returning footsteps but the man does not reappear
  9. At dawn they hike out early; the dusty SUV is still at the trailhead
- **Resolution:** Police notified; no outcome. Brothers conclude the man was attempting to lead them into the dark under false pretenses.
- **Key Detail:** The man's consistent grin; the lone SUV still at the trailhead on exit, raising concern for the other hiker
- **Narrator Framing:** Retrospective; what the man intended is explicitly left open but characterized as likely horrible

### Story 02
- **Setting:** Remote campground, not described by region; far out, not scenic, few visitors
- **Narrator Profile:** Adult; formerly camped with college friends; now camping solo after schedules diverged
- **Core Event Sequence:**
  1. Sets up camp; a man with only a small backpack (no tent, no gear) arrives and stays at the far end of the campground
  2. Around 6pm the man leaves — late to be walking back given darkness and trail length
  3. Narrator eats, hangs lantern outside tent, turns it off, goes to sleep
  4. Middle of the night: wakes to footsteps approaching; grabs lantern, unzips tent
  5. A man-shaped figure is standing roughly 20 feet away — appears to be the same person from earlier
  6. Figure is completely still; does not respond to narrator calling out
  7. Narrator retrieves pocket knife and waits; 15 minutes pass with figure barely moving
  8. Figure turns and walks away in the same direction as before; narrator can see from the motion that the figure had been facing them the entire time
- **Resolution:** Narrator stays up the rest of the night; leaves at dawn without further incident. Has not been camping since.
- **Key Detail:** The 15 minutes of silent, motionless staring; the realization the figure was facing toward the narrator the whole time
- **Narrator Framing:** Retrospective; narrator frames the scariest implication as what might have happened if they had not woken up

### Story 03
- **Setting:** Backcountry campsite by a lake; roughly 1 mile from the trailhead; narrator's own established camp
- **Narrator Profile:** Adult; not an avid hiker; spending a weekend at a familiar backcountry lake spot
- **Core Event Sequence:**
  1. Returns from a supply run to find a second tent set up at the far edge of the lake; no one visible
  2. Fishes for 3 hours; tent owner never appears
  3. Lights campfire at dusk; immediately sees a shadow move at the tree line just beyond firelight range
  4. Assumes it is the other camper gathering sticks; relaxes
  5. Half hour later: four men emerge from the forest and walk directly toward the campsite
  6. Narrator stands and asks what they need; no one responds
  7. Two men move behind narrator toward the tent; two others push narrator away from camp
  8. Narrator watches the two search through belongings while the other two continue forcing him away from camp and into the dark forest
  9. In the dark between trees, the group stops; one of the men reaches for something
  10. Narrator bolts, runs by feel and general sense of direction back to the trail and straight to his truck
- **Resolution:** Police report filed; almost all belongings stolen; no suspects identified. Narrator describes himself as "beyond lucky" to have escaped.
- **Key Detail:** The deliberate separation from camp and from light; the tent at the lake edge as likely staging or distraction
- **Narrator Framing:** Retrospective; narrator states the intended outcome of being dragged into the forest is "probably obvious"
