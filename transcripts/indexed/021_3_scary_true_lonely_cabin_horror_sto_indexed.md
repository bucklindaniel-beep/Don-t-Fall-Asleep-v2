**Setting:** Rented cabin in the woods, winter, unspecified rural location
**Status:** Unresolved — Lucy's identity unknown; no record found on social media or inmate databases

2. Less than a week before the trip, Randy and his girlfriend break up; narrator offers refund; Randy insists on proceeding

15. Randy replaces the knife without confronting Lucy; group drives home in tense silence

17. Stacy searches for Lucy on social media and inmate records over subsequent years — no results found; no record of this person exists under that name

## Story 02 — The Man in the Suit

**Entities:** Victor (narrator), unnamed man in suit, one police officer

**Status:** Unresolved — man never identified; motive unknown

**Event Sequence:**

4. Saturday night — drinks alone; around 11:00 p.m., hears a knock at the front door; looks through door windows — no one visible; second knock follows
5. Victor turns on front light — a man is standing outside: clean-cut, approximately 6 ft, athletic build, clean-shaven, slicked-back hair, wearing a suit; no visual signs of distress or disorientation
6. Victor asks through the door what the man wants; after a long, unnatural pause, man claims he was driving with his girlfriend, something happened, he has been walking for hours looking for her, and he is now lost; asks to come inside to call for help
7. Victor declines; tells man to wait at the end of the driveway; will call for help from inside
8. Man says again he needs to come inside; states something is outside and he will be "in trouble" if he doesn't get in
9. Victor says he is calling police and man can wait or leave; as Victor walks away, man says he wants to "hide inside" the cabin and needs to hide
10. Victor grabs phone; man begins aggressively pounding on door
11. Victor runs to the door yelling; stops midsentence — the man is laughing; his face is pressed against the window glass, inches from Victor's face

15. Victor tells officer everything; officer notes no reports from surrounding residents; this is the first such call he has received
16. Officer suggests driving to a hotel; Victor discloses he has been drinking; officer offers to drive him
17. Walking to patrol car, officer reacts with surprise; points to Victor's car — all four tires are slashed/deflated
18. Officer drives Victor to a nearby hotel; Victor contacts his father, who retrieves him the next morning
19. Narrator never returns to the cabin; does not accept a refund; hires someone else to clean and vacate

- Emotional affect described as flat or absent throughout, contrasting with the circumstances claimed

## Story 03 — The Attic Man

**Entities:** Narrator (male), deceased father, unidentified male squatter, local police
**Setting:** Narrator's late father's inherited cabin — rural, unspecified; attic/crawl space
**Status:** Squatter identified visually; fled before police arrival; no further information

**Event Sequence:**
1. Narrator's father passes away approximately 6 months prior to events; leaves narrator the cabin in his will
2. To prepare cabin for sale, narrator travels to the cabin alone to clean and assess repairs
3. On arrival — strong smell of death/decay throughout the cabin; no obvious source found
4. Cabin interior unchanged from narrator's childhood (approximately 20+ years prior); same furniture and layout
5. Narrator spends several hours deep cleaning the main floor
6. During cleaning — repeated sounds of rustling and movement from above; determines they are coming from the attic/crawl space
7. Attic accessed via pull-cord in back hallway; retracts to a ladder
8. First attic visit: discovers extensive trash (food wrappers, empty beer cans, garbage scattered throughout); dismisses it as father's mess; smell is severe; leaves after brief inspection; removes some trash
9. Goes to bed; wakes during the night to rustling sounds again — clearly from directly above
10. Returns to back hallway: attic door latch is open and ladder is hanging down (was latched when he left it); a snack wrapper is on the ground at the base of the ladder
11. Picks up wrapper, goes to kitchen to discard it; sits on living room couch in low lamplight
12. While sitting, narrator notices a blanket in the corner of the room covering something next to the recliner; did not see it during cleaning
13. Approaches blanket; stops — sees what appears to be the bottom of a boot protruding from the edge
14. Watches the blanket: it shifts visibly; someone is breathing underneath it; blanket moves with the breath
15. Narrator retreats to kitchen, grabs phone and keys, exits cabin and drives down driveway
16. While reversing, looks back — a "mangy-looking" man runs out the front door; does not chase the car; stands in front of the cabin
17. Narrator calls local police and reports the incident
18. Police explain: cabin had been unoccupied for approximately a year before the father's death; a person likely moved in for warmth and stayed when no one came; estimate he was there approximately one year based on the smell
19. Police arrive — squatter is gone; no arrest; no identification made
20. Narrator never re-enters the cabin; hires a crew to clear it out without explanation; sells the cabin as-is

**Observable Mechanics:**
- Smell of decay on arrival is consistent with long-term occupation in poor sanitary conditions
- Trash in attic is the first material evidence of long-term presence — initially misattributed to father
- Attic latch found open and new wrapper at ladder base are the behavioral tells that someone is active and present
- Blanket in living room during the night, not present during daytime cleaning, confirms the occupant had been moving through the space
- Boot protrusion and breathing blanket movement constitute a staged concealment that failed at close range
- Squatter's exit behavior (standing in front of cabin, not pursuing) suggests non-confrontational disposition
- Police assessment (one year of occupation) is consistent with all observed evidence
