### Story 01 — The Paradox of Horror (Philosophical Essay)

**Type:** Analytical / Academic monologue
**Format:** Single continuous argument
**Subject:** Philosophy of horror as a genre

**Core Argument:**
Horror is paradoxical because it generates an emotional state — horror — that combines fear and disgust, both typically unpleasant, yet audiences actively seek it out.

**Key Concepts Extracted:**

| Concept | Description |
|---|---|
| The Paradox of Horror | How audiences enjoy a genre that produces unpleasant emotional states |
| Horror as compound emotion | Horror = fear + disgust |
| Disgust in horror | Monsters depicted as oozing, decaying, slimy — triggering gag response |
| Fear vs. disgust | Fear can be enjoyed (skydiving, roller coasters); disgust is harder to explain |
| Psychoanalytic approach | ID uses disgust to fool superego into permitting suppressed erotic desire |
| Critique of psychoanalytic approach | Not comprehensive — doesn't account for monsters without erotic dimension |
| Tradeoff theory (Carroll's own) | Disgust is traded for fascination; horror satisfies cognitive/epistemological curiosity |
| Curiosity as core driver | Horror addresses the unknown, unthinkable, unimaginable |
| Discovery structure | Horror narratives follow a process of revealing the monster/cause |
| Horror as descendant of fairground anomalies | Like the cow with two heads — spectacle of the unnatural |

**Argument Progression:**
1. Horror is omnipresent and popular (cultural observation)
2. This popularity is paradoxical — horror produces unpleasant states
3. Horror = fear + disgust
4. Disgust is especially hard to explain enjoying
5. Psychoanalytic answer: ID uses disgust to mask desire → rejected as incomplete
6. Simple enjoyment-of-fear answer → doesn't explain disgust
7. Carroll's answer: tradeoff between disgust and fascination; curiosity drives engagement
8. Horror narratives structured as discovery processes
9. Curiosity is the engine — horror addresses the unknown
10. Not all horror rewards curiosity; great horror does
