## Stories

### Story 01 — Sunshine Mine Fire, Idaho (1972)
- date: May 2, 1972
- location: Sunshine Mine, Coeur d'Alene Mining District, Idaho, USA
- background: Silver mine; discovered 1884 by True and Dennis Blake; one of world's most productive silver mines (364 million oz over 97 years); tunnels up to 6,000 ft deep
- event: Fire originated between 3,500 and 3,700 levels (~4,000 ft deep); smoke detected ~11:40 AM; evacuation ordered 12:03 PM; fire on air-intake side circulated carbon monoxide through main tunnels; Jewel Shaft used as primary escape; hoist stopped at 1:02 PM (hoist operator died at post); 79 escaped
- outcome: 91 deaths; 2 survivors found alive at 5,200 ft depth; cause of fire undetermined (area destroyed by heat/collapse)
- legal_impact: Catalyst for Mine Safety and Health Act of 1977; required ventilation improvements, emergency plans, disaster training, personal oxygen tanks

### Story 02 — Lassing Talc Mine Collapse, Austria (1998)
- date: July 17, 1998
- location: Lassing Talc Mine, Eastern Alps, north of Lassing, Austria; above nearby village
- background: Mine 70+ years old; 607 ft deep; unapproved upper level dug under village
- event: Rainfall softened ground; water entered mine; mudslide blocked passages; 34 miners working; 6 at unapproved level; all escaped except Georg (24, male); trapped in safety bunker
- rescue_attempt: 9-miner rescue team + geologist entered to shore up ceilings; at 10:00 PM second collapse; crater width of football field formed in village center; 2 houses collapsed; 18 cracked; all 10 rescue team members killed
- georg_survival: Trapped in safety bunker; only air came through gaps in wet soil; drank seeping water; survived 9 days
- georg_rescue: Parallel drill shaft completed; team broke through Georg's chamber; hoisted out at approximately 7:00 PM on day 9 (July 25 or 26)
- legal: Mine director and deputy director convicted of criminal negligence for submitting misleading plans
- bodies: 10 rescue team members never recovered

### Story 03 — Julen Rosello Well Fall, Totalán, Spain (2019)
- date: January 13, 2019 (fell); January 24–25, 2019 (recovered)
- location: Private land near Totalán, Málaga province, Spain; borehole ~360 ft (110 m) deep, <10 inches wide
- subject: Julen Rosello (2, male); parents Joseph and Victoria Rosello (previously lost son Oscar, 2017, rare heart defect; also lost a pregnancy)
- event: Julen slipped into uncovered borehole on cousin Daniel's property; heard crying ~30 seconds then silence; blockage of dirt at ~246 ft obstructed camera; Julen below obstruction
- rescue: Two parallel shafts drilled; diamond drills, micro-explosives, pneumatic hammers; 200+ local villagers held vigil; rescuers reached chamber at 1:25 AM on January 25 (236 hours after start)
- outcome: Julen found dead; killed on impact falling ~30+ stories; had been dead from start
- legal: Cousin Daniel pleaded guilty; ordered to pay 180,000 euros damages + 660,000 euros rescue costs; suspended prison sentence
