### Case 01 — Sherry Rasmussen / Stephanie Lazarus
- Victim: Sherry Rasmussen, 27, Director of Nursing, Los Angeles; married 3 months to John Rutten
- Perpetrator: Stephanie Lazarus, LAPD officer; John's college ex-girlfriend
- Date of murder: February 24, 1986; Canoga Park, CA
- Motive: Romantic obsession and rejection; John had ended relationship to marry Sherry
- Pre-incident escalation: Lazarus showed up at victims' home in LAPD uniform while Sherry was alone; confronted Sherry at hospital and stated she'd be waiting if marriage failed; convinced John to sleep with her one final time shortly before wedding
- Attack mechanics: Lazarus gained entry (likely knocked, claimed she wanted to talk); physical struggle — Sherry fought back, tore off two fingernails, bite mark left on inner forearm; Lazarus smashed Sherry's face with ceramic vase; fired three shots execution-style at close range; staged scene as burglary (stacked electronics by door, rummaged drawers, stole BMW)
- Investigation failure: LAPD dismissed husband and father-in-law's identification of Lazarus as suspect; 1986 DNA technology insufficient; case classified as unsolved home invasion for 23 years
- Cold case break: 2005 DNA reanalysis confirmed attacker was female; 2009 detective Jim Nuttall identified staged burglary (blood on stacked equipment); Costco surveillance — officers retrieved discarded soda cup; DNA matched bite-mark saliva
- Arrest: June 5, 2009 — lured into interview room under pretext; arrested as she exited
- Trial outcome: 2012 — guilty, first-degree murder; sentenced 27 years to life; parole denied 2023 after she confessed
- Systemic note: LAPD investigated how Lazarus evaded scrutiny; questions raised about institutional protection of their own

### Case 02 — Brian Barrett / Thomas Montgomery
- Victim: Brian Barrett, 22, college student and factory worker, Buffalo, NY
- Perpetrator: Thomas Montgomery, 47, married father of two; same factory coworker as Barrett
- Date of murder: September 15, 2006
- Platform: Pogo.com online gaming chat rooms
- Deception structure: Montgomery created fake persona "Marine Sniper Tommy" — young, battle-hardened Marine; catfished "Jesse" (Tall Hot Blonde) for years; wife discovered gifts/lingerie sent by Jesse, exposed Thomas's real identity; Thomas then posed as "Tommy's father" to maintain contact; Barrett (young, real) entered picture and Jesse publicly favored him
- Third-party deception: "Jesse" was in fact Mary Sheeler — Jesse's actual mother — using daughter's photos and identity to engage men online; Mary admitted deception but faced no criminal charges; real Jesse unaware
- Motive: Humiliation and jealousy; Barrett was real replacement for fantasy; Thomas could not block or delete a coworker
- Attack mechanics: Montgomery dressed in camouflage and ski mask; armed with .30 caliber military-style rifle; waited in factory parking lot shadows; fired three shots at point-blank range through truck window; Barrett hit in neck and arm; killed instantly; body undiscovered for 2 days
- Investigation: Chat logs and co-worker accounts pointed to Montgomery quickly; arrested; admitted guilt; claimed addiction to fantasy and emotional manipulation
- Outcome: 2007 — pleaded guilty to first-degree manslaughter; maximum sentence 20 years; half-hearted apology to family; judge rebuked
- Note: Mary Sheeler faced no legal charges despite identity theft and role in precipitating murder; legal gap noted by creator

### Case 03 — Laura Black / Richard Farley
- Victim: Laura Black, 22 at start, female engineer; ESL Incorporated, Sunnyvale, CA
- Perpetrator: Richard Farley, mid-30s, computer technician; same employer
- Stalking timeline: 1984–1988 (4 years)
- Escalation sequence:
  - Gifts, notes, repeated requests for dates → firm refusals ignored
  - Appeared in hallways outside her office at unusual hours
  - Long rambling love letters; repeated phone calls
  - HR report filed → ESL directed Farley to counseling (not followed up); not fired; Laura told to avoid him
  - 1986: Farley fired from ESL; stalking continued post-termination
  - Sent package to Laura's lawyer containing doctored photos, false relationship evidence, and Laura's garage door opener (implicit threat of home access)
  - February 2, 1988: Laura files temporary restraining order; hearing set for February 17
  - Restraining order served = final trigger
- Legal gap: California law at time did not block firearm purchases based on restraining order; Farley legally purchased new shotgun after being served
- Attack: February 16, 1988 (day before hearing); Farley arrived at ESL in motor home; armed with two shotguns, rifle, several handguns, knives, 1,000+ rounds; wore bullet-resistant vest
  - Fired through security door glass to enter M5 building
  - Shot employees in sequence while moving toward Laura's office wing
  - Reached Laura's door; fired 12-gauge shotgun through wooden door; struck Laura in left shoulder; shrapnel punctured lung
  - Laura played dead — Farley did not fire again; stated he wanted her to remember what she "caused"
  - Continued rampage through building; 7 killed, 4 wounded
  - 3:15 PM: Farley called 911 himself; calm; described killings; threatened to kill hostages if police came within 300 yards
  - 5-hour standoff; negotiator Lt. Ruben Grielva maintained phone contact
  - 4:30 PM: Farley allowed rescue team to extract wounded; Laura evacuated to ambulance
  - 8:30 PM: Farley surrendered after demanding a turkey/ham/cheese sandwich (#26) and diet Pepsi from deli
- Laura's survival mechanics: Played dead; remained still despite wound; later moved by other survivors to safer location; 19 days in hospital; multiple surgeries
- Trial: Farley convicted on 7 counts of first-degree murder + additional charges; sentenced to death (1991); remains on California death row; no genuine remorse expressed
- Legal legacy: Case directly triggered California anti-stalking law (1990) — first in US; made credible threats and repeated harassment criminal offenses; became national model; all US states passed equivalent legislation within years
