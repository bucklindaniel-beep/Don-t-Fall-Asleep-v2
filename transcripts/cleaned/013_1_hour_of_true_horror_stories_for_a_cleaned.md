## Story 01 — Airbnb Intruder (Tattooed Man)

This happened in July of last year. My house was having some pretty big problems and I was left without any power for several days while it was getting repaired. I worked from home so this was a major issue, and I ultimately had to rent out an Airbnb to stay at for a few days until the power was back on.

I don't live in the biggest city so there weren't too many options for Airbnbs near me. The closest one at a reasonable price was about a 45-minute drive from my home, in an even smaller and more secluded area of the city — often referred to as not the best section to live in. I was only staying for a few days though, so the amount I was saving was worth it.

When I arrived I went right inside and basically straight to bed, exhausted from everything going on with my house.

In the morning I got up early and started setting up my laptop and workspace. Just before I had to start work though, there was a knock at the door. I debated whether or not to open it — thinking it might be the host or something, I went to check. On the porch was a man, maybe early 30s, covered in tattoos. He didn't look like an Airbnb host or even a salesperson. Unsure why he was there and not really wanting to know, I figured I'd leave it. I walked away from the door and went back to the desk.

I had some meetings and a lot of work to catch up on. I don't think I left the desk until 8:00 p.m., and even then I was considering going back to work after picking up dinner. Only a couple minutes after I got back and started eating though, another knock came at the door. I went to check and wasn't surprised to see it was that same guy again. What could he possibly want this late? Again, I walked away.

After about 30 minutes I decided to work for at least another hour. I was tired but the work had to get done. I put my headphones in and tried to focus. I got a lot done and once I was satisfied I finally closed my laptop for the night.

I hadn't realized how dark it had gotten. I went to the front to flip on the light, and a chill instantly rushed through me. Going down the front hallway were faint but unmistakable shoe prints. Looking around, they were all over the place — even going right up behind the desk I had been working at.

Terrified, I slowly followed them down to the back door, which was cracked open.

I don't even know how to describe what I was feeling. It was just pure horror. And only moments after that, footsteps started coming down the stairs. They were slow and careful, but when they got to the bottom a man peered out into the hallway and looked directly at me. It was that same man — easily distinguishable with all the tattoos. He looked surprised to see me walking around. But that surprise quickly turned into rage. His face had no emotion other than irritation and anger.

I was going to try to say something but no words came out. As my heart started pounding in my chest, I turned right back around and sprinted out the back door. I ran through the backyard and into some trees far behind the houses. I sat behind a tree and pulled my phone out, starting to dial 911 — but quickly closed it when I heard footsteps running toward me. I didn't expect them to follow me, but luckily they passed by without seeing me and continued to run out into the dark.

Once I got in contact with the police I went back to the Airbnb and waited for them. I gave a detailed description of the man and they actually found him the next day. It was confirmed to be him by the tattoos I had noticed. He'd actually broken into several homes in the days leading up to this, doing horrible things to those who lived in them. I don't know what he was planning to do to me or why he seemed to have left me alone at first, but I can rest easy knowing he won't be able to do anything anymore.

## Story 02 — Abandoned Gas Station, Pennsylvania

I was driving across the country for a kind of hectic trip — I had to get some things done for work at an office way up in Maine, and decided that while I was already on the road, I'd visit some friends and family on the East Coast.

During my fourth day of driving I was going through Pennsylvania. There were a lot of cool towns, but most of the drive was through nothing. To get where I was going it was taking me on a lot of side routes — long, empty, one-lane highways.

As it was getting late, rain started coming down and my fuel was running low. I hadn't passed any gas stations in a while. I continued through the rain for another 30 minutes and, barely through the fog, I saw a sign for a gas station. A couple moments later it showed up ahead of me — off the side of the road, nestled in the trees, really small. Only two pumps and a tiny concession building behind it.

Having no other choice, I pulled in and parked. When I tried to insert my card the pump didn't work. I wiped the dusty screen but it wasn't even on. The other pump was also off. Frustrated, I noticed a small light inside the concession building. Hoping it was a worker who could help, I ran up to the door. Moisture was covering the entire glass so I couldn't see inside.

I grabbed the handle and it screeched open. When I stepped inside I immediately knew something was wrong. The store shelves were empty and some were knocked over — the place looked like it had been destroyed. The light was coming from somewhere in the back, clearly some sort of lamp. I was only in there for a few seconds before I quickly stepped back outside.

As I backed out into the rain I turned toward my car — but a person was standing next to it. They were completely drenched and were just looking around my car and into the windows.

"Hey, what are you doing?" I yelled as I quickly made my way over to them. They shot a look at me, then moved their eyes behind me. I turned back to look at the building. The front door had opened, and a man came out into the rain — long black hair and a sickly look. He spent no time assessing the situation and instantly started running at me.

The other person by my car just backed away as I got inside, but the man running didn't slow down. He came right up to my door and tried opening it. As I pulled away a loud metallic scrape vibrated through the car.

I sped out of there and back onto the road, making it another half hour before my car ran out of gas. Luckily I had cell service and was able to get help. While I waited though, I looked at the outside of my car and saw a huge line going across the door — like the man had run a knife along it when I drove away.

Police informed me later that night that the gas station I was at had not been in service for at least a decade. Other than that nothing else came of it. What would have happened if I hadn't gotten out in time is left to nothing but theories.

## Story 03 — Abandoned Houses in the Woods (Chris)

This was about 10 months ago. I was spending a few days with my best friend from college — I'll call him Chris for privacy — in his hometown. It was a really small town, but Chris was showing me around and we went to the bars and just hung out and had a good time.

On my last night there, he mentioned a place he'd been wanting to explore for a while but hadn't had anyone to go with. It was some abandoned house or town he knew of a few miles out in the woods. We used to go exploring and backpacking all the time in college so it sounded like a pretty cool idea. Only 30 minutes after hearing about it we were already trekking through a path in the woods.

We still had some sunlight left but brought headlamps just in case. About 20 minutes of walking, the trail ended and we had to follow a very faint footpath through the trees. It went on for another hour before we made it. We could see a house in the distance and as we got closer we could see several more behind it. It wasn't what either of us expected though — the houses didn't really look that old. They looked weathered and heavily damaged but had modern siding, like any other house. There was even a tiny gravel road leading from each of the homes. It looked like someone had been trying to build a community and something went wrong, causing them to abandon it. Other people had been there before us, evident by the graffiti on the sides of the houses.

Chris and I went up to the first house and looked through the open doorway. There were weeds growing through the floorboards but it was otherwise empty. Before we stepped in though, a loud slamming sound echoed through the woods. Both of us jumped and stared down the gravel road we thought it came from. After that it was quiet. We had no idea what it was, but seeing nothing, we went inside.

There wasn't too much to look at, but what made it interesting was trying to figure out why anyone would abandon it. The house looked like it would have been nice before it was left behind. After walking around the first floor we went upstairs. Oddly it got way darker upstairs — like no windows were letting in any light. The hallway was short with only two doors and what looked like an attic in the ceiling.

We went up to the first door and opened it, seeing another empty room — but we were quickly interrupted by hearing something downstairs. There were shuffling footsteps coming through the grass and up to the house. We stayed quiet, sure that whoever it was had probably seen us outside.

They walked up to the front doorway then stopped just before entering.

"Hello?" We didn't respond.

"I just want to talk. No harm at all." He sounded older, almost like he was missing teeth, stumbling a bit over his words.

After that there was a whole minute of silence. He didn't say anything else, but we didn't hear him leave either. We waited another few minutes before Chris quietly went down the staircase and looked over at the front door. He turned and looked back at me with a pale face.

"There's nobody there."

I quickly went to see for myself. He was nowhere in the doorway. We carefully made our way to the doorway and looked out. There was one line of footprints in the grass coming from where the other houses were — but no footprints leaving.

Both of us figured it out at the same time and bolted for the trees, knowing the guy must have been hiding inside the house somehow. We got out of the woods as fast as we could.

Who that guy was and why he wanted to talk to us, we don't know. I'm sure if he'd found us though, we probably wouldn't be here.

## Story 04 — Cascades Backpacker / Missing Hiker

I was backpacking through a route I'd found in the Cascades National Park. It was an out-and-back trail that typically takes three days one way and three days back. I didn't know too much about it or how popular it was, but on long backpacking routes you usually don't run into many people anyway.

On my third day out I passed by one of the campsites and saw a man sitting by his tent. I took a break and we talked for a bit. He mentioned he was staying there for the night and heading to the peak of the trail the following day. I told him that's where I was heading today and that I'd probably see him when he got there.

I left shortly after and got to what would be my campsite for the next two nights. The view was something I'd never experienced before. For that whole day nobody passed through, and when night came I was still the only one camping there. I thought back to the guy I'd met — he said he'd be reaching the trail peak that day. He never showed up.

In the morning I packed up and started back down the way I came. I got a late start and before I knew it there was almost no daylight left. I put on my headlamp and continued on the trail, walking for another hour past sundown before I made it to the camp.

The tent that the man had was still there, set up like he never left. Knowing his plans to continue hiking and knowing that staying at one camp for several days while backpacking is unusual, I called out asking if he was okay.

No response.

I walked up closer and said I just wanted to check in. The flap on his tent was partially open and nobody was inside. I felt a lump in my throat. I stood and looked all around, not seeing a single sign of him anywhere — until I saw a glimmer in the distance. A faint light, sitting still, far out in the woods.

I began walking up to it. About 40 yards from the tents I found the source — a headlamp laying on the ground, so dim it must have been nearly out of batteries.

And it was just then that I noticed someone watching me.

They were standing not too far away — maybe 10 yards — clearly trying not to be seen.

"Hello? Do you need help?" They stood still and quiet.

"I'm sorry — I'll leave," I said shakily. Then I hurried back to the tent.

Luckily I hadn't unpacked anything yet, so I put my backpack on and started walking out of the campsite. As I did, the headlamp in the woods turned off.

I got away and walked as far as I could — another three miles to the next camp. I reported it as soon as I could. Unfortunately it was just what I thought it was. The man that I had met was missing. There were almost no traces for them to follow, and out in the backcountry it's nearly impossible to search for anyone.

Who that person I saw out there was is likely the biggest unknown that could give us answers as to what happened — though it seems like most of us are aware of the most probable scenario.

## Story 05 — Airbnb Stalker (Standing in the Road)

I had booked an Airbnb several weeks in advance in a town I'd never been to. A client I was meeting for work lived in the area so I was only staying there for a day and then driving home afterward.

The drive was three hours. When I pulled into the driveway and got out, the neighborhood was very quiet. Most of the houses' lights were off and trees covered the lawns, blocking out most of the view. The only thing I really saw was a person walking down the road — far down, nothing really looking off about it, but for some reason it just gave me a strange feeling.

I went inside and got situated. After making my rounds and checking the place out I opened the front door to get my other bag from my car. As I stepped onto the porch though, I noticed someone standing in the road. It was the person I'd seen walking a few minutes ago — but they weren't walking anymore. They were just standing in the road, facing the Airbnb.

As I started walking toward my car they noticed me and started walking away. I kept my eye on them but they seemed to just keep walking down the street. I went back inside, made sure everything was locked up, then opened the garage and pulled my car in just to be safe.

Throughout the next couple of hours I did a few checks outside. I didn't see him anymore. Having to get up early the next morning, I went to bed.

I woke up a few times during the night but my alarm went off at 4:00 a.m. The sun was still down. Groggy, I sat up in my bed — and right away saw a light coming under the bedroom door. I didn't remember leaving any lights on. I thought I must have just overlooked a kitchen light or something.

I got out of bed and walked up to the door. But just before I opened it I heard something on the other side — something moving around at the end of the hallway.

I opened the door. It was the living room light that was on, which I immediately knew I definitely would have turned off.

A guy was in the corner searching through one of my bags. He had a black hoodie and ripped-up jeans — exactly what the person I'd seen walking around was wearing. I only looked at him for a second before he turned around and faced me. He didn't look startled at all. It was almost like he was expecting me.

He dropped the bag on the ground and yelled at me to stay where I was. He began making his way over and I only had a split second to decide what to do. I turned and ran back to the room, slamming the door shut just before he caught up to me. He rammed into it and tried to kick the door down, then started messing with the lock on the other side.

I thought quickly and opened a window, jumping out and running away. By the time I was able to get police there the man wasn't in the house anymore. My bags were gone too. And to make things more terrifying — the bedroom door was opened. He had picked the lock and gotten in. So if I had just stayed and hidden in the bathroom or something, who knows what would have happened.

Police seemed to think there was more intent behind this man's actions aside from just robbing me. I pray he doesn't find me again.

## Story 06 — Wyoming Airbnb / Man in the Closet

Earlier this spring I was camping in the woods four hours north of where I live — a small lake I'd go to a lot. It was never crowded or even really known about, so it made for a nice, peaceful getaway.

When I got there and set everything up on the first day, nobody else was there. Not too long after I arrived I saw a man come out of the woods on the other side and set up a tent. He looked like an average camper and was keeping to himself.

Before sundown I saw him making his way around the lake toward my campsite. He had a friendly smile and introduced himself as William. He said he saw me across the lake and just wanted to say hi since we were both out in the middle of nowhere at a lake that nobody usually comes to.

We talked for a while and he was really cool. William was 26 — around the same age as me — and we both liked this spot because of how secluded it was. After 30 minutes he went back to the other side as the sun came down.

The next day we ended up hanging out for most of the day. He shared a six-pack of beers with me and we sat on the beach and had some good casual conversations. When we went back to our tents at the end of the day I was sitting by my small campfire — really happy I'd met William.

As it got later I started getting ready to put out the fire and go to bed, when I saw William walking around the lake. His campfire had gone out a while ago so I thought he was already sleeping.

He walked up to my campfire and right away something was different about him.

"Hey — everything good, man?" He smiled and was suddenly back to how he was before, but he said he came over to ask if I wanted to see a cool spot he found with a great view.

I said it was kind of late to be going out, but he assured me the view was great because it was dark out. The way he was hyping it up made me feel like I couldn't not see it.

I got up and followed him to the other side of the lake, then trailed behind him as he led us through the woods. There was no path or anything, so I pretty quickly knew that whatever spot this was, William had to have been there many times before to know the exact way without getting lost — especially in the dark.

We walked for maybe 15 minutes before William started slowing down. He pointed ahead and smiled like this was the view. But when I walked in front of him and looked out, there was a small structure — an old RV or camper that looked like it had been abandoned and succumbed to the elements for years. Moss grew all over it and holes of rust covered the siding.

I turned back to look at William. His face was now cold and empty. He looked like a different person.

"William — what is this?" He didn't answer.

Feeling a rush of fear I started frantically looking around, and that's when I heard the rusty metal door swing open behind me. I only saw for a quick second as someone else came running out before I started sprinting away.

I could hear both William and the other person following close behind, but I gave every bit of my strength to keep going until I lost them.

By then I'd run so far into the woods that I had no idea where I was. I just kept walking and keeping my eyes on the trees until the sun came up. By the time I found my way back to the lake I saw from the tree line that all of my camping gear was gone — like I was never even there.

I stayed hidden and got back on the trail, making it back to my car. I made a report with the local police but they never mentioned finding anything.

What was planned for me that night is horrifying to think about. But at least I got out before having to witness it firsthand.

## Story 07 — DoorDash Office Building (Three Intruders)

I was on a road trip from Portland to Chicago — nearly a three-day drive. I had some places booked to sleep at along the route. I went with Airbnbs so I could enjoy the road trip a bit more rather than staying in a crowded hotel.

My first Airbnb was in Wyoming. The route through the state doesn't pass through any big cities, so it was in a really small town — probably no bigger than two or three miles long.

As I passed the border into Wyoming it started snowing heavily, only dragging on the long day of driving. By the time I got into town the roads were covered in snow and the sun had already set.

I was hoping there would be a McDonald's or something for a quick dinner, but it was very clear there wasn't going to be. So I drove through the town and turned down the road to my Airbnb — a long driveway leading up to a small home in the middle of a field with tall snow-covered pine trees lined up in the front yard.

I parked, got inside, closing the door behind me and turning on the lights. It was small — probably 500 square feet. Just a living room and bedroom.

I took my shoes off and dropped my bag next to the bed, then sat on the couch. When I did, a thump came from the bedroom. I looked back at the doorway. It was pretty loud. I got up and looked, checking the small bathroom inside as well. It all looked good. The only other door in the house was the bedroom closet. I walked over and tugged on the handle — but it didn't budge. It seemed like it was locked from the other side.

At first I was pretty weirded out that a bedroom closet would be kept shut, but then I thought it could be a maintenance closet the owner was storing stuff in. That was the final conclusion I came to.

I went back to the living room and rested my eyes on the couch for a while. Then I got back up and went over to the window to see if the snow was still coming down. It was eerily dark outside with no lights from the town showing behind the tall pine trees.

I shut the blinds and turned back to the couch — but I saw a shadow move in the bedroom. It was slow and smooth, like the shadow of a door opening or closing. I started walking over, listening as I approached. I looked inside the room. Everything was in place. Nothing looked like it could have caused the shadow I saw. But I continued looking around until my eyes fell back on the closet.

It was still closed — but it gave me an unsettling feeling. I stepped up and grabbed the handle. It opened with ease. It wasn't locked anymore. But inside there wasn't anything — just a small, empty walk-in closet.

Before I could fully process everything, I heard someone behind me.

When I turned around, a large man was standing in the doorway to the bathroom. He looked beaten and tired, with scratches and cuts all over his face. As I stared at him he didn't even seem to care. He stepped into the bedroom and kneeled down, grabbing a dirty, worn duffel bag from under the bed. Then he stood back up and didn't even look at me as he slowly walked out of the room and out the front door.

I ran up and saw him trailing through the snow until he was beyond the lights from the house.

I tried to get police involved but after giving my description they really only talked to the Airbnb host. After leaving town I didn't get any more information.

I think it's most likely he was just using the place to sleep. But the way he looked makes me second-guess that. I just can't even think of what could have happened if I'd gone right to bed that night with the man hiding in the closet just inches away from me.

## Story 08 — DoorDash Office Building

This was a few months ago while driving for DoorDash. I only drive as a side gig, maybe 10 to 15 hours a week. This night I was only doing a couple of orders since it was already late, and I had picked up a small order around 1:00 a.m. It was to be delivered to a business building somewhere across town.

When I pulled into the parking lot though, it was empty — no cars at all. I parked near the front and got out with the food. The place was definitely an office building — smaller than most, only two stories and not very wide. All the lights were off in the windows aside from one light behind the front door.

I walked up to the door and checked the app, seeing it had specific drop-off instructions: enter the main hallway and leave the bag outside of room 232.

I tested the door and it was unlocked, so I stepped inside. I expected to hear people working or talking, but instead didn't hear anything. Not a single sound in the whole building — just white noise from the overhead lights buzzing above me.

As I passed the first few doors they were all numbers in the 100s, like 102 and 106. Room 232 would be on the second floor. In the middle of the hallway was a doorway leading to the staircase, held open with a door stopper — but the lights weren't on.

I stepped in and looked up. The whole stairwell was pitch black. Not even any light was coming from above.

I told myself this was too sketchy. I stepped out of the stairwell and back into the hallway — and just as I did, the door right across from me opened. The lights in that room were off, but a man emerged from behind the door and stepped into the hallway. He was wearing street clothes, and just didn't look like an office worker at all. He stood in front of me and pointed past me at the stairwell.

"232 is up there."

I nodded but didn't move. Neither did he. He just looked me in the eyes with a look like he was suppressing his anger.

After an intense few seconds I dropped the bag on the ground and started quickly walking to the exit. The man didn't follow me, but I heard his footsteps go into the stairwell and up the stairs as I left the building.

I didn't know what that was or if I was maybe overreacting, so I didn't call the police.

The following day though, the police called me. Apparently the office reported a break-in, and in the CCTV footage they saw my car and tracked it back to me. They also had footage of the break-in, so they called me in to question me.

It turned out there were actually three people that broke inside. One was downstairs — who I saw — but the other two were shown on cameras hiding at the top of the stairs, seemingly waiting for me to go up.

Several other delivery orders were made to that same office that night. All of the other drivers were smarter than I was, leaving the orders outside by the front door.

What would have taken place in that office had any of us gone up to the second floor is unknown — but hopefully police can find the three men before anyone falls victim to it.

## Story 09 — Washington State Cabin / Person on the Roof

My wife and I take yearly vacations to different places. This year we had a cabin booked in Washington state — far out, described online as quiet and peaceful, away from any other places or people. Most of our life is in crowded cities, so this sounded great.

When we flew in we picked up groceries and took a taxi out to the cabin — just over an hour from the town. Most of the drive was between huge mountain ranges and tree-covered landscape. Really beautiful.

At just about 7:00 p.m. we arrived. The cabin was completely alone in the middle of the forest. Even the driveway leading to it wasn't much more than some tire tracks in the dirt.

We unpacked the groceries and made a quick dinner since we were tired from the flight, then opened a bottle of wine to settle in for the night. We lit up the fireplace and chatted about how we were looking forward to the next few days.

It was around 11:00 p.m. when we both suddenly heard something outside. Someone was walking in the grass near the cabin — loud, like they were really close.

I got up and looked out the window. I looked around seeing nothing but large trees and pitch-black darkness around them. I told my wife I didn't see anyone, then went over to the front door and stepped onto the porch, turning on the outdoor lights. I didn't see anyone on the driveway either.

I went back inside and asked my wife if she heard the footsteps anymore. She said the sound didn't fade out — it just stopped suddenly from somewhere behind the cabin.

My brave act was starting to fall through. I told her to go up to the loft and stay quiet while I figured out what was going on.

I went around and checked all the windows again, seeing nothing. I was preparing myself to go back outside when a sudden thump shook the cabin.

I looked up. My wife was looking up at the ceiling.

Someone was on the roof of the cabin.

I stayed inside and called the police, going over lengthy directions on how to get to the cabin. Over the course of the hour we had to wait, we could occasionally hear footsteps walking across the top of the cabin. About 45 minutes in, we heard their boots hit the ground — followed by them calmly walking away. I looked out the window and saw their outline disappearing into the forest.

Police took our report and gave us a ride into town where we spent the rest of our vacation.

What that person was doing miles from civilization, in the middle of a forest, is terrifying. Seeing as they were likely waiting for us to either go to bed or go outside, we're just thankful we got out before something else could have happened.

## Story 10 — Gaming Room / Hidden Person in Empty Bedroom

I've owned and lived in the same house for eight years. It's a normal family-sized home — relevant when I had roommates, but when they moved out it was just me and I had nothing to do with the extra space. Technically a four-bedroom house, I only used two: my main bedroom and the gaming room. The other two were on the far end of the upstairs hallway — one with a few storage boxes, the other completely empty.

After getting out of work at 9, I picked up Wendy's on the way home and went right to the game room. I was off tomorrow, so I put my headphones on and loaded up CoD.

Before I had time to even load into a match, I thought I heard something like a knock at the front door. I took my headset off and listened — not hearing anything, I figured they would have knocked again if someone was there, and went back to my game.

Probably a whole two hours went by, and then I heard it again — something like a knock or a door closing. With my headphones on and my room door closed it was way too quiet to tell.

I got up and opened the door, looked out into the hallway, waited. After a minute I went downstairs and checked the front door — nobody outside, everything seemed normal. I made sure the door was locked before going back upstairs. To be more cautious I left my room door open this time. I put my headset back on and continued gaming.

I played through a whole round — maybe 20 minutes. As the game ended and I leaned back in my chair, a figure rushed past the open doorway just off to the side of my view.

I immediately threw my headphones off and jumped from my chair. I stared at the doorway. I didn't get the courage to move for several minutes.

When I looked into the hallway and eventually the rest of the house, nobody was there. But the front door was unlocked again.

When police came and investigated, it was clear that the person had been hiding — and very likely living — in the empty room. I hadn't been in there for weeks or maybe even months. So this whole time they could have been just on the other side of the wall from me.

It's really scary to think about, especially if I'd happened to confront him.

## Story 11 — Uber Driver / Farmhouse

When I was 19 I worked as an Uber driver. I hadn't figured out what I wanted to do with my life yet so I was just doing Uber to pay my bills.

This night I was driving from 7:00 p.m. to 3:00 a.m. Around 11:00 I picked up a lady from a bar and drove 35 minutes away to drop her off at home — pretty much at the edge of the city in a secluded area. After that, another pickup request came through from somewhere 10 minutes away. I was reluctant because the drop-off location wasn't back toward the city but further out. I still ended up accepting it.

I pulled onto the road. It was through a bunch of farmland and not much else — pretty empty of any other cars. Once I got to the last turn it was down a thin dirt road going between a field of tall grass or wheat. At the end I could see a farmhouse on top of a small hill.

I pulled up and parked in the open grass just in front of the house. The lights were off and nobody was outside. After a few seconds I lightly honked.

I had my windows rolled down. A sudden tap on the passenger door startled me.

I hadn't thought to look back at the road I came from. The man leaned his head through the open window and looked at me. There was a strange moment as he held a straight face and said nothing. I asked if he was the one who ordered the Uber. His eyes moved to the back seat — looking around — before he nodded and looked back at me.

"Can you help me carry something out to the car?"

Being at work, my reactive answer was sure. I assumed it was just some luggage or something, but I quickly regretted answering without thinking.

He began walking toward the house so I got out and followed loosely behind. Once he got up to the porch he opened the door and somewhat rushed inside without even looking back at me. As I matched his pace and got onto the porch, the wide-open door showed an empty hallway with still no lights turned on — and the man was nowhere to be seen. I couldn't even hear any footsteps of him walking around.

"Sir?" The house remained quiet.

Past the doorway it quickly fell to nearly pitch black. There was no way I was going in there. I turned and ran back to my car, then drove back through the field and down to the main road, cancelling the Uber.

My biggest regret from that night was not saying anything to anyone. I thought since nothing happened I had no good reason to notify the police. But every time I think back on it, there seems to be less of a chance that it wasn't something horrible.

I even looked up some information on the farmhouse about a month after it happened. All I could find was that it was last sold in the 1960s. So the man I saw almost definitely wasn't the owner.

What was waiting for me inside that house, had I followed him in, is something I'll never know for sure.
