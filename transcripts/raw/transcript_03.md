## Story 01 — Intruder Seen at Apartment Doorframe

My dream was always to get my own place. Ever since I was in high school, I really couldn't wait to move out because I hated my dad growing up and my mom wasn't much better. We would always butt heads and my dad would always be drunk on the weekends and honestly, it was just a mess.

As I got older and girls obviously became a factor, it became even more of a problem because there was no way I'd bring any girls back to that household.

So, because of all this, I took the first chance I could get and moved out when I was 19 in college.

I didn't go away to school. I went to a commuter college not far from home. My parents wouldn't pay for me to go away to school, so I was on Zillow, Facebook, and Craigslist every day for a while.

And when I finally found a rental that looked promising and affordable, I went to see it right away.

The layout is pretty important, so I'll describe it now.

As I walked in, I was welcomed with the living room/dining area, which was one open space. Then there was the kitchen adjacent to that, which was separated by a wall.

Then to get to the single bedroom in the apartment, you went down the hallway, which also had two closets. One which I used as a pantry and one as a storage closet. And then the door to the bathroom.

At the end of the hall was the bedroom.

The bedroom was generously sized. It fit a queen-sized bed easily. It had two more closets, and I fit a couple of dressers and a TV in there easily.

I was sold immediately, especially because it was budget friendly.

I moved in the next month, and life was fine.

Fast forward a few months, maybe.

I went out with friends in town, and we ran into some girls we knew.

I ended up taking this girl home that I'd known for a while through school, but we'd never hung out in that way. I'll just call her Sammy.

We ended up drinking at my place and watching a movie, but that was really all we did.

She didn't want to take it further than that on our first time hanging out, and I respected that.

We ended up in the bedroom finishing the movie that we started in the living room, and I think we both just passed out from being drunk somewhere through the movie.

The next thing that happened was I woke up to footsteps outside in the hallway.

It was still the middle of the night.

I looked to my side and I was alone in the bed.

I jumped out of bed looking down the hallway and I saw Sammy in the living room grabbing her stuff off the couch, but she was doing so quietly.

She was trying to leave now to avoid the awkwardness in the morning. And I understood that.

So, I didn't say anything and went back to bed quietly.

I heard her eventually walk out the front door.

For good measure, I called out Sammy to no response, and that confirmed she was gone.

I got comfortable in bed relatively quickly and drifted back off to sleep.

It was probably another hour that passed when I woke to something loud.

I immediately shot up in my bed and my heart started pounding relatively quickly.

I called out Sammy again. There was no response.

I was honestly kind of scared to get up and check.

So, I picked up my phone and texted Sammy's number asking if she was still in my house.

I realized that was a little weird to do, but I was actually scared in that moment.

I looked at the door, which was still open because I was too lazy to get up and shut it after Sammy left.

I was definitely still a little drunk from the shots we were taking in the living room, but I felt like I saw something wrapped around the door frame.

My breathing only intensified.

I still can't believe I had the nerve to do this, but I swiped down on my iPhone to reveal the control panel and I pressed the flashlight button.

And with the phone still pointed at the door frame, I realized there were two hands wrapped around the door frame.

But as if responding immediately to being revealed, whoever's hands they were tightened and then pulled back out of sight.

And the scream I was holding in for those few seconds finally came out, mixed with screams of dread.

I also screamed, "Get out! Get out!" over and over.

By the time I finally got up, I looked down the hallway.

It looked empty.

I flipped on the light immediately.

After a few minutes of calming my breathing and just trying to listen for sounds, I built up the courage to check the hallway closets and bathroom, and they were clear.

I then moved to the main living area, and trust me, there weren't any places to hide in there.

I held my phone as if it were a weapon as I crept to the nearest light switch and turned it on.

I felt instantly better when the living space was lit up, but there was still the kitchen.

I crept to the opening that led to the kitchen and flicked that light on as well, finally lighting up the entire apartment.

There was no one there.

I checked everywhere.

When I realized it was clear, I ran to the front door to lock it.

The weird thing though was that I never heard the front door close, and yet there was not a single soul except for me in there.

I called Sammy, and she confirmed she had left earlier.

The next morning, I checked the door frame and saw fingerprints exactly where the hands had been.

## Story 02 — Intruder Calling From Inside House

My parents' house never really felt like a parents' house.

My dad traveled for work constantly, and my mom was always out with friends or staying over my aunt's house.

From the time I was 16 and up, I basically lived alone.

I knew every noise that house made.

This happened on a night where I knew for a fact I was going to be alone.

Around 11:00, I went upstairs to my room and started watching videos.

About an hour later, my phone rang.

It said no caller ID.

I ignored it at first, but it rang again.

So I answered.

At first there was silence, then faint TV audio.

Then I realized the audio matched my TV downstairs perfectly.

That meant whoever was on the phone was inside my house.

I yelled downstairs.

The call ended.

Then I heard a floor creak.

Then someone cleared their throat downstairs.

I locked myself in my room and called my uncle.

While on the phone, the no caller ID called again.

This time I could hear footsteps on the phone that matched footsteps outside my door.

Then my door knob jiggled.

Whoever was outside tried to open the door.

My uncle heard everything.

The person left before my uncle arrived.

Later, police traced the call to someone I knew from high school, but he claimed his phone was stolen.

## Story 03 — Intruder in Basement at Friend’s House

It was a weekend like many others.

We were hanging out at my friend's house.

After going out, I stayed over and slept in the basement.

I woke up in the middle of the night thirsty.

I looked toward the corner of the room and saw a dark figure.

At first I thought it was nothing.

Then I realized it was moving.

It was a person.

I pretended not to notice and calmly walked toward the stairs.

As I went upstairs, I looked back and saw the figure moving toward the stairs.

I ran up and slammed the door.

We woke up my friend's dad and called the police.

The basement door and back door were found open.

The person was gone.

I never slept in that basement again.
