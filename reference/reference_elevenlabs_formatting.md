# Reference — ElevenLabs Formatting

## Purpose

Defines sentence-level formatting rules for AI voice narration.

Use this file to make narration sound natural, paced, and performable when spoken aloud.

---

## Core Principle

Narration must be formatted for speech, not silent reading.

Punctuation controls:

- breath
- hesitation
- interruption
- emphasis
- rhythm

---

## Approved Formatting Signals

### Ellipsis
Use:
`...`

Function:
- hesitation
- trailing thought
- delayed realization
- dread beat

Use sparingly.

---

### Period
Use:
`.`

Function:
- full stop
- breath reset
- emphasis through isolation
- sentence separation

---

### Comma
Use:
`,`

Function:
- light pause
- conversational flow
- natural phrase grouping

---

### Pause Marker
Use:
`(pause)`

Function:
- extended silence
- dramatic beat
- rare, intentional moment

Do NOT overuse.

---

## Speech Rules

- always prefer contractions
- write numbers as words
- break complex thoughts into simple sentence units
- put emphasis in sentence position, not typography

---

## Kill List

Replace:

- em dash `—`
- semicolon `;`
- parentheses `()`
- nested sentence structures

With:

- ellipsis
- period
- comma
- natural spoken phrasing

---

## Examples

### Bad
I opened the door—slowly, carefully—and looked into the hallway.

### Better
I opened the door slowly. Carefully. Then I looked into the hallway.

### Bad
The lock lever was in the horizontal position.

### Better
The lock was turned sideways. Like it wasn't fully locked.

---

## Failure Conditions

Narration fails if:

- punctuation is optimized for reading rather than speech
- long sentences stack too many clauses
- emphasis relies on typographic tricks instead of structure
