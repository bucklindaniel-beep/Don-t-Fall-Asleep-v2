## Prose Editing Essentials: 3 High-Impact Fixes for Stronger Writing

---

### Clean Summary

This guide outlines three common prose issues that weaken writing and how to fix them. The focus is on improving immersion, clarity, and rhythm through small but powerful adjustments: removing filter words, varying sentence length, and diversifying sentence content. These changes reduce distance between reader and story, improve pacing, and create more dynamic, engaging prose.

---

### Key Concepts

* Filter words create distance between reader and character
* Sentence variety improves pacing and emotional impact
* Sentence content should go beyond actions (add depth)
* Rhythm controls tension and immersion
* Writing should feel lived-in, not observed
* Layering sensory details, thoughts, and context adds richness

---

### Expanded Notes

#### 1. Remove Filter Words and Pronoun Overuse

* Common pattern:

  * “He saw,” “She noticed,” “I realized”
* Problem:

  * Creates narrative distance
  * Makes prose feel repetitive and flat
* Fix:

  * Remove filter phrases and present events directly
  * Shift sentence subjects for variety

**Example Transformation**

* Before:

  * “He saw the door open and heard footsteps.”
* After:

  * “The door swung open. Footsteps echoed in the hallway.”

**Result**

* More immediate, immersive experience
* Reader feels inside the scene, not observing it

---

#### 2. Vary Sentence Length to Control Rhythm

* Sentence length affects pacing:

  * Short sentences → urgency, tension, impact
  * Long sentences → atmosphere, introspection, emotional depth
* Problem:

  * Uniform sentence length = flat, monotonous pacing
* Fix:

  * Mix short, medium, and long sentences intentionally
  * Match rhythm to scene purpose

**Emotional Scene Insight**

* Use longer, flowing sentences to mirror spiraling thoughts

**Action Scene Insight**

* Use short, punchy sentences for speed and urgency
* Insert fragmented thoughts for intensity

**Result**

* Scenes feel dynamic instead of repetitive
* Emotional and action beats become distinct

---

#### 3. Vary Sentence Content (Layer Your Writing)

* Problem:

  * Writing that only describes actions feels like stage directions
* Fix:

  * Combine multiple elements within scenes:

    * Actions (movement)
    * Sensory details (sight, sound, touch, etc.)
    * Character thoughts
    * Questions or internal dialogue
    * Context/backstory

**Layering Approach**

* Action = momentum
* Thought + sensation = depth

**Example Shift**

* Before:

  * Pure action sequence with no emotion or context
* After:

  * Adds:

    * Environmental detail
    * Character reasoning
    * Emotional state
    * Subtle world-building

**Result**

* Writing becomes immersive and textured
* Reader understands both what is happening and why it matters

---

### Actionable Insights

* Highlight and remove at least 50% of filter words in a draft
* Rewrite sentences to eliminate “I saw / he felt / she noticed”
* Intentionally vary sentence length within each paragraph
* Match sentence rhythm to scene purpose (fast vs. reflective)
* Add at least one non-action element per sentence:

  * Thought, sensation, or context
* Avoid writing sequences that only describe physical movement
* Think in layers: action + perception + meaning
