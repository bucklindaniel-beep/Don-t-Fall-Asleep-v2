# Wordbank — Temporal / Pacing

## Purpose
Controls perceived timing, delay, and anticipation.

---

## Entries

### Early (Presence / Pattern)
- not immediately [stage: early, intensity: low]
- after a pause [stage: early, intensity: low]
- longer than expected [stage: early, intensity: low]
- not at first [stage: early, intensity: low]

### Mid (Escalation)
- just before it stopped [stage: mid, intensity: med]
- right as he noticed [stage: mid, intensity: med]
- before he could react [stage: mid, intensity: med]
- delayed by a fraction [stage: mid, intensity: med]

### Late (Proximity / Intrusion)
- too quickly to process [stage: late, intensity: high]
- immediately after [stage: late, intensity: high]
- without warning [stage: late, intensity: high]
- all at once [stage: late, intensity: high]

---

## Pairing Rules
- combine with spatial shifts for strongest effect
- use sparingly in early stages

---

## Anti-Repetition
- do not reuse the same phrase within 2 scenes
