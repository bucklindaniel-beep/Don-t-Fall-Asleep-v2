# Wordbank — Environment

## Purpose

Provides controlled environmental language for horror visuals.

Use this bank for:
- room and location condition
- environmental progression
- spatial deterioration
- continuity of layout and object logic

All environmental descriptors MUST remain:
- physically plausible
- reusable across shots
- escalation-compatible
- tied to continuity

---

## Stable Environment

- orderly room with balanced spatial arrangement
- clean surfaces with minimal interruption
- furniture aligned with deliberate placement
- open, readable floor plan
- clear sightlines across the room
- undisturbed surfaces and intact layout
- familiar domestic arrangement with stable geometry
- controlled, uncluttered visual space

---

## Slight Disturbance

- furniture slightly misaligned from its original position
- one object subtly out of place
- minor inconsistency in object spacing
- barely noticeable shift in room symmetry
- chair rotated a few degrees from prior position
- drawer or cabinet not fully closed
- small visual irregularity interrupting the room’s order
- subtle signs that the space has changed without obvious cause

---

## Active Disturbance

- multiple objects no longer matching established placement
- room symmetry beginning to break down
- signs of recent disturbance without visible source
- surfaces showing fresh displacement or disruption
- environment no longer fully matching prior shot state
- objects appearing handled, nudged, or repositioned
- scattered inconsistencies accumulating across the space
- increased visual disorder without full chaos

---

## Environmental Degradation

- gradual clutter accumulation disrupting clean surfaces
- surfaces showing marks, wear, or recent disturbance
- floor space becoming less open and more constrained
- visual density increasing across the frame
- room arrangement losing its previous order
- object relationships becoming less coherent
- once-stable layout now visibly compromised
- environmental calm replaced by visible disarray

---

## Oppressive Environment

- walls and corners feeling visually heavier and closer
- negative space shrinking as the frame fills with tension
- room appearing tighter than before
- environment pressing inward around the subject
- spatial depth becoming harder to read
- familiar room now feeling constrained and hostile
- layout remaining technically unchanged but visually oppressive
- environment dominating the subject rather than containing them

---

## Interior Compromise

- door left partially ajar without explanation
- object displaced near a threshold or passageway
- room state suggesting unseen movement through the space
- evidence of entry without direct confirmation
- a previously closed boundary now visually altered
- interior order broken by subtle signs of access
- part of the environment behaving as if recently occupied
- a secure space no longer appearing fully secure

---

## Environmental Damage / Late-Stage Shift

- disturbed furniture breaking established pathways
- objects displaced far beyond prior subtle misalignment
- surfaces visibly disrupted by recent struggle or force
- room condition now markedly altered from baseline
- environment carrying visible signs of escalation
- formerly stable elements now compromised or broken
- clutter and obstruction interfering with spatial clarity
- space transformed from familiar to unstable

---

## Resolution / Residual Change

- environment mostly restored but no longer pristine
- layout unchanged yet subtly less reassuring
- one or two displaced objects left unresolved
- room appearing calmer but still altered
- traces of disturbance remaining in an otherwise stable setting
- visible evidence that the space has changed permanently
- order partially restored without full reset
- the environment holding residual signs of prior escalation

---

## Spatial Relationship Cues

- foreground object partially blocking the room
- narrow gap between subject and doorway
- deeper portion of room visually unreadable
- background space remaining open but underlit
- subject positioned with limited escape space
- threshold area becoming more visually important
- negative space held behind or beside the subject
- room layout emphasizing distance or lack of distance

---

## Usage Rules

Environmental descriptors MUST:
- preserve layout continuity
- evolve gradually unless direct action justifies sudden change
- track object persistence across shots
- reinforce escalation through physical space

Environmental descriptors MUST NOT:
- reset room condition without cause
- contradict previous layout
- introduce random clutter or damage
- replace continuity with novelty
