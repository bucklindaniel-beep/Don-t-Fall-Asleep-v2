# Wordbank — Lighting

## Purpose

Provides controlled, cinematic lighting language for horror visuals.

Use this bank for:
- image prompt construction
- scene atmosphere
- escalation through light behavior
- continuity of lighting conditions across shots

All lighting descriptors MUST remain:
- grounded in realism
- reusable across scenes
- escalation-compatible
- visually precise

---

## Stable Lighting

- soft ambient room light
- balanced natural daylight
- even overhead illumination
- steady bedside lamp glow
- controlled warm interior lighting
- clear directional window light
- neutral room exposure
- stable practical light source

---

## Dim Lighting

- dim overhead bulb struggling to illuminate the room
- low amber lamp casting uneven pools of light
- weak hallway light fading into shadow
- soft practical light barely reaching the far wall
- partial illumination leaving corners underlit
- low interior light with limited spread
- subdued room light with soft falloff
- faint ambient light outlining only large shapes

---

## Harsh Lighting

- harsh single-source light producing elongated shadows
- exposed overhead light flattening the room unevenly
- cold fluorescent strip light with hard spill
- unforgiving directional light cutting across the frame
- bright practical source creating sharp contrast
- hard-edged light exposing surfaces while deepening shadows
- severe top-down lighting emphasizing facial tension
- sharp light break between lit and unlit areas

---

## Flicker / Instability

- fluorescent light flickering at irregular intervals
- intermittent light pulses disrupting visibility
- unstable lamp glow rising and falling subtly
- brief light fluctuation causing shifting shadow behavior
- unreliable overhead light stuttering in brightness
- weak practical light threatening to cut out
- momentary dimming followed by harsh recovery
- inconsistent light rhythm creating visual unease

---

## Shadow-Rich Lighting

- shadow gradients thickening toward corners and edges
- directional side lighting leaving half the room unreadable
- limited practical light surrounded by deep shadow
- darkness gathering beyond the reach of the light source
- uneven illumination leaving negative space dominant
- partial light revealing surfaces but not depth
- dim room light with shadows pooling beneath furniture
- narrow light spread creating isolated visibility

---

## Oppressive / Late-Stage Lighting

- light falloff creating pockets of near-darkness
- weak illumination overwhelmed by surrounding shadow
- visibility collapsing toward the edges of frame
- practical lighting no longer sufficient for full spatial clarity
- darkness overtaking previously readable space
- isolated light source surrounded by visual dead zones
- contrast intensifying as safe space diminishes
- lighting reduced to fragments of visibility

---

## Resolution / Aftermath Lighting

- partial return to stable light with lingering shadow weight
- calmer illumination with one area remaining underlit
- restored practical lighting that still feels uneven
- softened contrast with residual darkness in the frame
- mostly stable exposure with subtle visual unease
- room light returned but no longer fully reassuring
- more readable environment with one unresolved dark area
- visual balance restored only in part

---

## Lighting Progression Modifiers

Use for escalation progression:
- faintly
- slightly
- subtly
- noticeably
- significantly
- severely

Example:
- slightly dimmer than previous shot
- noticeably harsher contrast
- significantly reduced visibility

---

## Usage Rules

Lighting descriptors MUST:
- remain consistent across adjacent shots unless change is motivated
- intensify gradually across escalation stages
- support realism, not stylization
- reinforce camera framing and subject focus

Lighting descriptors MUST NOT:
- reset without cause
- jump from neutral to extreme instantly
- contradict the established light source
