# Horror Word Bank — Supernatural & The Unnatural

## Metadata
- Category: Supernatural & Unnatural
- Purpose: Vocabulary for defining non-human forces, entities, and rule-breaking phenomena
- Use Case: Supernatural horror, analog horror, cosmic horror, entity-driven narratives
- Tags: horror, supernatural, entity, unknown, rules, metaphysical, writing

---

## How to Use This File
This word bank is designed to:
- Define the *behavior* and *rules* of the unnatural
- Describe entities without over-explaining them
- Introduce forces that operate outside normal logic
- Maintain mystery while establishing consistency

Each entry includes:
- Definition (grounded meaning)
- Context (how it functions in horror)
- Example (usable sentence)
- Usage Tip (practical application)
- Why It Works (narrative or psychological reasoning)

---

## Core Principle
The supernatural should not just exist.

It should:
- Operate by rules the character doesn’t understand
- Violate expectations in specific, repeatable ways
- Feel consistent—but never fully explainable

---

## Supernatural Behavior Model (Extractable Framework)

Anomaly → Pattern → Rule → Violation → Escalation → Consequence

Use this to:
- Introduce something unnatural
- Let the character observe it
- Establish a pattern
- Then break or evolve that pattern

# Vocabulary Entries

## Anomaly
**Definition:** Something that deviates from what is standard or expected  

**Context:**  
Used as the first sign of the unnatural  

**Example:**  
The recording contained a brief anomaly—something that shouldn’t have been there  

**Usage Tip:**  
Introduce early, without explanation  

**Why It Works:**  
Signals disruption without defining the source  

---

## Manifestation
**Definition:** A physical or observable appearance of something abstract or unseen  

**Context:**  
Used when the supernatural takes form  

**Example:**  
The figure was a manifestation of something unseen  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use instead of naming what the entity is  

**Why It Works:**  
Suggests something larger exists beyond what is visible  

---

## Entity
**Definition:** Something that exists as a distinct and independent being  

**Context:**  
Used when the nature of the being is unknown or undefined  

**Example:**  
The entity did not move like anything human  

**Usage Tip:**  
Avoid describing it fully—keep identity vague  

**Why It Works:**  
Removes human reference points  

---

## Presence
**Definition:** The state or fact of existing in a place  

**Context:**  
Used when something is there but not seen  

**Example:**  
The presence in the room became impossible to ignore  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use when visibility is not required  

**Why It Works:**  
Creates tension through absence of form  

---

## Residue
**Definition:** A trace or remnant left behind  

**Context:**  
Used when something has passed through or interacted  

**Example:**  
A strange residue remained on the walls  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use to imply past activity  

**Why It Works:**  
Suggests continuity beyond the moment  

---

## Imprint
**Definition:** A mark or effect left on something  

**Context:**  
Used for repeated supernatural influence  

**Example:**  
Something had left an imprint on the space  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use for locations affected over time  

**Why It Works:**  
Indicates lasting impact  

---

## Distortion
**Definition:** A change that alters original form or meaning  

**Context:**  
Used when reality is altered by external force  

**Example:**  
The signal became distorted in a way that didn’t make sense  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use for media, sound, or physical space  

**Why It Works:**  
Suggests interference rather than randomness  

---

## Interference
**Definition:** Disruption of a signal or process  

**Context:**  
Used for technological or environmental anomalies  

**Example:**  
There was interference in the audio  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use in recordings, screens, or communication  

**Why It Works:**  
Bridges realism and supernatural behavior  

---

## Recurrence
**Definition:** Repeated occurrence  

**Context:**  
Used when phenomena repeat  

**Example:**  
The event showed a clear pattern of recurrence  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use to establish rules  

**Why It Works:**  
Creates predictability before breaking it  

---

## Pattern
**Definition:** A repeated or recognizable sequence  

**Context:**  
Used when behavior becomes observable  

**Example:**  
There was a pattern to when it appeared  

**Usage Tip:**  
Let characters notice this before explaining  

**Why It Works:**  
Encourages reader engagement  

---

## Rule
**Definition:** A governing principle or condition  

**Context:**  
Used implicitly—not stated directly  

**Example:**  
It only happened after midnight  

**Usage Tip:**  
Show rules through repetition, not explanation  

**Why It Works:**  
Gives structure without removing mystery  

---

## Threshold
**Definition:** A point of entry or boundary  

**Context:**  
Used for crossing into affected spaces  

**Example:**  
He hesitated at the threshold  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use for doors, mirrors, or transitions  

**Why It Works:**  
Defines where reality changes  

---

## Convergence
**Definition:** The coming together of multiple elements  

**Context:**  
Used when events align unnaturally  

**Example:**  
Everything pointed toward a convergence  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use when multiple anomalies connect  

**Why It Works:**  
Builds toward escalation  

---

## Invocation
**Definition:** The act of calling upon something  

**Context:**  
Used for summoning or triggering events  

**Example:**  
Something had been invoked  

**Usage Tip:**  
Keep cause unclear  

**Why It Works:**  
Suggests interaction without clarity  

---

## Binding
**Definition:** Restricting or tying something in place  

**Context:**  
Used for containment or limitation  

**Example:**  
The entity seemed bound to the house  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use to create partial safety rules  

**Why It Works:**  
Adds structure to threat behavior  

---

## Transference
**Definition:** Movement from one place or host to another  

**Context:**  
Used for spreading or shifting phenomena  

**Example:**  
The effect appeared to transfer between objects  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use for escalation across environments  

**Why It Works:**  
Prevents containment  

---

## Persistence
**Definition:** Continuing to exist despite attempts to remove  

**Context:**  
Used when phenomena cannot be stopped  

**Example:**  
The sound showed unnatural persistence  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use after failed attempts to stop something  

**Why It Works:**  
Removes resolution  

---

## Displacement
**Definition:** Being moved from original position  

**Context:**  
Used when objects or people are subtly relocated  

**Example:**  
There were signs of displacement in the room  

**Usage Tip:**  
Keep changes minimal at first  

**Why It Works:**  
Creates subtle, creeping inconsistency  

---

## Mimicry
**Definition:** Imitation of another form or behavior  

**Context:**  
Used when something copies human behavior  

**Example:**  
It spoke with near-perfect mimicry  

**Usage Tip:**  
Include slight errors  

**Why It Works:**  
Creates uncanny discomfort  

---

## Echoing
**Definition:** Repetition or imitation of sound or behavior  

**Context:**  
Used for delayed or altered repetition  

**Example:**  
The voice echoed back—slightly wrong  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use delays or distortions  

**Why It Works:**  
Breaks expected cause-effect timing  

---

## Breach
**Definition:** A break in a boundary  

**Context:**  
Used when normal reality is violated  

**Example:**  
There had been a breach  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use sparingly for impact  

**Why It Works:**  
Marks a clear shift into danger  

---

## Containment
**Definition:** Restriction of something within limits  

**Context:**  
Used when characters attempt control  

**Example:**  
Containment was no longer possible  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use before failure  

**Why It Works:**  
Creates temporary control illusion  

---

## Collapse
**Definition:** Sudden failure or breakdown  

**Context:**  
Used when systems or rules fail  

**Example:**  
Everything began to collapse at once  

**Usage Tip:**  
Use during climax  

**Why It Works:**  
Signals loss of structure

# Usage Patterns

## Pattern Before Explanation
Example:
It happened again. Same time. Same place  

Why:
Establishes rule without stating it  

---

## Rule Without Source
Example:
It only appeared when the lights were off  

Why:
Creates structure without explanation  

---

## Partial Understanding
Example:
He knew what triggered it—but not why  

Why:
Maintains mystery  

---

## Rule Violation
Example:
This time, it appeared early  

Why:
Breaks established safety  

---

## Spread / Escalation
Example:
It wasn’t just the house anymore  

Why:
Expands threat scope

# Key Principles

- Never fully explain the supernatural  
- Establish patterns before breaking them  
- Use rules without naming them directly  
- Let behavior define the threat  
- Keep entities partially unknowable

## Expansion Notes
- Add behavior-based terms, not creature types  
- Avoid cliché labels (ghost, demon, etc.)  
- Focus on interaction, not description  
- Maintain ambiguity in examples

## Integration Rule

Supernatural behavior MUST align with:

- escalation stage from `/maps/escalation_map.md`
- scene progression from `/maps/scene_flow_map.md`
- entity behavior from `/analysis/entities/`

You MUST:

- establish patterns before introducing rules
- imply rules before breaking them
- escalate through rule violation, not randomness

You MUST NOT:

- introduce supernatural behavior without pattern
- break rules that were never established
- explain the source of the phenomenon directly

The supernatural must feel structured, even if it is not understood.
