# Wordbank — Visual Horror Descriptors

## Purpose

Provides rich but controlled language for:

- visual consistency  
- environmental storytelling  
- escalation through detail  
- cinematic prompt construction  

All descriptors MUST remain:

- grounded in realism  
- reusable across scenes  
- compatible with structured prompt systems  

---

## Lighting

- low, amber-tinted lamp casting uneven pools of light  
- cold fluorescent strip light flickering intermittently  
- dim overhead bulb struggling to fully illuminate the room  
- soft directional light leaving portions of the space in shadow  
- shadow gradients thickening toward corners and edges  
- light falloff creating pockets of near-darkness  
- harsh single-source lighting producing elongated shadows  
- ambient light barely outlining shapes rather than revealing them  

---

## Shadow Behavior

- shadows pooling unnaturally along walls and floor edges  
- darkness gathering in areas where light should reach  
- shadow boundaries appearing slightly soft and inconsistent  
- silhouettes forming without a clear source  
- shadows stretching beyond expected proportions  
- negative space appearing deeper than surrounding darkness  

---

## Environment Stability (Baseline)

- orderly room with balanced spatial arrangement  
- clean surfaces with minimal visual interruption  
- objects placed with deliberate alignment  
- clear sightlines across the environment  
- stable, evenly distributed lighting  

---

## Subtle Environmental Disturbance

- furniture slightly misaligned from its original position  
- small objects shifted just enough to feel incorrect  
- faint signs of recent use despite no visible cause  
- minor inconsistencies in spatial arrangement  
- barely noticeable changes accumulating over time  

---

## Environmental Degradation

- gradual clutter accumulation disrupting clean surfaces  
- surfaces showing signs of wear, smudging, or disturbance  
- objects no longer aligned with previous positions  
- environmental symmetry breaking down  
- space feeling increasingly constrained or crowded  

---

## Environmental Oppression

- walls and corners feeling visually heavier and closer  
- negative space shrinking as objects fill the frame  
- environment appearing to close in around the subject  
- visual density increasing toward the center of focus  
- spatial depth becoming harder to read  

---

## Presence Indicators

- movement implied just outside the visible frame  
- objects displaced without visible interaction  
- subtle signs of occupancy without a visible subject  
- areas of the environment reacting without cause  
- visual inconsistencies suggesting unseen activity  

---

## Object Disturbance

- chair slightly rotated from its original angle  
- door left partially ajar without explanation  
- items appearing recently handled or repositioned  
- objects no longer matching their established placement  
- new objects introduced without visible origin  

---

## Atmosphere

- air appearing still and undisturbed  
- subtle haze softening distant edges  
- faint particulate visible in low light  
- atmosphere thickening as visibility decreases  
- sense of stillness becoming increasingly unnatural  

---

## Camera Feel (Cinematic Reinforcement)

- slow push-in toward subject increasing tension  
- off-center framing creating visual imbalance  
- foreground elements partially obscuring the view  
- negative space held intentionally within the frame  
- tighter framing reducing environmental awareness  

---

## Surface & Texture Realism

- worn fabric with visible creases and natural folds  
- matte surfaces absorbing light unevenly  
- reflective surfaces catching fragmented highlights  
- subtle imperfections in walls, flooring, and materials  
- natural texture variation across objects and surfaces  

---

## Escalation Modifiers

Use these to increase intensity progressively:

- slightly → subtly → noticeably → significantly → severely  

Example progression:
- slightly misaligned → noticeably displaced → significantly disturbed  

---

## Usage Rule

Descriptors MUST:

- support continuity (reuse across shots)  
- evolve gradually (no sudden jumps unless at climax)  
- reinforce escalation stage  
- remain grounded in physical reality  

---

## Compression Summary

Stable → Disturbed → Degraded → Oppressive → Unstable
