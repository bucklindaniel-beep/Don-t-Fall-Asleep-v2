# Wordbank — Presence

## Purpose

Provides controlled language for implied, partial, or direct signs of presence.

Use this bank for:
- subtle threat suggestion
- environmental evidence of unseen activity
- visual implication of an entity or intruder
- progression from uncertainty to confrontation

All presence descriptors MUST remain:
- grounded and observable
- escalation-compatible
- reusable across scenes
- subtle until escalation justifies clarity

---

## Implied Presence

- movement implied just outside the visible frame
- subtle indication of occupancy without a visible subject
- environmental inconsistency suggesting someone has been there
- visual cue implying unseen activity nearby
- suggestion of presence without direct confirmation
- part of the room feeling recently occupied
- evidence of activity with no visible actor
- the frame implying someone is close without showing them

---

## Peripheral Presence

- fleeting movement near the edge of frame
- partial figure implied beyond the focal plane
- shape-like interruption in peripheral space
- movement detected just beyond direct attention
- unclear form briefly occupying background space
- momentary suggestion of someone standing just outside view
- edge-of-frame activity too brief to confirm
- presence registering as motion before identity

---

## Object-Based Presence Indicators

- chair shifted without visible interaction
- door position changed between shots
- item appearing recently handled
- object displacement implying unseen access
- altered room state suggesting occupation
- threshold object no longer where it was
- new object introduced without visible origin
- established object now bearing signs of disturbance

---

## Shadow / Obstruction Indicators

- silhouette forming without a clear source
- shadow occupying space where no figure is visible
- darkened area suggesting depth beyond normal light behavior
- partial obstruction hinting at hidden occupancy
- negative space feeling occupied rather than empty
- shadow behavior implying presence rather than lighting alone
- blocked visual plane with no confirmed subject
- dark form suggested through occlusion rather than exposure

---

## Direct but Partial Presence

- figure partially visible through obstruction
- subject seen only in fragment or silhouette
- presence revealed through limited visual access
- body position readable but identity withheld
- a form occupying space without full exposure
- direct presence shown in incomplete visual terms
- one portion of a figure entering frame while most remains hidden
- presence confirmed, detail still restricted

---

## Watching / Observational Presence

- figure remaining still while visually fixed on the subject
- silent observation from a distance
- presence positioned as if already waiting
- subject being watched from a static position
- direct line of sight implied from darkness or depth
- stillness making the presence more disturbing than motion
- figure holding position instead of approaching
- observational threat prioritized over immediate contact

---

## Advancing Presence

- figure closing distance without sudden speed
- presence shifting from implied to active approach
- subject no longer alone in the frame’s usable space
- movement now directed toward the subject
- visual threat moving from edge to center
- approach turning uncertainty into direct pressure
- distance collapsing between threat and subject
- frame no longer allowing safe separation

---

## Confrontational Presence

- direct proximity with no remaining ambiguity
- figure fully occupying the threat position in frame
- immediate visual threat now undeniable
- presence no longer implied but dominant
- confrontation expressed through spatial closeness
- threat visually overriding the environment
- direct line between subject and threat now established
- frame structured around unavoidable presence

---

## Resolution / Lingering Presence

- presence no longer visible but still implied
- environment calmer while evidence of occupancy remains
- direct threat removed with residual signs left behind
- unresolved indication that the presence was real
- space appearing empty but no longer trustworthy
- subject alone again, but not restored to certainty
- visual absence carrying the weight of earlier visibility
- lingering implication replacing direct confrontation

---

## Escalation Modifiers

Use for progression:
- barely
- faintly
- subtly
- clearly
- directly
- unmistakably

Example:
- faintly implied presence
- clearly occupied negative space
- unmistakably direct threat

---

## Usage Rules

Presence descriptors MUST:
- move gradually from implication to confirmation
- align with escalation stage
- remain tied to observable visual evidence
- reinforce continuity and environment logic

Presence descriptors MUST NOT:
- reveal too much too early
- jump from absence to confrontation without progression
- rely on abstract emotional wording
- replace visual evidence with interpretation
