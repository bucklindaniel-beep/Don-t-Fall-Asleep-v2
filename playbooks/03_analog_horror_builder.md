## Purpose
This playbook defines how to construct analog horror content.

Use this file to:
- build fragmented, unsettling narratives
- simulate broadcasts, logs, or recordings
- structure pattern-based horror
- create ambiguity-driven tension

---

## Core Principle
Analog horror is not traditional storytelling.

It relies on:
- incomplete information
- pattern recognition
- gradual distortion
- lack of resolution

---

## Structural Overview

1. Normal Signal  
2. Subtle Anomaly  
3. Pattern Formation  
4. Reinforcement  
5. Distortion  
6. Breakdown  
7. Unresolved Ending  

---

## Construction Steps

### Step 1 — Establish Format
Choose format:
- broadcast
- emergency message
- recording
- log
- instructional message

**Goal:** create realism through structure

---

### Step 2 — Present Normal Signal
- neutral tone
- clear message
- no threat

**Goal:** establish baseline

---

### Step 3 — Introduce Anomaly
- small irregularity
- subtle inconsistency

**Goal:** create unease

---

### Step 4 — Reinforce Pattern
- repeat anomaly
- maintain consistency

**Goal:** build recognition

---

### Step 5 — Escalate Pattern
- increase frequency or intensity
- introduce variation

**Goal:** increase tension

---

### Step 6 — Distort Structure
- break format
- fragment information
- introduce contradictions

**Goal:** destabilize perception

---

### Step 7 — Breakdown
- loss of structure
- increased ambiguity
- incomplete information

**Goal:** peak unease

---

### Step 8 — End Without Resolution
- no clear explanation
- lingering ambiguity

**Goal:** leave lasting discomfort

---

## Format Types

### Broadcast
- official tone
- instructional

---

### Recording
- personal or found footage
- less structured

---

### Log / Report
- structured entries
- procedural tone

---

### Instructional Message
- commands or warnings
- impersonal delivery

## Pattern Integration

### Use Patterns To:
- establish rules

- build tension

---

### Break Patterns To:
- create shock
- escalate unpredictability

---

## Distortion Techniques

- repetition changes
- missing information
- contradictory instructions
- fragmented sentences

---

## Pacing Guidelines

### Early
- slow
- stable

---

### Mid
- repeated anomalies
- increasing frequency

---

### Late
- rapid distortion
- structural breakdown

---

## Language Guidance

### Effective Traits
- simple
- neutral
- repetitive

---

### Avoid
- emotional language
- narrative exposition
- clear explanations

- establish patterns early  
- reinforce consistently  
- distort gradually  
- end without resolution
