## Purpose
This playbook defines how to construct a complete horror story from start to finish.

Use this file to:
- build multi-scene narratives
- control pacing across a full story
- ensure consistent escalation
- generate high-retention horror scripts

---

## Core Principle
A horror story is a sequence of escalating scenes.

Each scene should:
- increase tension
- build on previous events
- move toward a climax

---

## Story Structure Overview

1. Hook  
2. Setup  
3. Early Escalation  
4. Mid Escalation  
5. Pre-Climax  
6. Climax  
7. Aftermath  

---

## Story Construction Steps

### Step 1 — Hook
- capture attention immediately
- imply something is wrong

**Goal:** engagement

---

### Step 2 — Setup
- establish character and setting
- create normal baseline

**Goal:** grounding

---

### Step 3 — Early Escalation
- introduce anomaly
- keep tension subtle

**Goal:** unease

---

### Step 4 — Mid Escalation
- reinforce anomaly
- increase frequency or intensity
- introduce pattern

**Goal:** sustained tension

---

### Step 5 — Pre-Climax
- remove safety
- increase urgency
- reduce distance between events

**Goal:** maximum pressure

---

### Step 6 — Climax
- confrontation or reveal
- peak tension

**Goal:** impact

---

### Step 7 — Aftermath
- resolution or ambiguity
- emotional fallout

**Goal:** closure or lingering tension

---

## Scene Integration

Each step consists of one or more scenes.

Use:

to construct individual scenes.

---

## Escalation Guidelines

### Across Story
- tension must always increase
- each phase stronger than previous

---

### Across Scenes
- avoid repeating same level of tension
- vary escalation types

---

## Technique Integration

### Early Story
- realism grounding
- delayed reveal

---

### Mid Story
- repetition pressure
- environmental escalation

---

### Late Story
- false safety
- pattern violation
- physical escalation

---

## Pacing Guidelines

### Beginning
- slower pacing
- more detail

---

### Middle
- increased frequency of events

---

### End
- rapid escalation
- short sentences

---

## Story Types

### True Story Style
- grounded
- first-person
- realistic pacing

---

### Cinematic Horror
- more structured
- stronger climax

---

### Analog Horror
- fragmented
- pattern-based
- minimal resolution

---

## Example — Minimal Story Flow

Hook → “I didn’t think anything of it at first.”

Setup → normal environment

Early → small anomaly

Mid → repeated anomaly

Pre-Climax → clear danger

Climax → reveal

Aftermath → unresolved

---

## Failure Modes

### 1. Weak hook
No engagement

---

### 2. No escalation
Flat story

---

### 3. Rushed climax
No impact

---

### 4. Over-explanation
Removes tension

---

## Reusable Rules
- start with a strong hook  
- escalate consistently  
- vary tension methods  
- delay full explanation  
- end with impact

## Compression Summary
