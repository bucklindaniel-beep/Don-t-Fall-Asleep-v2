# Playbook — Entity Application

## Metadata
- Type: Playbook
- Domain: Execution / Entity Integration
- Purpose:

  - translate entity behavior into narrative moments
  - control escalation through structured usage
- Dependencies:

  - /analysis/entities/
  - /analysis/entity_inspiration/
- Status: active

---

## Core Principle

Entities are not “introduced.”

They are **experienced through behavior over time**.

Application must:
- follow escalation
- maintain consistency
- reinforce perception

---

## Application Flow

1. Select Entity (via selection logic)  
2. Establish Presence  
3. Reinforce Pattern  
4. Escalate Behavior  
5. Transition or Converge  
6. Resolve or Withhold  

---

## Step 1 — Select Entity

Use:
/analysis/entities/entity_selection_logic.md

Determine:
- primary entity
- optional secondary entity (for escalation)

DO NOT:
- select randomly  
- over-stack entities early  

---

## Step 2 — Establish Presence

Goal:
Introduce the entity without confirming it.

### Methods

- indirect cues (sound, movement, placement)
- subtle inconsistencies
- environmental signals

### Rules

- no full explanation  
- no immediate clarity  
- minimal interaction  

---

## Step 3 — Reinforce Pattern

Goal:
Make the entity recognizable through repetition.

### Methods

- repeat behavior with slight variation  
- increase frequency  
- confirm pattern recognition  

### Rules

- repetition must evolve  
- avoid identical loops without change  

---

## Step 4 — Escalate Behavior

Goal:
Increase tension through the entity’s escalation driver.

Use entity-specific drivers:
- proximity  
- repetition  
- awareness  
- environment  

### Methods

- reduce distance  
- increase responsiveness  
- intensify interaction  

### Rules

- escalation must be gradual  
- do not skip stages  

---

## Step 5 — Transition or Converge

Goal:
Move from one entity state to another or combine entities.

### Valid Transitions

- observer → follower  
- unseen → mimic  
- mimic → intrusive  
- environmental → intrusive  

### Convergence Rules

- only combine if behavior is compatible  
- maintain clarity of primary threat  
- secondary entity should reinforce, not distract  

---

## Step 6 — Resolution or Withholding

Choose one:

### Resolution
- confrontation  
- forced proximity  
- outcome (escape, contact, failure)

### Withholding
- remove clarity  
- maintain ambiguity  
- leave tension unresolved  

---

## Behavior Class Application

---

### Observer

Use:
- distance  
- stillness  
- positioning  

Avoid:
- early movement  
- direct interaction  

---

### Mimic

Use:
- familiarity  
- subtle inaccuracies  
- delayed or incorrect responses  

Avoid:
- perfect imitation early  
- over-explanation  

---

### Follower

Use:
- movement-based tension  
- tracking behavior  
- proximity escalation  

Avoid:
- visual confirmation  
- inconsistent tracking  

---

### Environmental

Use:
- spatial distortion  
- layout inconsistency  
- navigation disruption  

Avoid:
- random changes  
- immediate extreme distortion  

---

### Intrusive

Use:
- boundary violation  
- proximity  
- confined space  

Avoid:
- early introduction  
- lack of buildup  

---

## Interaction Mode Application

| Mode | Usage |
|------|------|
| passive | early-stage tension |
| reactive | mid-stage escalation |
| adaptive | evolving threat |
| aggressive | late-stage / climax |

---

## Presence Channel Application

| Channel | Implementation |
|--------|---------------|
| visual | positioning, silhouette, distance |
| auditory | voices, movement, silence |
| spatial | navigation, proximity, direction |
| environmental | layout, objects, lighting |

---

## Escalation Patterns

---

### Pattern 1 — Distance Collapse

observer → closer observer → proximity → intrusion

---

### Pattern 2 — Perception Breakdown

unseen → repeated anomaly → mimic → confusion → intrusion

---

### Pattern 3 — Environmental Trap

normal space → inconsistency → loop/distortion → containment → intrusion

---

### Pattern 4 — Tracking Pressure

unseen → follower → proximity → unavoidable presence

---

## Scene-Level Application

Each scene should:

1. include ONE clear entity behavior  
2. reinforce or escalate that behavior  
3. avoid introducing unrelated elements  

---

## Story-Level Application

Across scenes:

- escalate entity behavior  
- shift interaction mode  
- reduce safety boundaries  
- increase proximity and certainty  

---

## Combination Rules

---

### Valid

- observer + unseen  
- unseen + follower  
- mimic + unseen  
- environmental + follower  
- mimic → intrusive  

---

### Invalid

- multiple intrusive entities  
- conflicting behavior classes  
- unrelated escalation patterns  

---

## Failure Conditions

Application is incorrect if:

- entity behavior is inconsistent  
- escalation is skipped  
- entity is explained instead of shown  
- multiple entities compete for focus  
- tension does not increase  

---

## Compression Summary

Select entity → establish presence → reinforce pattern → escalate behavior → converge or resolve
