## Purpose
This playbook provides a step-by-step method for building a complete horror scene.

Use this file to:
- construct scenes consistently
- apply escalation systems in practice
- generate high-retention horror content

---

## Core Principle
Every scene must:
- start grounded
- introduce tension
- escalate
- deliver an outcome

---

## Scene Construction Steps

### Step 1 — Establish Baseline
- define location
- define character state
- keep environment normal

**Goal:** create contrast

---

### Step 2 — Introduce Trigger
- add anomaly
- keep it subtle

**Goal:** start tension

---

### Step 3 — Reinforce Anomaly
- repeat or confirm event
- increase awareness

**Goal:** build engagement

---

### Step 4 — Escalate
- increase intensity or proximity
- combine systems:
  - environmental
  - psychological
  - emotional

**Goal:** raise tension

---

### Step 5 — Apply Technique
Choose one or more:
- delayed reveal
- repetition pressure
- false safety

**Goal:** structure tension behavior

---

### Step 6 — Approach Peak
- reduce time between events
- increase urgency

**Goal:** prepare for climax

---

### Step 7 — Deliver Peak
- confrontation or reveal
- highest tension point

**Goal:** impact

---

### Step 8 — Resolve or Transition
- outcome occurs:
  - escape
  - failure
  - ambiguity

**Goal:** close or continue

---

## Scene Checklist

Before finalizing a scene:

- does tension increase?
- is there a clear escalation?
- are multiple systems used?
- is pacing controlled?
- is the ending impactful?

### Transitional Scene

### False Safety

### Environmental Escalation

## Pacing Control

### Early
- slower pacing
- subtle detail

---

### Mid
- increased frequency

---

### Late

- short sentences

## Language Guidance

### Early
- descriptive but controlled

### Mid
- focused on changes

### Late

## Example — Minimal Scene

He entered the room.

Everything looked normal.

Then he noticed the window was open.

He was sure he had closed it.

A sound came from behind him.

---

## Example — Escalated Scene

At first, the house was quiet.

Then something moved upstairs.

He waited.

It happened again—closer.

By the time he reached the hallway, the lights were off.

Something was already there.

---

## Failure Modes

### 1. No escalation
Scene feels flat.

---

### 2. Over-escalation
Peak too early.

---

### 3. Weak trigger
No initial tension.

---

### 4. Weak ending
No impact.

---

## Reusable Rules
- always start grounded  
- introduce tension early  
- escalate consistently  
- combine multiple systems  
- end with impact
