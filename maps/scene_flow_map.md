# Map — Scene Flow Map

## Purpose

Defines how individual scenes are structured.

This map ensures:
- scenes are not static
- tension progresses within each scene
- structure is consistent across outputs
- scenes contribute to overall escalation

---

## Scene Structure

setup → disruption → reinforcement → escalation

---

## Stage Breakdown

### 1. Setup
- establish environment
- establish character state
- establish baseline normal
- anchor the scene in clarity

**Goal:**
Create a stable reference point before tension begins

---

### 2. Disruption
- introduce anomaly or irregularity
- break expectation
- shift attention
- trigger initial unease

**Goal:**
Interrupt normal and introduce uncertainty

---

### 3. Reinforcement
- repeat or clarify anomaly
- strengthen pattern recognition
- increase discomfort
- reduce ambiguity slightly (but not fully)

**Goal:**
Confirm that something is wrong without explaining it

---

### 4. Escalation
- intensify threat or implication
- reduce distance between subject and threat
- increase pressure (psychological, environmental, or physical)
- transition toward next scene

**Goal:**
Advance tension forward and raise stakes

---

## Scene Progression Rules

You MUST:

- follow all four stages in order
- ensure each stage is clearly distinguishable
- evolve tension within the scene
- connect escalation to the next scene

You MUST NOT:

- skip stages
- repeat structure without change
- create static or filler scenes
- resolve tension prematurely

---

## Scene-to-Scene Continuity

Each scene MUST:

- begin at or slightly above the previous scene’s escalation level
- introduce new tension (not repeat the same beat)
- reflect increasing entity influence
- reduce safety, clarity, or control over time

---

## Integration with Escalation Map

Scene flow operates at the **micro level**.

Escalation map operates at the **macro level**.

Relationship:

- each scene represents a stage within the larger escalation model
- scene escalation MUST align with global escalation progression

---

## Integration with Entities

Scene structure MUST reflect entity behavior.

You MUST:

- introduce entity presence during disruption
- reinforce entity pattern during reinforcement
- escalate entity influence during escalation

You MUST NOT:

- introduce entity behavior randomly
- separate scene structure from entity logic

---

## Integration with Wordbanks

Each stage should align with language layers:

- Setup → neutral or baseline language
- Disruption → subtle emotional/environmental shifts
- Reinforcement → psychological unease increases
- Escalation → physical, environmental, or perceptual pressure increases

---

## Retention Mechanics Alignment

Each scene must:

- create a curiosity gap (disruption)
- sustain tension (reinforcement)
- increase stakes (escalation)
- leave unresolved pressure moving into the next scene

---

## Failure Conditions

Scene is incorrect if:

- no clear disruption occurs
- reinforcement is missing
- escalation does not increase tension
- structure is flat or repetitive
- scene does not connect to larger progression

---

## Compression Summary

Scene = micro-escalation unit

setup → disruption → reinforcement → escalation  
→ feeds into global escalation system
