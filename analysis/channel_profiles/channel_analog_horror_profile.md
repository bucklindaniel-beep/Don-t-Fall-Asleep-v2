# Analysis — Channel Profile: Analog Horror

## Purpose
Defines the structure, tone, and behavior of analog horror storytelling.

Use this file to:
- guide analog horror content
- create ambiguity-driven narratives
- build unsettling, system-based horror

---

## Core Style Summary
- fragmented storytelling
- heavy ambiguity
- minimal explanation
- system-based horror (rules, signals, broadcasts)
- focus on unease over direct fear

---

## Narrative Structure

### Format
- non-linear
- often presented as found footage, recordings, or broadcasts

### Structure Pattern
1. normal or official message
2. subtle anomaly
3. repeated irregularity
4. escalation of distortion
5. partial breakdown
6. unresolved ending

---

## Opening Style

### Characteristics
- neutral or informational tone
- resembles official communication

### Example Pattern
“This message is intended for…”

---

## Escalation Style

- builds through:
  - repetition
  - distortion
  - pattern recognition
- avoids traditional pacing

---

## Ending Style

### Common Traits
- unresolved
- unclear meaning
- lingering discomfort

---

## Language Patterns

### Sentence Structure
- simple
- often repetitive
- sometimes fragmented

### Vocabulary
- neutral, technical, or official
- minimal emotional language

### Tone
- detached
- impersonal
- clinical

---

## Emotional Handling

- almost no explicit emotion
- fear emerges from implication

---

## Common Techniques

- repetition
- distortion
- corrupted messages
- delayed understanding
- pattern violation

---

## Psychological Elements

- confusion
- uncertainty
- lack of clarity
- gradual realization

---

## Environmental Usage

- minimal visual description
- relies on implied setting

---

## Physical Elements

- very limited
- often implied rather than shown

---

## What To Avoid

- over-explaining
- clear narratives
- emotional language
- traditional story arcs

---

## What Makes It Effective

- ambiguity
- unpredictability
- pattern disruption
- lack of resolution

---

## Application Guidelines

When generating:

- keep language simple  
- introduce anomalies slowly  
- repeat patterns  
- avoid explaining the threat  
- end without resolution
