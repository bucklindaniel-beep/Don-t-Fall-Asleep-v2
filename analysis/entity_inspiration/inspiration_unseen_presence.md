# Inspiration — Unseen Presence

## Core Pattern
An entity that is never directly seen, but whose existence is inferred through consistent, unexplainable effects.

---

## Definition of “Unseen”

The entity is not hidden—it is **unconfirmable**.

### Primary
- the protagonist cannot visually verify it  

### Secondary
- the viewer also lacks confirmation  

The key effect:
> something is clearly there, but cannot be proven

---

## Nature of the Presence

The entity expresses itself through **indirect evidence**, not appearance.

This may include:
- sound without source  
- movement without cause  
- changes in environment  
- interaction without visibility  

The goal is:
> certainty of presence without visual confirmation

---

## Common Traits

- footsteps with no visible origin  
- objects moving slightly or incorrectly  
- sounds that imply proximity  
- environmental reactions (doors, lights, shadows)  
- presence that feels directional but unseen  
- consistency without visibility  

---

## Fear Type

- dread  
- anxiety  
- anticipation  
- loss of control  

---

## Escalation Pattern

1. isolated anomaly  
2. repeated unexplained events  
3. pattern recognition  
4. increased proximity  
5. environmental manipulation  
6. implied presence within immediate space  

---

## Pattern Hooks

- something moving just out of sight  
- sound from an empty space  
- objects not where they should be  
- something reacting to the protagonist’s actions  
- presence that seems to follow  

---

## Perception Strategy

Effective use relies on:

- denying visual confirmation  
- reinforcing presence through multiple senses  
- increasing frequency of anomalies  
- narrowing perceived distance  
- allowing the audience to “fill in” the threat  

---

## Sensory Channels

Unseen entities rely heavily on:

### Sound
- footsteps  
- breathing  
- movement  

### Environment
- doors opening  
- lights changing  
- objects shifting  

### Spatial Awareness
- something being “behind” or “near”  
- directional tension  

---

## Escalation Behavior

- anomalies become more frequent  
- distance decreases  
- interaction becomes more direct  
- environment becomes reactive  

The tension comes from:
> knowing it is close, but not being able to see it

---

## Environmental Anchors

Strongest in:
- enclosed spaces  
- low visibility environments  
- quiet settings  
- familiar locations where change is noticeable  

---

## Usage Notes

Best used in:
- early-to-mid escalation  
- bridging into physical or psychological escalation  
- scenes where silence and space are emphasized  

---

## Avoid

- revealing the entity too early  
- inconsistent behavior  
- random, unpatterned events  
- overloading with too many effects at once  

---

## Compression Summary

Unseen Presence = confirmed existence + no visual proof + escalating proximity through indirect evidence
