# Inspiration — Imitation Entities

## Core Pattern
An entity imitates something familiar, but in a way that is subtly and unsettlingly wrong.

---

## Definition of “Familiar”

Familiarity should be anchored to:

### Primary
- the protagonist / narrator  
  (people they know, spaces they recognize, routines they trust)

### Secondary
- the viewer  
  (common human behaviors, social expectations, body language)

Best results occur when:
- the protagonist trusts it  
- the viewer notices something is off first  

---

## Nature of the Imitation

The imitation is not simply inaccurate—it is **unsettlingly incorrect**.

This may include:
- proportions that feel slightly off  
- timing that doesn’t match natural human response  
- expressions that don’t align with context  
- behavior that is technically correct but emotionally wrong  

The goal is:
> not obvious failure, but *perceptual discomfort*

---

## Common Traits

- delayed responses  
- incorrect emotional reactions  
- unnatural stillness or movement  
- gradual improvement over time  
- presence in places it shouldn’t be  
- awareness without understanding  

---

## Fear Type

- paranoia  
- identity instability  
- loss of trust in familiar people or environments  

---

## Escalation Pattern

1. subtle wrongness (barely noticeable)  
2. repeated exposure  
3. increased accuracy  
4. proximity increases  
5. replacement or identity confusion  
6. direct interaction  

---

## Pattern Hooks

- something responding too late  
- something watching without reacting  
- repeated encounters with slight variation  
- being recognized incorrectly  
- something appearing where it logically shouldn’t be  

---

## Perception Strategy

Effective use relies on:

- delaying confirmation  
- allowing doubt to exist  
- letting the viewer notice inconsistencies before the protagonist  
- maintaining ambiguity for as long as possible  

---

## Usage Notes

Best used in:
- confined or familiar environments  
- first-person or close perspective narration  
- slow-burn psychological horror  

---

## Avoid

- immediate obvious distortion  
- perfect imitation too early  
- over-explaining the entity  
- making the entity behave randomly  

---

## Compression Summary

Imitation Entities = familiar presence + subtly incorrect behavior + escalating proximity and accuracy
