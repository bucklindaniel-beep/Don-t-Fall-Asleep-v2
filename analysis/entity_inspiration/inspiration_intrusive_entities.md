# Inspiration — Intrusive Entities

## Core Pattern
An entity that does not remain at a distance—it crosses boundaries and enters the character’s space directly.

---

## Definition of “Intrusion”

Intrusion is defined by **violation of expected safety boundaries**.

### Primary
- physical space (room, home, immediate proximity)

### Secondary
- personal space (distance, body, perception)

The key effect:
> what was once safe is no longer protected

---

## Nature of the Behavior

The entity does not wait or observe—it **acts by entering**.

This may include:
- appearing inside protected spaces  
- closing distance rapidly or unexpectedly  
- interacting with objects near or on the character  
- initiating contact or near-contact  

The goal is:
> removal of distance as a source of safety

---

## Common Traits

- appearing inside rooms without entry  
- being present where it should not be able to access  
- sudden proximity shifts  
- interacting with the character’s immediate surroundings  
- breaching previously “safe” areas  

---

## Fear Type

- panic  
- loss of control  
- violation  
- immediate danger  

---

## Escalation Pattern

1. distant or implied presence  
2. boundary testing (doors, thresholds, proximity)  
3. entry into controlled space  
4. reduced distance between entity and character  
5. near-contact interactions  
6. direct contact or unavoidable confrontation  

---

## Pattern Hooks

- something inside the house when it shouldn’t be  
- something behind the character  
- something entering a locked or closed space  
- sudden appearance at close range  
- movement from outside → inside  

---

## Perception Strategy

Effective use relies on:

- establishing safety first  
- clearly defining boundaries  
- gradually violating those boundaries  
- accelerating proximity over time  
- limiting escape options  

---

## Boundary Types

### Structural Boundaries
- doors  
- walls  
- windows  

### Spatial Boundaries
- distance between character and threat  
- separation between rooms or areas  

### Psychological Boundaries
- expectation of safety  
- belief that something “can’t get in”  

---

## Escalation Behavior

- boundaries fail or are bypassed  
- proximity increases rapidly  
- interaction becomes unavoidable  
- control shifts from character to entity  

The tension comes from:
> realizing there is nowhere left to retreat

---

## Environmental Interaction

- doors opening or unlocking  
- movement through barriers  
- objects near the character being manipulated  
- space no longer providing protection  

---

## Usage Notes

Best used in:
- late-stage escalation  
- pre-climax or climax  
- transitions from tension to action  

---

## Avoid

- immediate intrusion without buildup  
- ignoring established boundaries  
- random proximity shifts without logic  
- overuse of direct contact (reduces impact)  

---

## Compression Summary

Intrusive Entities = boundary violation + rapid proximity + loss of safety and control
