# Inspiration — Observer Entities

## Core Pattern
An entity that watches without interacting, creating tension through presence rather than action.

---

## Definition of “Observation”

Observation should feel:

### Primary
- directed at the protagonist  
  (the character is being watched)

### Secondary
- implied to extend beyond the frame  
  (the entity may be watching more than just the current moment)

The key effect:
> awareness without confirmation of intent

---

## Nature of the Presence

The entity does not behave like a normal observer.

It is **too still, too consistent, or too aware**.

This may include:
- maintaining eye contact for too long  
- appearing in positions that allow observation but not interaction  
- never reacting when noticed  
- being present without logical entry or exit  

The goal is:
> not immediate threat, but *inescapable awareness*

---

## Common Traits

- unmoving or minimally moving presence  
- consistent positioning (doorways, hallways, distance)  
- appearing in peripheral vision  
- disappearing when approached  
- appearing again in new positions  
- watching without responding  

---

## Fear Type

- unease  
- dread  
- vulnerability  
- loss of privacy  

---

## Escalation Pattern

1. possible presence (uncertain)  
2. confirmed observation  
3. repeated sightings  
4. closer positioning  
5. sustained visibility  
6. transition from passive to implied intent  

---

## Pattern Hooks

- something standing in a doorway  
- something visible at a distance  
- being watched from outside  
- repeated sightings in different locations  
- something that remains even when acknowledged  

---

## Perception Strategy

Effective use relies on:

- ambiguity of intent  
- lack of movement  
- prolonged exposure  
- delayed escalation  
- contrast between stillness and expectation of action  

---

## Environmental Anchors

Observer entities are strongest when tied to:

- thresholds (doors, windows, hallways)  
- elevated positions (stairs, upper floors)  
- distance (across rooms, outside structures)  
- darkness or low visibility  

---

## Escalation Behavior

Unlike aggressive entities:

- escalation is driven by proximity, not action  
- stillness becomes more threatening over time  
- presence becomes harder to ignore  
- the lack of action becomes the tension  

---

## Usage Notes

Best used in:
- quiet environments  
- slow pacing scenes  
- early-to-mid escalation phases  
- before physical threat is introduced  

---

## Avoid

- immediate movement toward the character  
- early direct interaction  
- overuse without variation  
- turning the entity into a conventional attacker too quickly  

---

## Compression Summary

Observer Entities = persistent watching presence + minimal movement + escalating proximity and awareness
