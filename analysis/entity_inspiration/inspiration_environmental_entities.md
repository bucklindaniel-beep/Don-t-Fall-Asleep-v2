# Inspiration — Environmental Entities

## Core Pattern
The environment is not just a setting—it behaves with intention.

---

## Definition of “Environmental Entity”

The threat is not separate from the environment.

### Primary
- the environment itself is the medium of the entity  

### Secondary
- the environment behaves in ways that suggest awareness or control  

The key effect:
> the space is no longer passive—it is acting

---

## Nature of the Behavior

The environment does not simply change—it responds.

This may include:
- spatial inconsistency  
- objects behaving with intent  
- areas that do not behave logically  
- transitions that should not be possible  

The goal is:
> loss of trust in physical space

---

## Common Traits

- rooms that feel different upon re-entry  
- objects appearing or disappearing  
- layout changes (doors, hallways, distances)  
- space that feels larger or smaller than it should  
- repeated or looping areas  
- subtle environmental reactions to the character  

---

## Fear Type

- disorientation  
- loss of control  
- spatial uncertainty  
- entrapment  

---

## Escalation Pattern

1. subtle environmental inconsistency  
2. repeated irregularities  
3. noticeable spatial changes  
4. environment responding to presence  
5. loss of reliable navigation  
6. environment acting as an active threat  

---

## Pattern Hooks

- a door leading somewhere it shouldn’t  
- returning to a place that has changed  
- objects not remaining where they were  
- space feeling “off” without visible cause  
- movement through an area that should not exist  

---

## Perception Strategy

Effective use relies on:

- maintaining partial normalcy  
- introducing small inconsistencies first  
- reinforcing changes through repetition  
- delaying major distortions  
- allowing the character to question their own memory  

---

## Spatial Distortion Types

### Layout Distortion
- rooms rearranged  
- impossible connections  

### Scale Distortion
- spaces larger or smaller than expected  

### Looping Space
- repeating paths or areas  

### Directional Confusion
- movement not aligning with expectation  

---

## Environmental Responsiveness

At higher escalation:

- environment reacts to movement  
- environment reacts to attention  
- environment appears to anticipate behavior  

---

## Escalation Behavior

- inconsistencies become undeniable  
- space becomes unreliable  
- navigation becomes impossible  
- environment actively restricts or manipulates movement  

The tension comes from:
> being trapped in a system that no longer follows physical rules

---

## Environmental Anchors

Strongest in:
- indoor locations  
- familiar spaces (homes, workplaces)  
- confined areas  
- locations with clear layout expectations  

---

## Usage Notes

Best used in:
- mid-to-late escalation  
- transitions between scenes  
- scenes involving movement or exploration  

---

## Avoid

- immediate extreme distortion  
- constant large-scale changes without buildup  
- random, unstructured changes  
- removing all sense of baseline too early  

---

## Compression Summary

Environmental Entities = environment behaves with intent + space becomes unreliable + escalation through spatial distortion
