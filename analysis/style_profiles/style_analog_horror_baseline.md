## Purpose
This file defines the baseline style for analog horror storytelling.

Use this file to:
- create fragmented and ambiguous narratives
- structure non-linear horror content
- simulate broadcasts, recordings, or systems
- build unease through implication and distortion

---

## Core Style Summary
- fragmented and incomplete information
- minimal direct narration
- high ambiguity
- system-driven behavior
- focus on pattern recognition over explanation

---

## Tone Characteristics

### Primary Tone
- detached
- neutral
- impersonal

### Secondary Tone
- unsettling
- clinical
- procedural

---

## Narrative Voice

- often non-personal
- may simulate:
  - announcements
  - recordings
  - logs
  - instructions
- avoids emotional expression

---

## Sentence Structure

### General Pattern
- short, simple sentences
- repetitive phrasing
- minimal variation

### Fragmentation
- incomplete thoughts
- abrupt transitions
- missing context

---

## Vocabulary Guidelines

### Preferred
- neutral language
- technical or procedural wording
- simple phrasing

### Avoid
- emotional descriptors
- dramatic language
- complex metaphors
- narrative-heavy exposition

---

## Pacing Behavior

### Baseline
- irregular pacing
- uneven flow

### Escalation
- repetition increases
- anomalies occur more frequently

### Climax
- distortion intensifies
- structure breaks down

---

## Emotional Expression

### Approach
- emotion is not stated

### Effect
- unease is created through:
  - confusion
  - lack of clarity
  - incomplete information

---

## Description Style

### Effective Approach
- minimal description
- focus on anomalies
- highlight inconsistencies

### Avoid
- detailed environmental descriptions
- explicit explanations

---

## Structural Behavior

### Typical Pattern
1. normal or official message
2. subtle anomaly
3. repetition
4. escalation of irregularities
5. structural breakdown
6. unresolved ending

---

## Common Techniques

- repetition pressure
- environmental escalation (subtle)
- pattern establishment
- pattern violation
- delayed understanding

---

## Information Handling

### Key Principle
Information is incomplete.

### Behavior
- key details are missing
- context is limited
- audience must infer meaning

---

## Pattern-Based Storytelling

Analog horror relies heavily on:
- recurring elements
- recognizable sequences
- rule-like behavior

These patterns are:
- established
- reinforced
- then broken

---

## Distortion Behavior

Distortion may occur in:
- language
- structure
- sequence
- logic

Distortion increases over time.

---

## Realism Approach

- simulates real formats (broadcasts, tapes, logs)
- uses familiar systems
- then corrupts them

---

## Integration With Systems

### Supernatural Behavior
- rules are implied, not stated
- behavior appears consistent, then changes

### Psychological Escalation
- confusion
- uncertainty
- delayed realization

### Environmental Escalation
- subtle and indirect
- often implied rather than described

---

## Example — Minimal
This message is intended for all residents.

Do not leave your home after dark.

---

## Example — Escalated
This message is intended for all residents.

Do not leave your home after dark.

If you see someone outside, do not approach them.

They may not be real.

---

## Example — Distorted
This message is intended for all residents.

Do not leave your home after dark.

Do not leave your home.

Do not.

---

## Failure Modes

### 1. Over-explanation
Removes ambiguity.

### 2. Traditional storytelling structure
Breaks analog horror style.

### 3. Excessive clarity
Reduces unease.

### 4. No pattern establishment
Makes distortion meaningless.

---

## Reusable Writing Rules
- keep information incomplete
- use repetition strategically
- establish patterns before breaking them
- avoid emotional language
- let structure degrade over time

---

## Compression Summary
Analog horror baseline = fragmented structure + minimal information + pattern-based escalation.

---

## Notes
This style should override standard narration rules when applied.

It is most effective when:
- ambiguity is preserved
- patterns are clear but unexplained
- resolution is avoided
