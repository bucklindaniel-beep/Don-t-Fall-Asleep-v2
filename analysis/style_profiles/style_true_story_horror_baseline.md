## Purpose
This file defines the baseline style for true-story horror narration.

Use this file to:
- create believable first-person accounts
- increase immersion through realism
- structure anecdotal storytelling
- maintain credibility throughout escalation

---

## Core Style Summary
- first-person narration
- grounded, believable tone
- simple and direct language
- minimal exaggeration
- gradual escalation from normal to abnormal

---

## Narrative Voice

### Perspective
- first person (“I”)
- occasionally second-hand (“my friend told me”)

### Delivery
- conversational but controlled
- observational rather than dramatic
- reflective in hindsight

---

## Tone Characteristics

### Primary Tone
- neutral
- grounded
- matter-of-fact

### Secondary Tone
- uneasy
- tense
- reflective

---

## Sentence Structure

### General Pattern
- short to medium sentences
- natural conversational flow
- minimal complexity

### During Escalation
- shorter sentences
- quicker pacing
- more direct phrasing

---

## Vocabulary Guidelines

### Preferred
- everyday language
- specific but simple details
- concrete descriptions

### Avoid
- poetic language
- exaggerated descriptors
- abstract phrasing
- overly cinematic wording

---

## Realism Behavior

### Key Principle
Everything should feel like it could actually happen.

### Methods
- include small, believable details
- maintain logical cause-and-effect
- avoid unrealistic reactions

---

## Event Framing

### Opening Style
- casual setup
- normal situation

Example:
“I was staying at my cousin’s house for the weekend.”

---

### Escalation Style
- gradual increase in tension
- events build logically
- character initially dismisses anomalies

---

### Ending Style
- often unresolved or partially explained
- may include reflection

Example:
“I still don’t know what it was.”

---

## Emotional Handling

### Approach
- emotion is implied through behavior

### Show Through
- hesitation
- confusion
- reaction to events

### Avoid
- explicit emotional labeling
- dramatic statements

---

## Description Style

### Effective Approach
- focus on relevant details
- describe what changes
- keep descriptions concise

### Avoid
- long descriptive passages
- unnecessary detail

---

## Suspense Handling

- rely on realism
- introduce small irregularities
- delay confirmation of threat
- escalate gradually

---

## Character Behavior

### Key Traits
- rational thinking
- attempts to explain events
- delayed acceptance of danger

---

## Integration With Techniques

This style strongly supports:
- realism grounding
- delayed reveal
- repetition pressure
- false safety

---

## Example — Baseline
I got home around midnight and everything seemed normal.

I locked the door and went upstairs.

A few minutes later, I heard something move in the hallway.

---

## Example — Escalation
At first, I thought it was just the house settling.

But then it happened again.

This time, it sounded closer.

---

## Failure Modes

### 1. Over-dramatization
Breaks realism.

### 2. Unrealistic behavior
Reduces credibility.

### 3. Over-description
Slows pacing.

### 4. Immediate escalation
Removes buildup.

---

## Reusable Writing Rules
- keep narration grounded
- use simple, believable language
- escalate gradually
- show reactions, don’t explain emotions
- maintain logical progression

---

## Compression Summary
True story horror = first-person narration + realism + gradual escalation.

---

## Notes
This style is essential for high-retention YouTube horror content.

It works best when combined with:
- restrained tone
- realistic pacing
- subtle escalation
