## Purpose
This file defines the baseline style for horror narration.

Use this file to:
- standardize tone across scripts
- ensure clarity and consistency
- provide a neutral foundation before applying stylistic variations

---

## Core Style Summary
- clear and direct narration
- grounded tone
- controlled pacing
- minimal exaggeration
- focus on events rather than commentary

---

## Tone Characteristics

### Primary Tone
- serious
- calm
- controlled

### Secondary Tone (as needed)
- tense
- uneasy
- urgent (during escalation)

---

## Narrative Voice

- observational rather than expressive
- avoids unnecessary commentary
- describes what happens, not what it means
- maintains credibility and realism

---

## Sentence Structure

### General Pattern
- short to medium-length sentences
- clear and direct phrasing
- minimal complexity

### During Escalation
- shorter sentences
- increased fragmentation
- faster rhythm

### During Calm Moments
- slightly longer sentences
- smoother flow

---

## Vocabulary Guidelines

### Preferred
- simple, concrete words
- everyday language
- precise descriptions

### Avoid
- overly complex vocabulary
- abstract language
- poetic or decorative phrasing
- exaggerated descriptors

---

## Pacing Behavior

### Baseline
- steady, controlled pacing

### Escalation
- increase speed gradually
- reduce time between events

### Climax
- fast, sharp pacing

### Aftermath
- slow down again

---

## Emotional Expression

### Approach
- emotion is implied, not stated directly

### Show Through
- actions
- reactions
- pacing
- environment

### Avoid
- explicit emotional labeling
- dramatic declarations

---

## Description Style

### Effective Approach
- describe only what matters
- focus on changes
- prioritize clarity

### Avoid
- over-description
- unnecessary detail
- long descriptive passages

---

## Realism Guidelines

- maintain plausible behavior
- keep reactions grounded
- avoid unrealistic decisions
- support believability at all times

---

## Suspense Handling

- rely on implication
- delay explanation
- allow uncertainty to exist
- build tension gradually

---

## Common Structural Behavior

1. grounded opening
2. subtle anomaly
3. gradual escalation
4. increasing tension
5. controlled climax
6. minimal or partial resolution

---

## Integration With Techniques

This baseline supports:
- delayed reveal
- repetition pressure
- false safety
- environmental escalation
- realism grounding

---

## Adaptation Layer

This style is intended to be modified by:

### Channel Profiles
- Mr. Nightmare → more minimal
- Chilling Scares → more cinematic
- CreepsMcPasta → more descriptive

### Genre Adjustments
- analog horror → more fragmented and detached
- psychological horror → more internal focus

---

## Example — Baseline
I got home later than usual that night.

Everything seemed normal at first.

Then I heard something move upstairs.

---

## Example — Escalation
The sound came again.

This time, it was closer.

I knew it wasn’t coming from outside anymore.

---

## Failure Modes

### 1. Overwriting
Too much detail reduces pacing.

### 2. Over-dramatization
Breaks realism and immersion.

### 3. Flat delivery
No variation in pacing or structure.

### 4. Over-explanation
Removes tension.

---

## Reusable Writing Rules
- keep sentences clear and direct
- show emotion through action
- escalate pacing gradually
- avoid unnecessary description
- maintain realism

---

## Compression Summary
Horror narration baseline = clear, grounded, controlled storytelling with gradual escalation.

---

## Notes
This file serves as the default narration style.

All other styles should layer on top of this foundation.
