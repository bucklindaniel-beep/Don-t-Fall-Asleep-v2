## Core Concept
An entity that imitates familiar voices in a way that is initially believable, but becomes increasingly unsettling through subtle inaccuracies in timing, tone, and context.

---

## Behavior Class
- mimic

---

## Interaction Mode
- reactive
- adaptive (improves imitation over time)
- aggressive (late-stage, through proximity and intrusion)

---

## Presence Channel
- auditory
- spatial (secondary through perceived location)

---

## Perceptual Reliability
- deceptive (intentionally imitates and misleads)
- ambiguous (source of sound is unclear)

---

## Escalation Driver
- repetition
- awareness
- proximity (late-stage)

---

## Boundary Relationship
- external → threshold → internal (progresses inward over time)

---

## Flavor
- unknown
- psychological
- (optional: human / demonic depending on tone)

---

## Perceived Nature
- recognized first as a familiar voice  
- accepted before being questioned  
- understood as wrong through repetition  

The entity feels:
> believable at first, then unsettling as inconsistencies emerge

---

## Behavior Patterns

### Presence
- manifests through speech, whispers, or short phrases  
- appears when the protagonist is alone or expects someone nearby  

### Movement
- implied through shifting sound location  
- may seem to move closer between interactions  

### Interaction
- calls the protagonist by name  
- repeats known phrases or tones  
- responds incorrectly or with slight delay  
- imitates emotion without fully understanding context  

### Visibility
- typically not seen during early and mid stages  
- if revealed, visibility is incomplete or delayed  

---

## Implied Rules

- it learns from what it hears  
- it is more convincing in short phrases  
- it becomes more accurate with repetition  
- it uses familiarity to reduce suspicion  

---

## Pattern Hooks

- hearing a familiar voice in the wrong place  
- being called from an empty room  
- repeated phrases with slightly incorrect tone  
- responses that come too late or too quickly  
- voices coming from behind doors, walls, or darkness  

---

## Escalation Profile

### Early Stage
- brief voice occurrences  
- assumed to be real  

### Mid Stage
- repeated interactions  
- inconsistencies in tone or timing  
- voice appears in impossible locations  

### Late Stage
- direct attempts at communication  
- emotional mimicry becomes less accurate  
- proximity increases  

### Climax Behavior
- voice appears within immediate space  
- overlaps or interrupts real speech  
- may respond from inside a previously safe area  

---

## Psychological Effect

### Primary Fear Type
- paranoia  

### Cognitive Effect
- inability to trust familiar voices  
- confusion about presence and identity  
- breakdown of auditory reliability  

---

## Environmental Impact

- sound behaves unnaturally (wrong direction, wrong distance)  
- silence becomes heavy before or after speech  
- voices carry through barriers they shouldn’t  
- familiar environments become threatening due to “response”  

---

## Best Use Cases

- houses, apartments, hallways  
- scenes involving separation or distance  
- early-to-mid escalation  
- setups for intrusion  

---

## Avoid

- perfect imitation too early  
- long, clear conversations  
- immediate visual confirmation  
- random speech without pattern  

---

## Example Usage (Minimal)

I heard my mom call my name from downstairs.

That didn’t make sense.

She wasn’t home.

A few seconds later, I heard it again—

this time right outside my door.
