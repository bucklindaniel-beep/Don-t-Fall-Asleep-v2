## Core Concept
A space that repeats or redirects itself, preventing escape while subtly evolving, creating the realization that movement no longer guarantees progress.

---

## Behavior Patterns

### Presence
- manifests as a normal, grounded environment  
- repetition becomes noticeable through navigation  

### Movement
- forward movement loops back to previous locations  
- paths fail to lead where expected  
- turns and directions lose consistency  

### Interaction
- environment introduces subtle changes between iterations  
- may react to attempts to escape (blocking, redirecting)  
- may alter based on awareness  

### Visibility
- fully visible and realistic  
- distortion is recognized through inconsistency, not appearance  

---

## Implied Rules

- movement does not guarantee progress  
- loops are not identical—they evolve  
- exits are unreliable or misleading  
- awareness does not break the loop  

---

## Pattern Hooks

- returning to the same location unintentionally  
- noticing small differences between passes  
- counting steps or turns that don’t match outcomes  
- doors leading back to where they started  
- objects shifting slightly between iterations  

---

## Escalation Profile

### Early Stage
- subtle repetition  
- uncertainty about whether it’s coincidence  

### Mid Stage
- confirmed looping  
- environmental differences become noticeable  
- attempts to escape begin  

### Late Stage
- loop becomes aggressive or intentional  
- exits become misleading or disappear  
- environment feels responsive  

### Climax Behavior
- loop becomes unavoidable  
- space actively prevents escape  
- protagonist is fully trapped within system  

---

## Psychological Effect

### Primary Fear Type

### Cognitive Effect

## Environmental Impact

- repeated architecture  
- shifting object placement  
- subtle changes in lighting or layout  
- increasing distortion across iterations  

---

## Best Use Cases

- hallways, staircases, corridors  
- buildings with predictable layouts  
- scenes involving navigation or escape  
- mid-to-late escalation  

---

## Avoid

- identical loops with no variation  
- immediate obvious repetition  
- lack of escalation between iterations  
- resolving the loop too easily
