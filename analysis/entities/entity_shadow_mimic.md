## Core Concept
An entity that imitates a familiar human presence, but in a visually incomplete and unsettlingly incorrect way, improving over time.

---

## Behavior Class
- mimic
- observer (secondary)

---

## Interaction Mode
- passive (early)
- reactive (mid)
- adaptive (late)

---

## Presence Channel
- visual
- spatial

---

## Perceptual Reliability
- deceptive (imitates familiarity incorrectly)
- ambiguous (form is incomplete or obscured)

---

## Escalation Driver
- repetition
- awareness
- proximity (late-stage)

---

## Boundary Relationship
- threshold → internal (escalates inward over time)

---

## Flavor
- unknown
- psychological
- (optional: demonic / human depending on tone)

---

## Perceived Nature
- humanoid silhouette or shadow-like form  
- appears where a known person should be  
- recognized first as familiar, then questioned  

The entity feels:
> almost correct, but unsettlingly wrong in proportion, timing, or presence

---

## Behavior Patterns

### Presence
- appears in familiar locations (hallways, rooms, doorways)  
- often replaces or imitates expected individuals  

### Movement
- minimal or delayed  
- unnatural stillness  
- movement may lag behind expectation  

### Interaction
- responds incorrectly or too late  
- mimics behavior without emotional accuracy  
- may repeat known actions or patterns  

### Visibility
- seen in low light or shadow  
- lacks full detail (face, features, edges unclear)  
- clarity does not fully resolve even when observed  

---

## Implied Rules

- it imitates without full understanding  
- it becomes more accurate with repetition  
- it gets closer over time  
- it relies on familiarity to reduce suspicion  

---

## Pattern Hooks

- someone standing where a familiar person should be  
- delayed or incorrect responses  
- unnatural stillness  
- repeated sightings with improved accuracy  
- something that looks right until observed closely  

---

## Escalation Profile

### Early Stage
- subtle visual inconsistency  
- presence feels slightly off  

### Mid Stage
- repeated encounters  
- behavior becomes more convincing  
- delays and errors become noticeable  

### Late Stage
- replaces expected presence more convincingly  
- identity confusion begins  
- proximity increases  

### Climax Behavior
- direct proximity  
- interaction reveals failure of imitation  
- recognition that it is not what it appears to be  

---

## Psychological Effect

### Primary Fear Type
- paranoia  

### Cognitive Effect
- inability to trust familiar people  
- uncertainty about identity and perception  
- discomfort with “almost correct” behavior  

---

## Environmental Impact

- shadows appear heavier or more defined  
- lighting obscures detail intentionally  
- silence around the entity  
- familiar spaces feel subtly altered  

---

## Best Use Cases

- homes, apartments, familiar environments  
- stories involving known individuals  
- mid-stage escalation into identity horror  
- transitions from observer → intrusion  

---

## Avoid

- perfect imitation too early  
- full visual clarity  
- immediate confrontation  
- inconsistent mimic behavior  

---

I saw my brother standing in the hallway.

but something about the way he was standing felt wrong.

When I asked him a question, he answered.

Just a few seconds too late.
