## Core Concept
An entity that follows the protagonist without ever being directly seen, revealing its presence through consistent, proximity-based cues that indicate it is getting closer.

---

## Behavior Class
- follower

---

## Interaction Mode
- reactive
- adaptive (adjusts to movement and behavior over time)

---

- auditory
- spatial
- environmental (secondary)

---

## Perceptual Reliability
- ambiguous (never fully confirmed visually)
- stable (consistent tracking behavior)

---

## Escalation Driver
- proximity
- movement
- awareness (intensifies once recognized)

---

## Boundary Relationship
- external → threshold → internal (progresses inward over time)

- unknown

## Perceived Nature
- never directly visible  
- experienced through movement relative to the protagonist  
- understood as something always just out of sight  

The entity feels:
> constantly present, always behind or near, but impossible to confirm

---

## Behavior Patterns

### Presence
- begins at a distance, implied through subtle cues  
- remains consistently “nearby” once established  

### Movement
- tracks the protagonist’s movement  
- stops when the protagonist stops  
- resumes when movement continues  
- distance closes gradually over time  

### Interaction
- does not initiate direct communication early  
- interacts indirectly through sound or environmental cues  
- becomes more active as proximity increases  

### Visibility
- never clearly seen  
- may be suggested through shadows, reflections, or blocked sightlines  
- disappears when directly checked  

---

## Implied Rules

- it follows but does not rush  
- it is always closer than perceived  
- it cannot be directly observed  
- it adapts to the protagonist’s movement patterns  

---

## Pattern Hooks

- footsteps behind with no visible source  
- something stopping when the protagonist turns  
- movement resuming when the protagonist moves again  
- increasing sense of presence just out of sight  
- repeated confirmation that distance is shrinking  

---

## Escalation Profile

### Early Stage
- subtle auditory cues  
- uncertain presence  

### Mid Stage
- consistent following pattern  
- distance appears to shrink  
- behavior becomes recognizable  

### Late Stage
- presence becomes immediate  
- interaction with environment increases  
- movement feels synchronized with the protagonist  

### Climax Behavior
- entity occupies immediate proximity  
- escape becomes impossible  
- confrontation occurs through inevitability rather than visibility  

---

## Psychological Effect

### Primary Fear Type
- anxiety  

### Cognitive Effect
- constant tension during movement  
- inability to feel alone or safe  
- fear of what is just behind or out of sight  

---

## Environmental Impact

- footsteps or movement sounds without source  
- subtle disturbances behind the protagonist  
- silence when movement stops  
- spatial awareness becomes unreliable  

---

## Best Use Cases

- hallways, streets, forests, long paths  
- scenes involving movement or travel  
- early-to-mid escalation leading into intrusion  
- situations where the protagonist is alone  

---

## Avoid

- revealing the entity visually  
- inconsistent tracking behavior  
- random, unpatterned movement  
- immediate close proximity without buildup  

---

## Example Usage (Minimal)

I heard footsteps behind me.

I stopped.

They stopped too.

I kept walking.

A few seconds later—

they started again.
