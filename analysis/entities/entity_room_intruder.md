## Core Concept
An entity that appears within a space assumed to be secure, removing all remaining boundaries between the protagonist and the threat.

---

## Behavior Class
- intrusive

---

## Interaction Mode
- reactive
- aggressive (late-stage escalation)

## Presence Channel
- visual
- spatial
- environmental (secondary)

## Perceptual Reliability
- ambiguous (unclear form or positioning)
- stable (consistent presence once recognized)

- proximity
- awareness

## Boundary Relationship
- internal

---

## Flavor
- unknown
- psychological
- (optional: human / demonic depending on tone)

---

## Perceived Nature
- partially visible or obscured  
- defined more by presence than form  
- recognized through realization rather than arrival  

The entity is experienced as:
> something that is already inside, without explanation

---

## Behavior Patterns

### Presence
- appears within enclosed or “safe” spaces  
- often positioned in corners, behind objects, or just outside direct view  

### Movement
- rarely seen entering or moving  
- repositions when not directly observed  
- may appear closer without visible transition  

### Interaction
- interacts with nearby environment (subtle movement, sound, spatial pressure)  
- may respond to awareness by increasing proximity  
- does not require entry or permission  

### Visibility
- partially visible, often obscured by darkness or angles  
- difficult to fully resolve visually  
- clarity decreases as proximity increases  

---

## Implied Rules

- it does not need to enter—it is already present  
- barriers (doors, locks, walls) do not prevent it  
- awareness of it increases its perceived proximity  
- it occupies space the protagonist cannot fully control  

---

## Pattern Hooks

- something in the room that wasn’t there before  
- realizing a door was never opened  
- a shape in a corner that doesn’t belong  
- something positioned behind or beside the protagonist  
- something present in a place that was just checked  

---

## Escalation Profile

### Early Stage
- subtle inconsistencies in the room  
- discomfort without confirmation  

### Mid Stage
- discovery of presence  
- repeated confirmation that something is inside  
- attempts to rationalize or ignore  

### Late Stage
- proximity becomes immediate  
- entity occupies shared space  
- movement or repositioning becomes implied  

### Climax Behavior
- unavoidable proximity  
- direct or implied interaction  
- complete collapse of safety  

---

## Psychological Effect

### Primary Fear Type
- panic  

### Cognitive Effect
- total loss of perceived safety  
- inability to trust enclosed spaces  
- fear of immediate proximity and unseen movement  

---

## Environmental Impact

- silence becomes oppressive  
- small sounds feel amplified  
- space feels tighter or more confined  
- familiar layouts feel unsafe or altered  

---

## Best Use Cases

- bedrooms, apartments, small enclosed spaces  
- late-stage escalation and climax  
- scenes following buildup from unseen or observer entities  
- moments where safety is expected  

---

## Avoid

- introducing without prior buildup  
- clearly revealing full form too early  
- inconsistent positioning or logic  
- overuse without escalation context  

---

## Example Usage (Minimal)

I checked the room before I went to bed.

The door was locked.

Everything was exactly where it should be.

I lay there for a while—

and then I realized something was in the corner.
