## Purpose
This file defines realism grounding as a reusable horror-writing technique.

Use this file to:
- make stories feel believable
- increase audience immersion
- support subtle and psychological horror
- strengthen impact of escalation and reveal

---

## Core Definition
Realism grounding is a technique where ordinary details, plausible behavior, and familiar settings are used to anchor the story in reality, making unusual or threatening events feel more credible.

---

## Why This Technique Works
Fear is stronger when the audience believes the story could happen.

Realism:
- reduces skepticism
- increases immersion
- makes anomalies feel more threatening
- allows subtle escalation to remain effective

---

## Core Effect on the Audience
- increases believability
- creates relatability
- lowers resistance to suspension of disbelief
- makes small irregularities feel significant
- amplifies tension when normal reality is disrupted

---

## Structural Logic

1. establish normal, relatable context
2. reinforce realism with small details
3. introduce anomaly within that realism
4. maintain grounded reactions
5. escalate without breaking plausibility

---

## Canonical Pattern

### Step 1 — Familiar Context
Use recognizable settings and situations:
- home
- workplace
- street
- car

### Step 2 — Mundane Detail
Add small, specific details:
- time of day
- routine behavior
- ordinary objects

### Step 3 — Natural Behavior
Characters act in believable ways:
- hesitation
- dismissal of threat
- rational explanations

### Step 4 — Subtle Disruption
Introduce something slightly wrong.

### Step 5 — Maintain Plausibility
Even as tension increases, reactions remain grounded.

---

## Common Variants

### 1. Routine Anchoring
Establish daily habits or normal routines.

**Function:** Builds a stable baseline.

---

### 2. Specific Detail Anchoring
Use precise but ordinary details.

**Function:** Increases realism without over-description.

---

### 3. Rational Explanation Phase
Characters attempt to explain events logically.

**Function:** Delays acceptance of danger.

---

### 4. Gradual Breakdown
Reality shifts slowly instead of abruptly.

**Function:** Maintains believability during escalation.

---

### 5. Understatement
Serious events are described calmly.

**Function:** enhances realism and restraint.

---

## Best Use Cases

Use realism grounding when you want to:
- create “true story” style horror
- support slow-burn tension
- enhance psychological horror
- make subtle anomalies effective
- increase immersion in narration

---

## When To Avoid It

Avoid realism grounding when:
- the story is intentionally surreal or abstract
- heavy stylization is required
- pacing demands immediate intensity
- the tone is exaggerated or theatrical

## Pacing Guidance

### To strengthen realism
- include small, specific details
- avoid rushing events
- allow characters to process events naturally

### To maintain immersion
- keep descriptions grounded
- avoid exaggeration
- focus on observation over interpretation

---

## Language Guidance

### Effective language traits
- simple, clear wording
- concrete details
- observational tone

### Less effective language traits
- dramatic exaggeration
- overly descriptive or poetic language
- abstract phrasing

---

## Example Application — Minimal
It was around 11:30 when I got home from work.

I locked the door behind me and dropped my keys on the table.

A few minutes later, I heard something move upstairs.

---

## Example Application — Escalated
I had just gotten back from work, later than usual.

I remember checking the time because I had an early morning the next day.

Everything was normal—until I heard something shift upstairs.

At first, I assumed it was just the house settling.

But it happened again.

---

## Failure Modes

### 1. Over-detailing
Too many details slow pacing.

### 2. Breaking plausibility
Characters react unrealistically.

### 3. Sudden tonal shift
Story becomes exaggerated too quickly.

### 4. Ignoring logic
Events contradict established reality without explanation.

---

## Reusable Writing Rules
- start with something familiar
- use small, believable details
- keep reactions realistic
- delay extreme events
- let the abnormal emerge gradually

---

## Compression Summary
Realism grounding = believable context + natural behavior + gradual disruption.
