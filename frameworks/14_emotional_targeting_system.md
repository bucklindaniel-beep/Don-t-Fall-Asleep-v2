# Emotional Targeting System

## Metadata
- Type: Framework
- Domain: Viewer Emotion / Scene-Shot-Edit Alignment
- Status: active
- Priority: medium

---

## Purpose
Keep emotional progression intentional across scenes, shots, prompts, editing, and narration without over-explaining viewer response.

---

## Rules
- Use concise emotional targets only when they improve production clarity.
- Prefer implied emotional intent over direct explanation.
- Avoid repeating “the viewer feels/understands” phrasing.
- Track emotional movement, not constant intensity.
- Let silence, framing, pacing, and contrast carry emotion when possible.

---

## Emotional Progression
Common horror progression:
ordinary → mild wrongness → doubt → pressure → contradiction → dread → realization → unsettled residue

Vary this progression by story type.

---

## Application
- Scene: what should linger?
- Shot: what tension does the image carry?
- Prompt: one concise emotional target only when helpful
- Edit: where does emotion rise, flatten, break, or withhold release?
- Review: detect safe zones, unsupported spikes, or emotional monotony
