## Core Principle

Retention is not just pacing.

It is the controlled release of information that keeps the viewer:

- curious
- uncertain
- engaged
- anticipating what comes next

---

## Retention Model

Retention is driven by:

1. Curiosity Gap  
2. Pattern Reinforcement  
3. Escalation Timing  
4. Expectation Control  
5. Information Withholding  

---

## Mechanic 1 — Curiosity Gap

Introduce incomplete information.

The viewer should:
- understand something is happening
- NOT fully understand what or why

### Rules

- never explain immediately  
- delay clarity  
- answer questions slowly  

---

## Mechanic 2 — Pattern Reinforcement

Establish a pattern.

Then:
- repeat it
- slightly change it

Each repetition should:
- confirm the pattern
- increase discomfort

---

## Mechanic 3 — Escalation Timing

Tension must increase in waves.

Each wave:
- introduces new pressure
- builds on previous events

Do NOT:
- keep tension flat  
- escalate randomly  

---

## Mechanic 4 — Expectation Control

Set a mental expectation.

Then:
- delay it
- distort it
- subtly break it

Avoid:
- obvious jump scares  
- predictable outcomes  

---

## Mechanic 5 — Information Withholding

Do not fully reveal:

- the entity  
- the rules  
- the outcome  

The viewer should:
- infer  
- question  
- stay engaged  

---

## Layer Integration

---

### Story Level

- delay full understanding of the situation  
- introduce new unknowns before resolving old ones  

---

### Scene Level

Each scene must:
- introduce OR reinforce OR escalate  

Never:
- resolve everything in one scene  

---

### Shot Level

- reveal information gradually  
- avoid full visual clarity too early  

---

### Audio Level

- use silence intentionally  
- avoid constant intensity  
- allow anticipation to build  

---

## Retention Flow Model

curiosity → recognition → discomfort → escalation → anticipation → partial reveal

Then repeat.

---

## Interaction With Entity System

Retention must be driven through:

- entity behavior  
- entity escalation  
- entity perception  

NOT:
- external plot devices  
- forced twists  

---

## Failure Conditions

The system fails if:

- too much is explained too early  
- patterns do not evolve  
- tension does not increase  
- outcomes are predictable  
- viewer can fully understand the threat too soon  

---

## Compression Summary

Control information → reinforce patterns → escalate tension → delay answers → maintain engagement

---

## Organic Horror Retention Rule

Do not apply generic clickbait retention tactics. Horror retention should come from organic curiosity, delayed understanding, pattern disturbance, emotional tension, and payoff/reframe mechanics.

Use `/frameworks/10_organic_hook_system.md` and `/frameworks/11_organic_story_beat_system.md` when selecting retention mechanics for story generation.
