## Purpose
This framework defines how physical threat escalates in horror.

Use this file to:
- structure movement and proximity of threats
- control pacing from tension to action
- determine when contact occurs
- create impactful confrontations

---

## Core Principle
Physical escalation should be delayed and controlled.

The threat should:
- begin distant or indirect
- move closer over time
- increase in intensity
- eventually interact with the character

---

## Physical Layers

1. Presence (awareness of something)
2. Distance (how far away it is)
3. Movement (how it approaches)
4. Proximity (how close it gets)
5. Contact (interaction)
6. Constraint (restriction of movement)

---

## Escalation Stages

### Stage 1 — No Physical Threat
- no movement
- no presence
- safe environment

**Function:** Establish baseline

---

### Stage 2 — Implied Presence
- sounds or signals suggest something nearby
- no visible movement

**Function:** Introduce tension

---

### Stage 3 — Distant Movement
- movement occurs far away
- indirect visibility

**Function:** Build awareness

---

### Stage 4 — Approaching Movement
- threat moves closer
- more frequent or noticeable

**Function:** Increase pressure

---

### Stage 5 — Close Proximity
- threat is nearby
- within immediate space

**Function:** Heighten danger

---

### Stage 6 — Near Contact
- almost touching
- direct interaction imminent

**Function:** Peak tension before action

---

### Stage 7 — Contact
- physical interaction occurs

Examples:
- touch
- grab
- impact

**Function:** Deliver climax

---

### Stage 8 — Constraint or Aftermath
- character is:
  - trapped
  - injured
  - escaping

**Function:** Resolve or extend threat

## Pacing Guidance

### Early
- no movement
- implied presence

### Mid
- distant movement
- increasing proximity

### Late
- rapid escalation
- contact

---

## Language Guidance

### Early Stages
- indirect descriptions
- focus on sound or implication

### Mid Stages
- clearer movement
- increased detail

### Late Stages
- short, direct sentences
- action-focused

---

## Example Application — Minimal
He heard something move in the hallway.

A few seconds later, it was outside his door.

Then the handle started to turn.

---

## Example Application — Escalated
At first, it was just a sound.

Then something moved at the end of the hallway.

Each time he looked, it was closer.

By the time he realized it was right behind him, it was too late.

---

## Failure Modes

### 1. Early contact
Removes buildup.

### 2. No progression
Threat stays distant.

### 3. Overuse of action
Reduces tension.

### 4. Unrealistic movement
Breaks immersion.

---

## Reusable Rules
- delay physical contact  
- move threat closer gradually  
- vary movement patterns  
- increase proximity over time  
- use contact sparingly for impact  

---

## Compression Summary
Physical escalation = presence → movement → proximity → contact.

---

## Notes
This framework is critical for transitioning from tension to action.

It should be used sparingly and deliberately to preserve impact.
