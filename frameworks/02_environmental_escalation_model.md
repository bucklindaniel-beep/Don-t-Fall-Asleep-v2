## Purpose
This framework defines how to escalate tension through environmental changes.

Use this file to:
- structure atmosphere progression
- create immersive scenes
- maintain tension between events
- support psychological and emotional escalation

---

## Core Principle
The environment should evolve over time.

It should:
- begin stable and familiar
- introduce subtle irregularities
- gradually destabilize
- become threatening or hostile

---

## Environmental Escalation Layers

1. Sound
2. Lighting
3. Space / Layout
4. Objects
5. Movement
6. Atmosphere (overall feeling)

---

## Escalation Stages

### Stage 1 — Stable Environment
- normal lighting
- consistent sound
- familiar layout

**Function:** Establish baseline

---

### Stage 2 — Subtle Irregularity
- slight silence or noise shift
- minor lighting change
- small object displacement

**Function:** Create unease

---

### Stage 3 — Reinforced Irregularity
- repeated anomalies
- noticeable inconsistencies

**Function:** Build awareness

---

### Stage 4 — Escalation
- stronger environmental changes
- movement or sound becomes more apparent

**Function:** Increase tension

---

### Stage 5 — Distortion
- space feels wrong
- layout shifts
- environment behaves unnaturally

**Function:** Break normal expectations

---

### Stage 6 — Hostility
- environment actively contributes to danger
- blocks escape or reacts to character

**Function:** Support active threat

---

### Layered Change

### Sudden Shift

### Looping Environment

## Pacing Guidelines

### Early
- minimal changes
- subtle cues

### Mid
- increasing frequency
- more noticeable changes

### Late
- strong distortion
- hostile behavior

---

## Scene Application

1. establish environment  
2. introduce subtle change  
3. reinforce irregularity  
4. escalate environment  
5. distort setting  
6. create hostile conditions  

---

## Failure Modes

### 1. No baseline
Changes are not noticeable.

### 2. No progression
Environment stays static.

### 3. Over-intensity early
Breaks immersion.

### 4. Over-description
Slows pacing.

---

## Reusable Rules
- always establish normal first  
- escalate gradually  
- vary environmental elements  
- tie environment to tension  
- use environment to support narrative  

---

## Compression Summary
Environmental escalation = stable setting → subtle change → distortion → hostility.

---

## Notes
This framework is essential for immersive horror.

It allows tension to exist continuously without relying on direct action.
