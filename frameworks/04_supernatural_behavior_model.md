## Purpose
This framework defines how supernatural threats behave within a story.

Use this file to:
- create consistent threat logic
- design rule-based horror systems
- build tension through pattern recognition
- control how and when threats escalate

---

## Core Principle
Supernatural threats should follow patterns or rules.

Even if the rules are not explained:
- they must exist
- they must be consistent (until broken)

This allows the audience to:
- recognize patterns
- anticipate danger
- feel tension when rules are violated

---

## Behavior Layers

1. Presence (when it exists)
2. Detection (how it is noticed)
3. Movement (how it travels)
4. Interaction (how it affects environment or character)
5. Limitation (what it cannot do)
6. Trigger (what activates it)

---

## Behavior Stages

### Stage 1 — Unknown Presence
- threat is not confirmed
- anomalies suggest something exists

**Function:** Introduce mystery

---

### Stage 2 — Pattern Emergence
- repeated behaviors or signals
- audience begins to recognize structure

**Function:** Build understanding

---

### Stage 3 — Rule Recognition
- implicit rules become noticeable

Examples:
- only appears at night
- only moves when unseen

**Function:** Create anticipation

---

### Stage 4 — Rule Reinforcement
- behavior repeats consistently

**Function:** Strengthen pattern

---

### Stage 5 — Rule Tension
- character tests or approaches limits of rules

**Function:** Increase risk

---

### Stage 6 — Rule Violation
- rules change, break, or expand

**Function:** Create shock and escalation

---

### Stage 7 — Direct Threat
- entity interacts directly with character

**Function:** Deliver confrontation

---

## Common Rule Types

### Time-Based Rules
- specific hours
- cycles or intervals

---

### Visibility Rules
- only visible under certain conditions
- disappears when observed

---

### Spatial Rules
- limited to certain areas
- moves through specific paths

---

### Behavioral Rules
- reacts to sound, movement, or attention

---

### Interaction Rules
- requires permission, proximity, or trigger

---

## Pattern-Based Behavior

Supernatural threats often:
- repeat actions
- follow sequences
- create predictable patterns

These patterns:
- build tension
- encourage anticipation
- allow for escalation

---

## Rule Violation

### Purpose
Breaking established rules creates maximum impact.

### Methods
- expand abilities
- remove limitations
- change patterns
- invert behavior

---

## Interaction With Other Systems

### Emotional Escalation
Rules create:
- anticipation
- dread

Violation creates:
- panic
- terror

---

### Psychological Escalation
Rules influence:
- understanding
- paranoia
- expectation

---

### Environmental Escalation
Presence affects:
- lighting
- sound
- space

---

### Physical Escalation
Rules determine:
- when contact occurs
- how threat manifests

---

## Pacing Guidance

### Early
- subtle signals
- unclear patterns

### Mid
- consistent behavior
- recognizable rules

### Late
- rule tension and violation
- escalation to direct threat

---

## Language Guidance

### Effective Traits
- observational
- pattern-focused
- restrained explanation

### Avoid
- explicit rule explanation
- over-description
- full clarity

---

## Example Application — Minimal
It only appeared when the lights were off.

Every time I turned them back on, it was gone.

---

## Example Application — Escalated
At first, it only moved when I wasn’t looking.

Then it started moving even when I was.

That’s when I realized the rules had changed.

---

## Failure Modes

### 1. No consistency
Threat feels random.

### 2. Over-explanation
Removes mystery.

### 3. Weak rules
No tension or anticipation.

### 4. No escalation
Behavior does not evolve.

---

## Reusable Rules
- establish patterns early  
- reinforce behavior consistently  
- allow the audience to infer rules  
- build tension through expectation  
- break rules at the right moment  

---

## Compression Summary
Supernatural behavior = pattern → rule → reinforcement → violation → threat.
