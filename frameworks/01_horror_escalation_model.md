## Purpose
This framework defines the **authoritative escalation structure** for horror storytelling.

All scenes, patterns, and pacing systems MUST align with this model.

---

## Core Principle
Tension MUST increase in a controlled, sequential manner.

Escalation MUST:

- begin with ambiguity  
- transition into pattern  
- increase constraint and proximity  
- culminate in direct threat  
- resolve without full explanation  

---

## Core Escalation Stages (Authoritative)

All stories MUST follow:

1. Setup  
2. Disruption  
3. Reinforcement  
4. Escalation  
5. Climax  
6. Resolution  

---

## Stage Definitions

### 1. Setup
- environment is stable  
- character is in a normal state  
- no anomaly present  

**Function:** establish baseline  

---

### 2. Disruption
- first anomaly appears  
- ambiguity remains  
- no confirmation of threat  

**Function:** introduce unease  

---

### 3. Reinforcement
- anomaly repeats or intensifies  
- pattern begins forming  
- doubt decreases  

**Function:** build recognition  

---

### 4. Escalation
- threat becomes structurally clear  
- proximity, frequency, or control increases  
- options begin to narrow  

**Function:** increase pressure  

---

### 5. Climax
- direct threat or confrontation occurs  
- immediate danger is present  
- decisive action is required  

**Function:** deliver peak tension  

---

### 6. Resolution
- escape, interruption, or withdrawal  
- threat may remain unexplained  
- tension is reduced but not fully removed  

**Function:** conclude or extend tension  

---

## Escalation Layers

Escalation may occur across:

- emotional  
- psychological  
- environmental  
- supernatural  
- physical  

Multiple layers SHOULD be used, but stage order MUST remain consistent.

---

## Escalation Integrity Rules

- stages MUST progress forward  
- stages MUST NOT be skipped  
- reinforcement MUST occur before escalation  
- escalation MUST occur before climax  
- climax MUST occur before resolution  

A sequence FAILS if:

- escalation jumps stages  
- threat appears without buildup  
- climax occurs without reinforcement  
- resolution occurs before climax  

---

## Escalation Control Levers

### Increase Tension
- reduce available information  
- increase repetition  
- decrease distance  
- increase unpredictability  
- remove safety  

---

### Decrease Tension
- restore normal conditions  
- introduce partial explanation  
- increase distance from threat  
- slow pacing  

---

---

## Failure Modes

### 1. No escalation
Story remains static  

### 2. Over-escalation
Climax occurs too early  

### 3. Stage skipping
Progression feels unnatural  

### 4. Weak climax
Build-up does not match payoff  

---

## Reusable Rules
- escalate gradually  
- reinforce before revealing  
- maintain ambiguity early  
- increase constraint over time  
- avoid full explanation  

---

## Compression Summary
Escalation = ambiguity → pattern → pressure → threat → confrontation → exit
